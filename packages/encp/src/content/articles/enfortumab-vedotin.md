## Chemical Identity

| Property          | Value                                    |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Molecular Weight  | ~152 kDa (ADC)                           |
| Peptide Payload   | Monomethyl auristatin E (MMAE)           |
| Linker            | Valine-citrulline (protease-cleavable)   |
| DAR               | 3.8 (drug-to-antibody ratio)             |
| Target            | Nectin-4                                 |

## Structure

Enfortumab vedotin consists of a fully human anti-Nectin-4 IgG1 monoclonal antibody conjugated to the microtubule inhibitor MMAE via a protease-cleavable valine-citrulline dipeptide linker and a maleimidocaproyl spacer. After internalization, lysosomal proteases cleave the linker to release the cytotoxic payload.

## Mechanism of Action

The antibody component binds Nectin-4 on urothelial cancer cells, triggering receptor-mediated endocytosis. In the lysosome, cathepsin B cleaves the Val-Cit linker, releasing MMAE. The free MMAE binds tubulin, inhibiting microtubule polymerization and causing G2/M arrest and apoptosis. Bystander killing of Nectin-4-negative neighboring cells also occurs.

## Clinical Applications

- **Urothelial carcinoma**: Locally advanced or metastatic, after platinum and PD-1/PD-L1 therapy (Padcev)
- **First-line urothelial cancer**: With pembrolizumab (EV-302 trial)
- **Nectin-4-expressing solid tumors**: Under investigation
- **Bladder preservation**: Neoadjuvant trials ongoing

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 3.6 days (antibody), 2.8 days (MMAE)
- **Cmax (MMAE)**: ~5 ng/mL
- **Steady state**: ~3 weeks
- **Metabolism**: MMAE metabolized by CYP3A4
- **Route**: IV infusion (days 1, 8, 15 of 28-day cycle)

## Safety and Side Effects

Peripheral neuropathy (50%), fatigue (51%), rash (including fatal skin reactions), hyperglycemia, ocular disorders (keratitis), neutropenia, and diarrhea. Fatal skin reactions (SJS/TEN-like) led to boxed warning.
