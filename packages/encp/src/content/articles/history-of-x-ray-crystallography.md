## Overview

X-ray crystallography has provided the majority of atomic-resolution structures for peptides and proteins since the early 20th century.

## Key Milestones

- **1895**: Wilhelm Rontgen discovers X-rays
- **1912**: Max von Laue demonstrates X-ray diffraction
- **1953**: Watson and Crick determine DNA structure
- **1958**: Kendrew determines myoglobin structure
- **1960**: Perutz determines hemoglobin structure
- **1962**: Nobel Prize to Kendrew and Perutz
- **2000s**: Synchrotron radiation advances

## Impact

X-ray crystallography remains the gold standard for high-resolution peptide and protein structures, essential for drug design and mechanistic studies.
