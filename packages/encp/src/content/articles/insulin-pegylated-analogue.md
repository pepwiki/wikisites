## Overview

PEGylation attaches polyethylene glycol chains to insulin, dramatically increasing hydrodynamic radius and reducing renal clearance. This approach extends half-life while maintaining bioactivity.

## Key Features

- **Half-life extension**: 10-100x depending on PEG size
- **Mechanism**: Reduced glomerular filtration
- **Trade-off**: Potential reduced receptor binding
- **Applications**: Ultra-long-acting basal insulins

## Challenges

- Site-specific PEGylation required
- PEG size optimization
- Maintaining receptor binding affinity
- Immunogenicity considerations
