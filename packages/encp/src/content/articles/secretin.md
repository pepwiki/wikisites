## Structure

Secretin is a 27-amino acid linear peptide hormone and the first hormone to be discovered (1902). It belongs to the secretin-glucagon-VIP family, sharing structural homology with these peptides. The C-terminal amide is essential for receptor binding.

## Mechanism of Action

Secretin binds secretin receptors on pancreatic ductal cells, activating adenylyl cyclase and increasing cAMP. This stimulates copious bicarbonate-rich fluid secretion that neutralizes gastric acid entering the duodenum. Secretin also stimulates bile flow and inhibits gastric acid secretion.

## Clinical Applications

- **Pancreatic function testing**: Secretin stimulation test for exocrine insufficiency
- **Gastrinoma diagnosis**: Secretin provocative test
- **Autism**: Investigational (IV secretin for GI symptoms)
- **Gastric acid regulation**: Endogenous acid neutralization

## Pharmacology

- **Half-life**: 3-5 minutes
- **Release**: S-cells (duodenum) in response to acid (pH <4.5)
- **Target**: Pancreatic ductal cells
- **Analogs**: Not widely used clinically
