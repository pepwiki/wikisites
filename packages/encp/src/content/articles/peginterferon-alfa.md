## Structure

Peginterferon alfa is interferon alfa conjugated to polyethylene glycol (PEG). Two forms exist: peginterferon alfa-2a (Pegasys, 40 kDa branched PEG) and peginterferon alfa-2b (Peg-Intron, 12 kDa linear PEG). PEGylation reduces renal clearance and immunogenicity while extending half-life.

## Mechanism of Action

Like native IFN-alpha, peginterferon activates IFNAR1/IFNAR2, inducing ISGs with antiviral, antiproliferative, and immunomodulatory effects. The extended half-life maintains sustained interferon signaling with once-weekly dosing, improving efficacy and adherence.

## Clinical Applications

- **Hepatitis B**: Peginterferon alfa-2a (first-line option)
- **Hepatitis C**: With ribavirin (historical standard, now replaced by DAAs)
- **Hairy cell leukemia**: Peginterferon alfa-2a
- **Essential thrombocythemia**: Off-label
- **Melanoma**: Adjuvant therapy (high-dose)

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 77 hours (Pegasys), 54 hours (Peg-Intron)
- **Tmax**: 72-96 hours (Pegasys), 15-44 hours (Peg-Intron)
- **Bioavailability**: 61-84% (SC)
- **Dosing**: Weekly SC injection
- **Route**: SC

## Safety and Side Effects

Flu-like symptoms (50-80%), fatigue (60%), depression (20-30%), neutropenia, thrombocytopenia, hepatotoxicity, autoimmune thyroiditis, and retinopathy. Similar to interferon alfa but with more sustained exposure.
