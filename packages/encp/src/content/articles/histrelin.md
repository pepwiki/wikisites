## Structure

Histrelin is a synthetic nonapeptide GnRH agonist with N-ethyl-D-histidine at position 6 and ethylamide at the C-terminus. It is formulated as a non-biodegradable hydrogel implant (Supprelin LA, Vantas) that provides continuous drug release for 12 months through diffusion-controlled mechanism.

## Mechanism of Action

Histrelin is one of the most potent GnRH agonists available. After initial stimulation, sustained receptor occupancy causes profound downregulation and desensitization of pituitary gonadotrophs, suppressing LH and FSH to prepubertal or castrate levels.

## Clinical Applications

- **Central precocious puberty**: First-line treatment (Supprelin LA)
- **Prostate cancer**: Androgen deprivation therapy (Vantas)
- **Gender-affirming care**: Puberty suppression in transgender youth
- **Endometriosis**: Severe cases refractory to other treatments

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 1-2 hours (from implant)
- **Duration**: 12 months per implant
- **Steady state**: Achieved within 1-2 weeks
- **Elimination**: Renal and hepatic
- **Route**: Subcutaneous implant (inner upper arm)

## Safety and Side Effects

Hot flashes, mood changes, fatigue, headache, weight gain, initial disease flare, and implant site complications (migration, infection, breakage). Implant insertion and removal require minor surgical procedure.
