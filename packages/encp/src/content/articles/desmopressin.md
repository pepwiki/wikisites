## Structure

Desmopressin (DDAVP) is a synthetic analog of vasopressin with two key modifications: deamination of the N-terminal cysteine (increasing V2 selectivity and resistance to vasopressinase) and substitution of D-arginine for L-arginine at position 8 (reducing vasopressor activity).

## Mechanism of Action

Desmopressin selectively activates V2 receptors in the renal collecting duct, promoting cAMP-mediated aquaporin-2 insertion and water reabsorption. It also stimulates endothelial V2 receptors to release von Willebrand factor and factor VIII from storage sites.

## Clinical Applications

- **Central diabetes insipidus**: Replacement of deficient ADH
- **Primary nocturnal enuresis**: First-line pharmacotherapy
- **Hemophilia A and von Willebrand disease**: Release of stored coagulation factors
- **Uremic bleeding**: Correction of platelet dysfunction

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 2-3 hours (IV), 4-8 hours (intranasal)
- **Onset**: 1 hour (oral), 30 minutes (intranasal)
- **Duration**: 8-12 hours
- **Bioavailability**: 5% (oral), 10-20% (intranasal)
- **Route**: IV, intranasal, oral, sublingual

## Safety and Side Effects

Hyponatremia (most serious, risk of seizures), headache, nausea, abdominal cramps, and rare hyponatremic seizures in children. Fluid restriction mandatory during treatment.
