## Structure

Buserelin is a synthetic nonapeptide GnRH agonist with D-serine at position 6, tert-butyl serine amide at the C-terminus, and removal of glycine at position 10. These modifications increase receptor affinity and metabolic stability compared to native GnRH.

## Mechanism of Action

Buserelin initially stimulates then desensitizes pituitary GnRH receptors through receptor downregulation and post-receptor signaling defects. This suppresses gonadotropin release and sex hormone production, achieving medical castration with continued administration.

## Clinical Applications

- **Prostate cancer**: Androgen deprivation therapy (Suprefact)
- **IVF**: Pituitary downregulation in controlled ovarian stimulation
- **Endometriosis**: Hormonal suppression of endometrial implants
- **Premenstrual syndrome**: Severe refractory cases
- **Breast cancer**: Premenopausal advanced disease

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 80 minutes (SC), 3 hours (nasal)
- **Bioavailability**: 70% (nasal), 90% (SC)
- **Duration**: Continuous suppression with daily dosing
- **Metabolism**: Proteolytic degradation
- **Route**: SC injection, nasal spray

## Safety and Side Effects

Hot flashes, decreased libido, erectile dysfunction, osteoporosis, initial disease flare, headache, mood changes, and nasal irritation (nasal formulation).
