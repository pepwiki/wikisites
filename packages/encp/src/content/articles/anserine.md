## Overview

β-alanyl-Nτ-methyl-L-histidine. Dipeptide found in skeletal muscle of birds, rabbits, and marine fish. Functions as intracellular pH buffer (pKa ~9.5) and antioxidant (scavenges singlet oxygen, hydroxyl radicals). More resistant to oxidation than carnosine due to N-methylation. Found in chicken, turkey, rabbit meat. Concentration: 1-3 μmol/g in muscle tissue.

## Clinical Significance

This molecule has important therapeutic or diagnostic applications in clinical medicine.
