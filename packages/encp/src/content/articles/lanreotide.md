## Structure

Lanreotide is a synthetic cyclic octapeptide analog of somatostatin containing D-tryptophan, L-lysine, L-threonine, D-cysteine, and L-cysteine residues in a disulfide-bridged ring structure. The Autogel formulation provides sustained release over 28 days from a supersaturated aqueous solution.

## Mechanism of Action

Lanreotide binds preferentially to somatostatin receptor subtype 2 (SSTR2) and to a lesser extent SSTR5, inhibiting growth hormone, TSH, and various gastrointestinal hormone secretion. In neuroendocrine tumors, it suppresses tumor growth through anti-proliferative and anti-angiogenic effects.

## Clinical Applications

- **Acromegaly**: Control of GH and IGF-1 excess (Somatuline)
- **Neuroendocrine tumors**: Carcinoid syndrome symptom control
- **Thyrotropinoma**: TSH-secreting pituitary adenomas
- **Hepatocellular carcinoma**: TACE combination therapy

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 23-30 days (Autogel)
- **Tmax**: 6-24 hours (Autogel)
- **Duration**: 28 days per injection
- **Protein binding**: 78%
- **Route**: Deep SC (Autogel)

## Safety and Side Effects

GI disturbances (diarrhea, steatorrhea, abdominal pain), gallstones (20-30%), bradycardia, hyperglycemia, injection site reactions, and vitamin B12 malabsorption with long-term use.
