## Structure

Enfuvirtide (T-20, Fuzeon) is a synthetic 36-amino acid peptide corresponding to a region of the HIV-1 gp41 transmembrane glycoprotein. It mimics the C-terminal heptad repeat region of gp41 that binds to the N-terminal heptad repeat, preventing the conformational change required for membrane fusion.

## Mechanism of Action

During HIV entry, gp41 undergoes a conformational change exposing the N-terminal and C-terminal heptad repeat regions, which fold into a six-helix bundle that drives membrane fusion. Enfuvirtide binds to the N-terminal heptad repeat, preventing six-helix bundle formation and blocking the final step of viral-cell membrane fusion.

## Clinical Applications

- **HIV-1 infection**: Treatment-experienced patients with multidrug-resistant virus (Fuzeon)
- **TROTORO and TORO trials**: Significant viral load reduction in heavily pretreated patients
- **Salvage therapy**: When other antiretroviral options exhausted
- **Bridge therapy**: While awaiting other treatment options

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 3.8 hours
- **Tmax**: 4-8 hours (SC)
- **Bioavailability**: 84% (SC)
- **Metabolism**: Proteolytic degradation
- **Dosing**: 90 mg SC twice daily
- **Route**: SC injection (thigh, abdomen, upper arm)

## Safety and Side Effects

Injection site reactions (98%, most common reason for discontinuation), bacterial pneumonia (increased risk), insomnia, depression, peripheral neuropathy, and eosinophilia. Requires proper injection technique training.
