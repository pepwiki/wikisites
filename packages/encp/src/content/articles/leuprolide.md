## Structure

Leuprolide is a synthetic nonapeptide analog of GnRH with D-leucine at position 6 and ethylamide substitution at the C-terminus. These modifications increase receptor binding affinity 20-fold and resist enzymatic degradation, extending half-life from 3-4 minutes (native GnRH) to 3 hours.

## Mechanism of Action

Initially, leuprolide stimulates GnRH receptors (flare effect), causing transient LH/FSH and testosterone increase. With continued administration, receptor downregulation and desensitization occur, suppressing gonadotropin release and reducing testosterone to castrate levels within 2-4 weeks.

## Clinical Applications

- **Prostate cancer**: Androgen deprivation therapy (Lupron)
- **Endometriosis**: Pain reduction and lesion regression
- **Uterine fibroids**: Pre-surgical volume reduction
- **Precocious puberty**: Central precocious puberty
- **Breast cancer**: Premenopausal hormone receptor-positive disease
- **IVF**: Controlled ovarian stimulation protocols

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 3 hours (single dose)
- **Tmax**: 2-6 hours (depot formulations provide sustained release)
- **Duration**: 1 month, 3 months, 4 months, or 6 months (depot)
- **Route**: IM depot, SC, implant
- **Bioavailability**: 90% (subcutaneous)

## Safety and Side Effects

Hot flashes (60-80%), fatigue, erectile dysfunction, decreased libido, osteoporosis (long-term), gynecomastia, weight gain, and initial disease flare. Bone density monitoring required for long-term use.
