## Structure

Insulin aspart replaces proline with aspartic acid at position B28 of the insulin B chain. The negative charge of the aspartic acid residue reduces self-association, preventing hexamer formation and promoting rapid monomer absorption from subcutaneous tissue.

## Mechanism of Action

Activates insulin receptor tyrosine kinase for glucose uptake and hepatic glucose suppression. The faster onset better mimics physiological mealtime insulin secretion compared to regular human insulin.

## Clinical Applications

Prandial insulin for diabetes management. Used in basal-bolus regimens and insulin pump therapy. The BEGIN trial program demonstrated non-inferior HbA1c reduction with improved postprandial glucose control versus regular insulin.

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 1.2 hours
- **Tmax**: 40-50 minutes
- **Onset**: 10-20 minutes
- **Duration**: 3-5 hours
- **Bioavailability**: 38-54%

## Safety and Side Effects

Hypoglycemia (most common), weight gain, injection site reactions, and rare allergic responses. Fiasp (faster-acting insulin aspart) contains niacinamide for even faster absorption.
