## Structure

Insulin degludec has a C16 fatty diacid (hexadecanedioic acid) linked via a glutamic acid spacer to lysine B29, with threonine B30 deleted. In the injection depot, degludec forms multi-hexamer chains that slowly disassemble, providing ultra-long duration of action.

## Mechanism of Action

After subcutaneous injection, degludec self-associates into soluble multi-hexamer chains. Slow detachment of monomers and subsequent albumin binding creates a smooth, ultra-long glucose-lowering effect lasting >42 hours with minimal peak-to-trough fluctuation.

## Clinical Applications

Once-daily basal insulin for type 1 and type 2 diabetes. BEGIN and SWITCH trials demonstrated comparable glycemic control with 25-36% reduction in nocturnal hypoglycemia versus insulin glargine U100. Flexibility in dosing time (minimum 8 hours between doses).

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 25 hours
- **Tmax**: 9 hours
- **Effective duration**: >42 hours
- **Steady state**: 2-3 days
- **Bioavailability**: 89%

## Safety and Side Effects

Hypoglycemia (lower nocturnal risk than glargine), weight gain, injection site reactions. Cardiovascular safety confirmed in DEVOTE trial.
