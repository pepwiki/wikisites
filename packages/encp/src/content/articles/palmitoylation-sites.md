## Overview

S-palmitoylation attaches palmitate (C16) to cysteine residues via thioester bonds, regulating membrane association.

## Characteristics

Unlike prenylation, palmitoylation is reversible, allowing dynamic regulation of protein localization.

## Biological Significance

Palmitoylation regulates Ras signaling, receptor trafficking, and synaptic protein function. DHHC enzymes are the palmitoyl acyltransferases.
