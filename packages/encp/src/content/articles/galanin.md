## Physiological Functions

### Appetite Regulation
- Galanin stimulates feeding (potent orexigenic)
- Co-localized with NPY in arcuate nucleus
- GalR2 mediates feeding stimulation
- GalR1 mediates fat intake preference

### Pain Modulation
- **Analgesic**: Galanin in dorsal horn reduces pain transmission
- **Neuropathic pain**: GalR1 activation reduces hypersensitivity
- **Co-localized** with substance P and CGRP in DRG neurons

### Cognition and Memory
- Galanin modulates cholinergic transmission in hippocampus
- GalR1 antagonists improve memory in animal models
- **Alzheimer's disease**: Galanin is upregulated in surviving neurons

### Neuroprotection
- GalR2 activation protects neurons from excitotoxicity
- Galanin promotes neurite outgrowth
- Anti-inflammatory effects in CNS

| GalR1 antagonist | GalR2260 | Memory | Phase II |

### Disease Associations
- **Alzheimer's disease**: Galanin upregulation in surviving neurons
- **Epilepsy**: Galanin is anticonvulsant
- **Depression**: GalR1 polymorphisms associated with depression
- **Obesity**: Galanin stimulates feeding, GalR2 antagonists reduce body weight
