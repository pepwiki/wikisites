## Structure

Relugolix is a non-peptide, orally bioavailable GnRH receptor antagonist. While not a classical peptide, it represents an important class of therapeutics targeting peptide hormone receptors. The molecule contains a quinoline core with urea and sulfonamide functional groups.

## Mechanism of Action

Relugolix competitively binds to GnRH receptors in the anterior pituitary, blocking endogenous GnRH signaling. This immediately suppresses LH and FSH secretion, rapidly reducing testosterone production without the initial testosterone surge seen with GnRH agonists. Castrate-level testosterone is achieved within days.

## Clinical Applications

- **Prostate cancer**: Androgen deprivation therapy (Orgovyx)
- **Uterine fibroids**: Heavy menstrual bleeding (Myfembree with estradiol/norethindrone)
- **Endometriosis**: Pain management (Myfembree)
- **Advanced prostate cancer**: HERO trial demonstrated non-inferiority to leuprolide

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 34-64 hours
- **Tmax**: 1-2 hours
- **Bioavailability**: 49% (with high-fat meal)
- **Metabolism**: CYP3A (minor), UGT1A1
- **Elimination**: Fecal (73%), renal (4%)
- **Route**: Oral

## Safety and Side Effects

Hot flashes (54%), fatigue (14%), constipation (12%), diarrhea (9%), and musculoskeletal pain. No testosterone flare. Avoid with strong CYP3A inducers. Cardiac monitoring recommended in patients with congenital QT prolongation.
