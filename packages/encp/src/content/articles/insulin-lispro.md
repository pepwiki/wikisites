## Structure

Insulin lispro is a rapid-acting insulin analog produced by reversing the proline and lysine residues at positions B28 and B29 of the insulin B chain. This modification reduces dimerization and hexamer formation in subcutaneous tissue, allowing faster absorption into the bloodstream.

## Mechanism of Action

Binds insulin receptor to promote glucose uptake and suppress hepatic glucose output. The rapid absorption profile matches physiological prandial insulin release, reducing postprandial glucose excursions.

## Clinical Applications

Prandial insulin for type 1 and type 2 diabetes. Can be dosed immediately before or after meals. Clinical trials show equivalent HbA1c reduction to regular insulin with improved postprandial glucose control and reduced late postprandial hypoglycemia.

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 1 hour
- **Tmax**: 30-90 minutes
- **Onset**: 15 minutes
- **Duration**: 3-5 hours
- **Bioavailability**: 55-77%

## Safety and Side Effects

Hypoglycemia, weight gain, injection site reactions. Risk of hypoglycemia is lower than regular insulin in the 2-4 hour postprandial window.
