## Overview

Peptide mimetics are molecules that mimic the biological activity of natural peptides while overcoming their limitations.

## Types

- **Small molecule mimetics**: Non-peptide compounds
- **Peptide hybrids**: Mixed peptide-small molecule structures
- **Beta-peptides**: Beta-amino acid backbone
- **Cyclic peptides**: Conformationally constrained

## Applications

Peptide mimetics improve oral bioavailability, metabolic stability, and receptor selectivity. Examples include tirofiban (GPIIb/IIIa mimetic) and boceprevir (protease inhibitor).
