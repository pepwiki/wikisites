## Chemical Identity

| Property          | Value                                    |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Molecular Weight  | ~153 kDa (ADC)                           |
| Peptide Payload   | Monomethyl auristatin E (MMAE)           |
| Linker            | Valine-citrulline (protease-cleavable)   |
| DAR               | ~4                                       |
| Target            | CD30                                     |

## Structure

Brentuximab vedotin (Adcetris) is a chimeric anti-CD30 IgG1 monoclonal antibody conjugated to 4 molecules of MMAE via a valine-citrulline dipeptide linker. The linker is stable in plasma but cleaved by lysosomal cathepsins after receptor-mediated endocytosis.

## Mechanism of Action

The antibody binds CD30 on Hodgkin Reed-Sternberg cells and ALCL cells, triggering internalization. After lysosomal cleavage of the Val-Cit linker, released MMAE disrupts microtubule networks, causing cell cycle arrest and apoptosis. Bystander effect kills neighboring CD30-negative cells.

## Clinical Applications

- **Classical Hodgkin lymphoma**: After autologous stem cell transplant failure, or frontline with AVD
- **Anaplastic large cell lymphoma**: Systemic ALCL after prior chemotherapy
- **Cutaneous T-cell lymphoma**: CD30-expressing mycosis fungoides
- **CD30-expressing lymphomas**: Various subtypes

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 4-6 days (ADC), 3-4 days (MMAE)
- **Steady state**: ~21 days
- **Cmax (MMAE)**: ~5 ng/mL
- **Metabolism**: MMAE by CYP3A4
- **Route**: IV infusion every 3 weeks (1.8 mg/kg)

## Safety and Side Effects

Peripheral neuropathy (dose-limiting, cumulative), neutropenia, thrombocytopenia, anemia, fatigue, nausea, diarrhea, and rare progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy (JC virus). Stevens-Johnson syndrome reported rarely.
