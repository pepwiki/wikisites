## Structure

Goserelin is a synthetic decapeptide GnRH agonist with D-serine at position 6 and an azaglycine amide at the C-terminus. The molecule is formulated as a biodegradable poly(D,L-lactide-co-glycolide) implant that provides sustained release over 1 or 3 months.

## Mechanism of Action

Like other GnRH agonists, goserelin initially stimulates then downregulates pituitary GnRH receptors, achieving castrate testosterone levels within 2-4 weeks. The sustained-release implant maintains continuous receptor occupancy, preventing gonadotropin recovery.

## Clinical Applications

- **Prostate cancer**: Androgen deprivation therapy (Zoladex)
- **Breast cancer**: Premenopausal HR+ advanced breast cancer
- **Endometriosis**: Pain management and lesion reduction
- **Uterine fibroids**: Pre-surgical volume reduction
- **IVF**: Pituitary downregulation protocols

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 4-5 hours (from implant)
- **Duration**: 28 days (3.6 mg) or 12 weeks (10.8 mg)
- **Steady state**: Within 12-24 hours of implant insertion
- **Elimination**: Renal
- **Route**: SC implant (abdomen)

## Safety and Side Effects

Hot flashes, sexual dysfunction, osteoporosis, fatigue, initial disease flare, weight gain, mood changes, and injection site reactions. Bone density monitoring recommended for treatment >6 months.
