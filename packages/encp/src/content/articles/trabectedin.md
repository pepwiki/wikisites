## Structure

Trabectedin (ecteinascidin 743) is a tetrahydroisoquinoline alkaloid originally isolated from the tunicate Ecteinascidia turbinata, now produced semi-synthetically. It consists of three fused tetrahydroisoquinoline rings (A, B, C subunits) with a bridged structure. The molecule contains a bridging sulfide bond and multiple stereocenters.

## Mechanism of Action

Trabectedin binds to the minor groove of DNA, with the A-ring portion alkylating the N2 of guanine. This induces a bend toward the major groove, disrupting transcription factor binding (particularly NF-Y), blocking cell cycle progression, and inducing apoptosis. It also affects DNA repair pathways by interfering with nucleotide excision repair.

## Clinical Applications

- **Soft tissue sarcoma**: After failure of anthracyclines and ifosfamide (Yondelis)
- **Ovarian cancer**: Platinum-sensitive relapse in combination with PEG-liposomal doxorubicin
- **BRCA-mutated tumors**: Enhanced sensitivity due to DNA repair deficiency
- **Liposarcoma and leiomyosarcoma**: Specific activity in these subtypes

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 180 hours (terminal)
- **Protein binding**: 97%
- **Metabolism**: CYP3A4 (primary)
- **Elimination**: Primarily fecal
- **Route**: IV infusion over 24 hours every 3 weeks

## Safety and Side Effects

Neutropenia (dose-limiting), hepatotoxicity (elevated transaminases), nausea, fatigue, constipation, and rhabdomyolysis (rare). Premedication with dexamethasone reduces hepatotoxicity risk. Avoid with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors.
