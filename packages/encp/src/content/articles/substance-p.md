## Structure

Substance P is an 11-amino acid member of the tachykinin peptide family, characterized by the conserved C-terminal sequence Phe-X-Gly-Leu-Met-NH2. It is produced from the preprotachykinin A (PPT-A) gene by proteolytic processing. The C-terminal amidation is essential for receptor binding.

## Mechanism of Action

Substance P binds preferentially to NK1 receptors (neurokinin 1), activating Gq/PLC signaling and increasing intracellular calcium. This mediates pain transmission in the dorsal horn, neurogenic inflammation (vasodilation, plasma extravasation), smooth muscle contraction, and emesis through NK1 receptors in the brainstem.

## Clinical Applications

- **Emesis**: NK1 antagonists (aprepitant) for chemotherapy-induced nausea
- **Pain**: Substance P involvement in nociceptive transmission
- **Neurogenic inflammation**: Asthma, inflammatory bowel disease
- **Depression**: NK1 antagonists investigated as antidepressants
- **Respiratory**: Bronchoconstriction and mucus secretion

## Pharmacology

- **Half-life**: 1-2 minutes (rapid degradation by NEP and ACE)
- **Release**: C-fiber nociceptors, enteric neurons
- **NK1 receptor**: High affinity (nM range)
- **Co-release**: With CGRP and glutamate
