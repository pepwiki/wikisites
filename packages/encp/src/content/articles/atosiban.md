## Structure

Atosiban is a synthetic peptide antagonist of oxytocin and vasopressin V1a receptors. It is a cyclic peptide derived from modifications of oxytocin structure, containing a modified disulfide bridge and substitutions that convert agonist activity to antagonist activity.

## Mechanism of Action

Atosiban competitively blocks oxytocin receptors on uterine myometrium, inhibiting oxytocin-mediated calcium mobilization and reducing frequency and force of uterine contractions. It also has weak V1a antagonist activity. The combined effect delays preterm labor.

## Clinical Applications

- **Preterm labor**: Tocolytic to delay delivery for 48 hours to allow corticosteroid administration
- **Fetal maturation**: Enables antenatal corticosteroid therapy
- **Tocolysis**: Alternative to beta-agonists with fewer cardiovascular side effects
- **Approved in Europe**: Not FDA-approved in the United States

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 1.7 hours
- **Steady state**: Within 1 hour of infusion
- **Metabolism**: Liver (cysteine aminopeptidase)
- **Elimination**: Renal and fecal
- **Dose**: Loading dose 6.75 mg IV bolus, then 300 mcg/min for 3 hours, then 100 mcg/min for up to 45 hours

## Safety and Side Effects

Nausea, vomiting, headache, dizziness, and injection site reactions. Fewer cardiovascular side effects than beta-agonist tocolytics. Contraindicated before 24 weeks gestation, placenta previa, eclampsia, and fetal distress.
