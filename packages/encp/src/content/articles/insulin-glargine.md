## Structure

Insulin glargine is a recombinant human insulin analog with two modifications: substitution of asparagine for glycine at position A21 and addition of two arginine residues at the C-terminus of the B chain. These modifications shift the isoelectric point from 5.4 to 6.7, making the molecule soluble at acidic pH but precipitating at physiological pH after subcutaneous injection.

## Mechanism of Action

Insulin glargine binds to the insulin receptor tyrosine kinase, activating PI3K/Akt signaling to promote glucose uptake in skeletal muscle and adipose tissue while suppressing hepatic glucose production. The microprecipitate formation at the injection site provides slow, steady release over 24 hours.

## Clinical Applications

Basal insulin for type 1 and type 2 diabetes. LANTUS trials demonstrated comparable HbA1c reduction to NPH insulin with 25% reduction in nocturnal hypoglycemia. Once-daily dosing improves adherence compared to twice-daily NPH regimens.

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 12 hours (effective 24-hour duration)
- **Tmax**: 4-8 hours (flat profile)
- **Bioavailability**: 60% after subcutaneous injection
- **Onset**: 1-2 hours
- **Duration**: 20-24 hours

## Safety and Side Effects

Hypoglycemia (most common), weight gain (2-4 kg), injection site reactions, and rare cases of lipodystrophy. No peak effect reduces nocturnal hypoglycemia risk versus NPH insulin.
