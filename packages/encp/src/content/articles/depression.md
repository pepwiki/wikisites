## Overview

Major depressive disorder (MDD) characterized by persistent depressed mood or anhedonia for ≥2 weeks, plus ≥4 additional symptoms (weight change, sleep disturbance, psychomotor changes, fatigue, worthlessness, concentration difficulties, suicidal ideation). Pathophysiology: monoamine hypothesis (serotonin, norepinephrine, dopamine deficits), HPA axis dysregulation, neuroinflammation, neuroplasticity deficits. Treatments: SSRIs (fluoxetine, sertraline), SNRIs (venlafaxine, duloxetine), TCAs, MAOIs, ketamine/esketamine (nasal spray), ECT, TMS.
