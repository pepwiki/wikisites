## Structure

Adrenomedullin (ADM) is a 52-amino acid vasodilatory peptide with a single intramolecular disulfide bond forming a 6-membered ring in the N-terminal region and a C-terminal amidation. It belongs to the calcitonin gene-related peptide (CGRP) family and shares structural homology with amylin and CGRP.

## Mechanism of Action

ADM binds calcitonin receptor-like receptor (CLR) with RAMP2 (AM1 receptor) or RAMP3 (AM2 receptor), activating adenylyl cyclase and increasing cAMP. This causes potent vasodilation, natriuresis, inhibition of aldosterone, and cardioprotective effects including anti-fibrotic and anti-apoptotic actions.

## Clinical Applications

- **Heart failure**: Elevated levels as compensatory mechanism
- **Pulmonary hypertension**: Vasodilatory and anti-remodeling effects
- **Sepsis**: Elevated levels correlate with severity
- **Pre-eclampsia**: Altered ADM levels in pregnancy complications
- **Cancer**: ADM promotes tumor angiogenesis

## Pharmacology

- **Half-life**: 22 minutes (plasma)
- **Potency**: 10x more potent than CGRP as vasodilator
- **Distribution**: Adrenal medulla, heart, vasculature, brain
- **Receptor**: CLR/RAMP complexes (shared with CGRP and amylin)
