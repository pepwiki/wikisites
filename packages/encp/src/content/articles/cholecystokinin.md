### Signaling

```
CCK → CCK-A (GI) or CCK-B (Brain)
    → Gαq → PLC → IP₃ + DAG
    → ↑ Ca²⁺ → Smooth muscle contraction
    → ↑ cAMP → Enzyme secretion
```

## Physiological Functions

### Gallbladder Contraction
- CCK-8S is the primary stimulus for gallbladder contraction
- Released by I-cells in response to dietary fat and amino acids
- Activates CCK-A receptors on gallbladder smooth muscle
- Results in bile release into duodenum for fat digestion

### Pancreatic Secretion
- Stimulates pancreatic acinar cells via CCK-A receptors
- Increases enzyme (amylase, lipase, protease) secretion
- Synergistic with secretin for bicarbonate secretion

### Satiety
- CCK-8 released from GI tract during meals
- Activates vagal afferents (CCK-A receptors)
- Signals to hypothalamus → appetite suppression
- **Short-term**: Reduces meal size
- **Long-term**: Weight regulation

### Other Functions
- **Gastric acid**: Modulates gastric acid secretion
- **GI motility**: Delays gastric emptying
- **Brain**: Neurotransmitter (CCK-B receptors)
- **Pain**: Modulates visceral pain perception

### Diagnostic Applications
- **CCK-stimulated cholescystigraphy**: CCK injection → gallbladder contraction → diagnostic imaging
- **CCK-HIDA scan**: Functional gallbladder assessment
- **Serum CCK levels**: Biomarker for GI disorders

### Disease Associations
- **Gallstones**: CCK hypersecretion may contribute
- **Pancreatitis**: CCK receptor dysfunction
- **Obesity**: CCK signaling defects
- **Anxiety**: CCK-B receptor polymorphisms
