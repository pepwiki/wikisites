## Overview

Peptide vaccines have evolved from simple epitope-based approaches to sophisticated multi-epitope and personalized designs.

## Key Milestones

- **1980s**: T-cell epitope identification
- **1990s**: First cancer peptide vaccine trials
- **1995**: HLA-restricted epitope peptides
- **2000s**: Multi-epitope vaccine designs
- **2010s**: Neoantigen discovery platforms
- **2020s**: Personalized mRNA-peptide vaccines

## Advantages

Safety, stability, rapid design, and specificity make peptide vaccines attractive for infectious disease and oncology.

## Current Applications

HPV, melanoma, and personalized cancer vaccines represent the most advanced peptide vaccine programs.
