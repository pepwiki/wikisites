## Structure

Angiotensin II (Ang II) is an octapeptide produced from angiotensinogen through sequential cleavage by renin (to Ang I) and angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) (to Ang II). The His6-Pro7 bond is essential for receptor binding. Ang II is the primary effector of the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS).

## Mechanism of Action

Ang II binds AT1 receptors (vasoconstriction, aldosterone, inflammation, fibrosis) and AT2 receptors (vasodilation, anti-proliferation). AT1 activation causes potent vasoconstriction, sodium and water retention (via aldosterone), sympathetic nervous system activation, cardiac hypertrophy, and vascular remodeling.

## Clinical Applications

- **Blood pressure regulation**: Primary endogenous vasopressor
- **Angiotensin II (Giapreza)**: Synthetic Ang II for distributive shock
- **RAAS blockers**: ACE inhibitors, ARBs target Ang II signaling
- **Heart failure**: RAAS activation in disease progression
- **Kidney disease**: Ang II-mediated glomerular hypertension

## Pharmacology

- **Half-life**: 1-2 minutes (circulating), effects sustained by local production
- **Potency**: 40x more potent than Ang I as vasoconstrictor
- **Production**: ACE in lungs (primary), local tissue ACE
- **Degradation**: Aminopeptidases (to Ang III), ACE2 (to Ang 1-7)
