## Structure

Pasireotide is a cyclohexapeptide somatostatin analog with a unique chemical structure incorporating four unnatural amino acids: D-tryptophan, 4-(2-aminoethyl)-phenylacetyl-phenylalanine, O-benzyl-L-tyrosine, and O-benzyl-D-lysine. This structure enables binding to four of five somatostatin receptor subtypes.

## Mechanism of Action

Pasireotide binds with high affinity to SSTR1, SSTR2, SSTR3, and SSTR5, with 30-40-fold greater SSTR5 affinity than octreotide. In Cushing's disease, SSTR5 activation on corticotroph adenomas suppresses ACTH secretion. In acromegaly, multi-receptor binding provides GH/IGF-1 control in patients resistant to SSTR2-selective analogs.

## Clinical Applications

- **Cushing's disease**: First medical therapy approved (Signifor)
- **Acromegaly**: Second-line when SSTR2-selective analogs fail (Signifor LAR)
- **Neuroendocrine tumors**: Carcinoid syndrome and tumor control
- **TSH-secreting adenomas**: Off-label use

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 12 hours (SC), 15 days (LAR)
- **Tmax**: 0.5-0.75 hours (SC)
- **Protein binding**: 88%
- **Metabolism**: Hepatic (carboxylesterase)
- **Route**: SC (twice daily) or IM LAR (monthly)

## Safety and Side Effects

Hyperglycemia (most significant, 73%), diabetes mellitus (46%), diarrhea, nausea, cholelithiasis, QT prolongation, bradycardia, and injection site reactions. Requires glucose monitoring before and during treatment.
