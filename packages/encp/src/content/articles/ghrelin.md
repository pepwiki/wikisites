## Structure

Ghrelin is a 28-amino acid peptide hormone primarily produced by X/A-like cells in the gastric fundus. It has a unique octanoyl (C8:0) modification on serine at position 3, catalyzed by ghrelin O-acyltransferase (GOAT). This acylation is essential for biological activity and receptor binding.

## Mechanism of Action

Octanoylated ghrelin binds the growth hormone secretagogue receptor (GHS-R1a), stimulating GH release from pituitary somatotrophs and activating hypothalamic appetite circuits (AgRP/NPY neurons). It promotes food intake, adipogenesis, and gastric motility. Des-acyl ghrelin (without octanoylation) has distinct metabolic effects.

## Clinical Applications

- **Appetite regulation**: Primary endogenous orexigenic hormone
- **GH release**: Synergizes with GHRH for pulsatile GH secretion
- **Cachexia**: Therapeutic target in cancer and chronic disease cachexia
- **Obesity**: Ghrelin levels suppressed in obesity, elevated after weight loss
- **Gastroparesis**: Promotes gastric motility

## Pharmacology

- **Half-life**: 27-31 minutes (acylated)
- **Peak**: Preprandial rise, postprandial fall
- **Source**: 80% from stomach
- **GOAT**: Essential enzyme for activation
- **Levels**: Elevated by fasting, sleep deprivation, stress
