## Overview

The discovery of insulin in 1921 by Frederick Banting and Charles Best at the University of Toronto transformed diabetes from a fatal diagnosis into a manageable condition.

## Key Milestones

- **1869**: Paul Langerhans describes pancreatic islet cells
- **1889**: Minkowski and von Mering link pancreas to diabetes
- **1921**: Banting and Best isolate insulin from dog pancreas
- **1922**: First human injection of insulin
- **1923**: Banting and Macleod win Nobel Prize
- **1955**: Frederick Sanger sequences insulin
- **1963**: First synthetic insulin produced
- **1982**: Recombinant human insulin approved (Humulin)

## Impact

Insulin was the first peptide hormone to be isolated, sequenced, and produced recombinantly. It set the precedent for all subsequent peptide therapeutic development.
