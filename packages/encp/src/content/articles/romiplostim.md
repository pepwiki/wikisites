## Structure

Romiplostim (Nplate) is a peptibody consisting of two disulfide-linked identical polypeptide chains, each containing a human IgG1 Fc domain fused to a TPO receptor-mimetic peptide. Each Fc domain carries two copies of a 14-amino acid peptide that activates the TPO receptor.

## Mechanism of Action

The peptibody peptides bind the TPO receptor (c-Mpl) on megakaryocyte precursors, activating JAK2/STAT5 signaling. This stimulates megakaryocyte proliferation, differentiation, and platelet production. The Fc domain extends half-life through FcRn recycling.

## Clinical Applications

- **Immune thrombocytopenia**: Chronic ITP in adults (Nplate)
- **Myelodysplastic syndromes**: Thrombocytopenia management
- **Chemotherapy-induced thrombocytopenia**: Investigational
- **Surgical thrombocytopenia**: Pre-procedural platelet boost

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 3.5 days (dose-dependent)
- **Tmax**: 7-50 hours (SC)
- **Platelet response**: Peak at 12-15 days
- **Dosing**: Weight-based, titrated to platelet count
- **Route**: SC weekly

## Safety and Side Effects

Headache (29%), fatigue (19%), arthralgia (15%), bone marrow reticulin formation (reversible), and rebound thrombocytopenia upon discontinuation. Bone marrow biopsy recommended with prolonged use.
