## Structure

Insulin detemir is a soluble, long-acting insulin analog with a C14 (myristic) fatty acid acylated to lysine at position B29 and deletion of threonine at B30. The fatty acid side chain enables reversible binding to albumin in the subcutaneous depot and circulation, prolonging absorption and distribution.

## Mechanism of Action

Standard insulin receptor activation with albumin-mediated prolongation of action. Self-association into dihexamers in subcutaneous tissue further slows absorption, providing duration of 18-24 hours.

## Clinical Applications

Once- or twice-daily basal insulin for type 1 and type 2 diabetes. PREDICTIVE and BEGIN trials demonstrated lower intra-individual variability and less nocturnal hypoglycemia compared to NPH insulin, though higher doses were typically required.

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 5-7 hours (effective duration 18-24 hours)
- **Tmax**: 6-8 hours
- **Bioavailability**: 60-70%
- **Albumin binding**: >98%
- **Duration**: 18-24 hours (dose-dependent)

## Safety and Side Effects

Hypoglycemia, weight gain (less than NPH in some studies), injection site reactions. More variable absorption at very high doses compared to insulin glargine.
