## Structure

Nafarelin is a synthetic decapeptide GnRH agonist with D-naphthylalanine (D-2-Nal) at position 6. This aromatic D-amino acid provides 200-fold greater receptor affinity than native GnRH and excellent metabolic stability. The molecule is administered as a nasal spray (Synarel).

## Mechanism of Action

Nafarelin desensitizes pituitary GnRH receptors through sustained stimulation, leading to downregulation and reduced gonadotropin secretion. Estrogen levels decrease to menopausal range within 4-8 weeks, achieving therapeutic suppression of estrogen-dependent conditions.

## Clinical Applications

- **Endometriosis**: First-line hormonal therapy for pain management
- **Central precocious puberty**: Puberty suppression in children
- **Uterine fibroids**: Pre-surgical volume reduction
- **Fertility preservation**: Ovarian suppression during chemotherapy

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 3 hours
- **Bioavailability**: 2.8% (intranasal)
- **Tmax**: 10-40 minutes
- **Duration**: Continuous suppression with twice-daily dosing
- **Metabolism**: Proteolytic degradation
- **Route**: Intranasal spray

## Safety and Side Effects

Hot flashes, vaginal dryness, decreased libido, headache, nasal irritation, acne, mood changes, and initial disease flare. Bone density loss with prolonged use (>6 months).
