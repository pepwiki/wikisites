## Structure

Teduglutide (Gattex) is a recombinant analog of human GLP-2 with a single amino acid substitution: glycine replaces alanine at position 2. This modification confers resistance to DPP-4 cleavage, extending the half-life from 7 minutes (native GLP-2) to approximately 2 hours.

## Mechanism of Action

Like native GLP-2, teduglutide activates GLP-2 receptors on intestinal subepithelial myofibroblasts, stimulating mucosal growth, enhancing villus height and crypt depth, improving barrier function, and increasing mesenteric blood flow. The extended half-life provides sustained intestinal trophic effects.

## Clinical Applications

- **Short bowel syndrome**: Adults dependent on parenteral nutrition (Gattex)
- **Intestinal failure**: Reducing PN volume and frequency
- **STEP trials**: 63% achieved 20-50% PN volume reduction
- **Pediatric SBS**: Under investigation

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 2 hours (vs 7 min for native GLP-2)
- **Tmax**: 3-5 hours (SC)
- **Bioavailability**: 88% (SC)
- **Metabolism**: Proteolytic degradation
- **Dosing**: 0.05 mg/kg SC once daily

## Safety and Side Effects

Abdominal pain (30%), nausea (22%), injection site reactions (13%), headache, and upper respiratory infections. Intestinal polyp monitoring recommended (colonoscopy at baseline and 1 year). Theoretical risk of colorectal neoplasia.
