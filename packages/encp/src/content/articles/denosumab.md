## Structure

Denosumab (Prolia, Xgeva) is a fully human IgG2 monoclonal antibody targeting RANKL (receptor activator of nuclear factor-kappa B ligand). The antibody contains two heavy chains (451 amino acids each) and two light chains (219 amino acids each) with characteristic IgG2 disulfide bonding.

## Mechanism of Action

Denosumab binds and neutralizes RANKL, preventing its interaction with RANK on osteoclast precursors and mature osteoclasts. This blocks osteoclast differentiation, function, and survival, reducing bone resorption. RANKL is the essential cytokine for osteoclast biology, making denosumab a potent anti-resorptive agent.

## Clinical Applications

- **Postmenopausal osteoporosis**: SC every 6 months (Prolia)
- **Bone metastases**: SC every 4 weeks (Xgeva)
- **Giant cell tumor of bone**: Unresectable or surgically morbid disease
- **Male osteoporosis**: After failure of other therapies
- **Glucocorticoid-induced osteoporosis**: High-risk patients

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 25.4 days
- **Tmax**: 10 days (SC)
- **Bioavailability**: 61%
- **Metabolism**: Proteolytic degradation
- **Route**: SC (60 mg every 6 months for osteoporosis, 120 mg every 4 weeks for cancer)

## Safety and Side Effects

Hypocalcemia (important in renal impairment), osteonecrosis of the jaw (ONJ, 1-2% in cancer), atypical femoral fractures, serious infections, dermatologic reactions, and rebound vertebral fractures upon discontinuation.
