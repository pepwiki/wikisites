## Structure

Degarelix is a synthetic linear decapeptide GnRH antagonist with multiple unnatural amino acids including D-amino acids, N-methylated residues, and a C-terminal amide. The peptide contains a urea bond and several modifications that prevent enzymatic degradation and enhance receptor binding affinity.

## Mechanism of Action

Degarelix competitively blocks GnRH receptors on pituitary gonadotrophs, immediately suppressing LH and FSH release. This causes rapid testosterone reduction to castrate levels within 1-3 days, without the initial testosterone flare characteristic of GnRH agonists. Sustained suppression requires monthly injections.

## Clinical Applications

- **Advanced prostate cancer**: First-line androgen deprivation therapy
- **Biochemical recurrence**: Rising PSA after primary treatment
- **Neoadjuvant therapy**: Before radiation or surgery
- **Palliative therapy**: Metastatic prostate cancer

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 53 days (after steady state)
- **Onset**: Within 1 day
- **Duration**: 28 days per injection
- **Bioavailability**: 40-50% (subcutaneous)
- **Route**: Subcutaneous (abdomen)

## Safety and Side Effects

Injection site reactions (pain, erythema, nodules - 28%), hot flashes (26%), weight gain, fatigue, elevated LFTs, and QT prolongation. Initial testosterone flare does not occur, reducing risk of spinal cord compression.
