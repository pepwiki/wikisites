## Chemical Identity

| Property          | Value                                    |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Molecular Weight  | ~160 kDa (ADC)                           |
| Peptide Payload   | SN-38 (irinotecan metabolite)            |
| Linker            | CL2A (acid-cleavable)                    |
| DAR               | 7.6                                      |
| Target            | Trop-2                                   |

## Structure

Sacituzumab govitecan (Trodelvy) is a humanized anti-Trop-2 IgG1 antibody conjugated to SN-38 via a pH-sensitive CL2A linker. The high DAR (~7.6) delivers significant cytotoxic payload to Trop-2-expressing tumors. The linker is cleavable in acidic tumor microenvironment and lysosomes.

## Mechanism of Action

The antibody binds Trop-2, a transmembrane glycoprotein overexpressed on many epithelial cancers. After internalization and lysosomal processing, SN-38 is released, inhibiting topoisomerase I and causing DNA strand breaks during replication. The hydrolyzable linker also allows local bystander killing.

## Clinical Applications

- **Triple-negative breast cancer**: After two or more prior therapies (Trodelvy)
- **HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer**: After endocrine therapy
- **Urothelial carcinoma**: After platinum and PD-1/PD-L1 therapy
- **Trop-2-expressing solid tumors**: Under investigation

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: ~23 hours (total antibody)
- **Cmax (SN-38)**: ~167 ng/mL
- **Steady state**: Cycle 1
- **Metabolism**: SN-38 metabolized by UGT1A1
- **Route**: IV infusion days 1, 8 of 21-day cycle (10 mg/kg)

## Safety and Side Effects

Neutropenia (63%), diarrhea (59%), nausea (66%), fatigue (51%), alopecia (46%), anemia, and febrile neutropenia. UGT1A1*28 homozygotes at higher risk for toxicity. Premedication required.
