## Structure

Triptorelin is a synthetic decapeptide GnRH agonist with D-tryptophan at position 6 of the native GnRH sequence. This substitution increases receptor binding affinity and resistance to enzymatic degradation. The molecule is available as free peptide (Trelstar) or pamoate salt depot formulations.

## Mechanism of Action

Initial GnRH receptor stimulation causes transient gonadotropin and sex hormone elevation. Continuous exposure leads to receptor downregulation and desensitization, suppressing LH/FSH and achieving castrate hormone levels within 2-4 weeks. Effect is maintained with continued treatment.

## Clinical Applications

- **Prostate cancer**: Androgen deprivation therapy (Trelstar, Decapeptyl)
- **Endometriosis**: Pain management and lesion reduction
- **Precocious puberty**: Central (gonadotropin-dependent) precocious puberty
- **Uterine fibroids**: Pre-surgical volume reduction
- **IVF**: Pituitary desensitization protocols
- **Breast cancer**: Premenopausal HR+ disease

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 3 hours (free peptide)
- **Duration**: 1 month, 3 months, or 6 months (depot)
- **Bioavailability**: 80% (IM depot)
- **Metabolism**: Proteolytic cleavage
- **Route**: IM depot, SC

## Safety and Side Effects

Hot flashes, erectile dysfunction, decreased libido, osteoporosis, fatigue, initial disease flare, weight gain, and mood changes. Injection site reactions with depot formulations.
