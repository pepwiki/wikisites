## Structure

Polymyxin B is a mixture of closely related cyclic lipopeptides (B1 and B2) consisting of a heptapeptide ring with a tripeptide side chain acylated with a fatty acid at the N-terminus. The molecule contains L-alpha,gamma-diaminobutyric acid (Dab) residues providing positive charges, and the fatty acid tail is essential for membrane interaction.

## Mechanism of Action

Polymyxin B binds to lipid A of lipopolysaccharide (LPS) in the gram-negative outer membrane via electrostatic interaction between cationic Dab residues and anionic phosphate groups. The fatty acid tail displaces divalent cations (Mg2+, Ca2+) and inserts into the outer membrane, causing destabilization, increased permeability, and ultimately bacterial cell death.

## Clinical Applications

- **MDR gram-negative infections**: Last-line therapy for Pseudomonas, Acinetobacter, Klebsiella
- **Meningitis**: Intrathecal/intraventricular for gram-negative meningitis
- **Ophthalmic infections**: Topical for gram-negative conjunctivitis
- **Urinary tract infections**: Oral for resistant organisms

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: 6 hours
- **Protein binding**: 58%
- **Elimination**: Renal (primarily)
- **Route**: IV, intrathecal, topical, ophthalmic
- **Dose adjustment**: Required in renal impairment

## Safety and Side Effects

Nephrotoxicity (dose-limiting, 20-60% incidence), neurotoxicity (paresthesias, dizziness, neuromuscular blockade), and hypersensitivity reactions. Narrow therapeutic index requires careful monitoring.
