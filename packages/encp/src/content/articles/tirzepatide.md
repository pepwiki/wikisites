## Structure

Tirzepatide is a 39-amino acid synthetic peptide based on the GIP sequence with specific modifications including a C20 fatty diacid moiety at position 20 for albumin binding, providing extended half-life. The peptide incorporates Aib (alpha-aminoisobutyric acid) substitutions to resist DPP-4 degradation.

## Mechanism of Action

Tirzepatide activates both GIP and GLP-1 receptors with balanced potency, enhancing insulin secretion in a glucose-dependent manner, suppressing glucagon, slowing gastric emptying, and reducing appetite through central pathways. The dual incretin mechanism produces additive effects on metabolic parameters.

## Clinical Applications

FDA-approved as Mounjaro for type 2 diabetes (2022) and Zepbound for obesity (2023). In SURPASS trials, tirzepatide 15 mg reduced HbA1c by 2.4-2.6% and achieved 15-22% weight loss. The SURMOUNT-1 trial demonstrated mean weight loss of 22.5% at the highest dose.

## Pharmacokinetics

- **Half-life**: ~5 days, supporting once-weekly dosing
- **Tmax**: 24-48 hours
- **Bioavailability**: 80% after subcutaneous injection
- **Elimination**: Proteolytic cleavage and renal excretion

## Safety and Side Effects

Common adverse effects include nausea (12-18%), diarrhea (12-17%), decreased appetite (5-11%), and vomiting (5-9%). GI effects are dose-dependent and typically transient. Contraindicated in patients with personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN2.
