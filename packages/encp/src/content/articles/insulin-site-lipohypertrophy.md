## Overview

Lipohypertrophy is the most common injection-related complication of insulin therapy, occurring in 30-60% of patients due to local anabolic effects on subcutaneous adipose tissue.

## Clinical Consequences

- Erratic insulin absorption (25-50% variability)
- Unexplained glycemic variability
- Increased insulin requirements
- Poor glycemic control despite appropriate dosing

## Prevention and Management

- Systematic site rotation at every injection
- Regular palpation of injection sites
- Education on proper injection technique
