Thymosin beta-4 (TB-500) and GHK-Cu are both tissue-repair peptides with distinct but complementary mechanisms. TB-500 acts through actin polymerization and cell migration, while GHK-Cu functions through copper-dependent enzyme activation and extracellular matrix remodeling. Their combination offers synergistic tissue repair.

## Structural and Chemical Differences

### Thymosin Beta-4 (TB-500)

- **Sequence**: Ac-SDKPDMAELEKFDKSKLKKTETQEKNPLPSKETIEQEKQAGES (43 amino acids)
- **Modifications**: N-terminal acetylation
- **MW**: 4,921 Da
- **Charge at pH 7.4**: −4
- **Half-life**: 2–4 hours (serum)
- **Stability**: Susceptible to proteolysis

### GHK-Cu

- **Sequence**: Gly-His-Lys (tripeptide) + Cu²⁺
- **Modifications**: Copper(II) chelation
- **MW**: 340 Da (peptide) + 63.5 Da (Cu) = 403 Da
- **Charge at pH 7.4**: +1 (peptide) + 2 (Cu) = +3
- **Half-life**: 1–2 hours (serum)
- **Stability**: Copper binding stabilizes peptide

## Mechanistic Differences

### Thymosin Beta-4

**Primary mechanisms:**
- **Actin sequestration**: Binds G-actin, preventing polymerization
- **Cell migration**: Promotes endothelial and keratinocyte migration
- **Angiogenesis**: VEGF upregulation, new vessel formation
- **Anti-apoptosis**: Reduces cell death in damaged tissues
- **Inflammation modulation**: Reduces inflammatory cytokines

**Molecular details:**
- Binds G-actin in 1:1 ratio
- Maintains actin monomer pool for cell motility
- Upregulates VEGF, MMP-2, MMP-9
- Activates PI3K/Akt survival pathway

### GHK-Cu

**Primary mechanisms:**
- **Copper delivery**: Provides bioavailable copper to tissues
- **Enzyme activation**: Activates Cu/Zn-SOD, lysyl oxidase, tyrosinase
- **Collagen remodeling**: Stimulates collagen synthesis and degradation
- **Anti-inflammatory**: Reduces TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1β
- **Antioxidant**: Activates superoxide dismutase

**Molecular details:**
- Delivers copper to intracellular compartments
- Activates copper-dependent enzymes
- Stimulates collagen I, III, and elastin synthesis
- Promotes organized extracellular matrix formation

## Dosing Protocols

### Thymosin Beta-4

- **Standard dose**: 2.5–5 mg (1–2× weekly)
- **Loading dose**: 5 mg × 4 weeks
- **Maintenance**: 2.5 mg weekly
- **Administration**: Subcutaneous injection
- **Timing**: Evening (mimics physiological rhythm)

### GHK-Cu

- **Standard dose**: 1–5 mg (1–2× weekly)
- **Loading dose**: 5 mg × 4 weeks
- **Maintenance**: 1–2 mg weekly
- **Administration**: Subcutaneous injection or topical
- **Timing**: Morning (copper utilization)

### Combination Protocol

- **Phase 1 (4 weeks)**: TB-500 5 mg + GHK-Cu 5 mg weekly
- **Phase 2 (4 weeks)**: TB-500 2.5 mg + GHK-Cu 2.5 mg weekly
- **Maintenance**: TB-500 2.5 mg + GHK-Cu 1 mg weekly
- **Administration**: Separate injection sites, 30 minutes apart

## Safety Profile

### Thymosin Beta-4

**Common adverse events (>5%):**
- Injection site reactions (10–15%)
- Headache (5–10%)
- Fatigue (5–10%)
- Nausea (3–5%)
- Dizziness (3–5%)

**Serious adverse events (rare):**
- Allergic reactions
- Immune suppression (high doses)
- Potential tumor promotion (controversial)

### GHK-Cu

**Common adverse events (>5%):**
- Injection site reactions (5–10%)
- Skin irritation (topical) (5–10%)
- Headache (3–5%)
- Nausea (2–4%)
- Dizziness (2–4%)

**Serious adverse events (rare):**
- Copper toxicity (high doses)
- Allergic reactions
- Metal taste (injection)

### Combination Safety

- **Additive effects**: No significant potentiation
- **Monitoring**: Copper levels (if high-dose GHK-Cu)
- **Contraindications**: Wilson's disease (copper metabolism disorder)
