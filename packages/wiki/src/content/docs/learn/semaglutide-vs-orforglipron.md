Semaglutide and orforglipron represent two distinct pharmacological strategies for GLP-1 receptor agonism. Semaglutide is a peptidic GLP-1 analog available in injectable and oral formulations, while orforglipron is a fully synthetic, non-peptide, orally administered small molecule that allosterically activates the GLP-1 receptor.

## Molecular Architecture

### Semaglutide

Semaglutide is a 31-amino acid GLP-1 analog with three key modifications:

1. **Aib8 substitution**: α-aminoisobutyric acid at position 8 replaces Ala, conferring DPP-4 resistance and extending plasma half-life.
2. **Arg34Gly substitution**: prevents C-terminal amidation.
3. **Lys26 acylation with a C-18 fatty diacid via a linker**: enables albumin binding, extending the half-life to approximately 165 hours (once-weekly) for the injectable formulation.

The oral formulation (Rybelsus) includes the absorption enhancer SNAC (sodium N-(8-[2-hydroxybenzoyl]amino)caprylate), which facilitates gastric absorption by promoting transcellular transport across gastric epithelium.

### Orforglipron

Orforglipron (LY3502970) is a fully synthetic, non-peptide, orally bioavailable small molecule that acts as an allosteric agonist at the GLP-1 receptor. Unlike semaglutide, it:

- Is not a peptide — it is a small molecule with a defined chemical structure
- Does not require peptide engineering for metabolic stability
- Achieves oral bioavailability through inherent physicochemical properties
- Acts at a distinct allosteric binding site on the GLP-1 receptor

This non-peptidic mechanism eliminates the need for injection, refrigeration, or the absorption enhancers required by oral semaglutide.

## Pharmacokinetics

### Absorption and Distribution

Semaglutide's injectable formulation achieves near-complete bioavailability, with peak plasma concentrations at approximately 1–3 days post-injection. The albumin-bound fraction exceeds 99%, and the volume of distribution is approximately 12.5 L.

Oral semaglutide achieves only ~1% bioavailability, with peak concentrations at approximately 1 hour when taken under fasting conditions with the SNAC absorption enhancer. Despite low bioavailability, the high dose (3–14 mg daily vs 0.25–1 mg weekly injectable) compensates to achieve therapeutic plasma levels.

Orforglipron achieves approximately 50% oral bioavailability without absorption enhancers, with peak plasma concentrations at approximately 2–4 hours. Its non-peptide nature eliminates the proteolytic degradation that limits oral peptide bioavailability.

## Clinical Efficacy

### Weight Reduction

| Trial | Agent | Dose | Weight Loss |
|-------|-------|------|-------------|
| STEP 1 | Semaglutide SC | 2.4 mg weekly | ~14.9% at 68 wks |
| OASIS 1 | Semaglutide SC | 50 mg weekly | ~19.6% at 72 wks |
| OASIS 2 | Semaglutide Oral | 50 mg daily | ~15.1% at 72 wks |
| ATTAIN-1 | Orforglipron | 36 mg daily | ~12.4% at 72 wks |
| ATTAIN-2 | Orforglipron | 36 mg daily | ~10.5% at 72 wks |

Semaglutide demonstrates superior weight reduction compared to orforglipron in head-to-head efficacy data. However, orforglipron's oral administration and lack of food timing requirements offer practical advantages.

### Cardiovascular Outcomes

Semaglutide has demonstrated cardiovascular benefit in the SUSTAIN-6 and SELECT trials, with significant reductions in MACE. Orforglipron's cardiovascular outcomes data are pending from ongoing outcomes trials.

GI side effects are dose-dependent for both agents and typically diminish over 4–8 weeks. Orforglipron may have a modestly more favorable GI tolerability profile, though direct head-to-head data are limited.

## Key Takeaways

Semaglutide remains the benchmark GLP-1 receptor agonist for efficacy, with the highest weight reduction and glycemic control among approved agents. Orforglipron represents a paradigm shift as the first non-peptide oral GLP-1 agonist, eliminating the complex absorption requirements of oral semaglutide and offering room-temperature stability. While orforglipron's efficacy appears modestly lower than semaglutide's, its practical advantages — no injection, no food timing, no refrigeration — may improve real-world adherence and access.
