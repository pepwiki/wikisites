GHK-Cu and Copper Peptide-1 refer to the same molecular entity but represent different nomenclature systems. GHK-Cu is the biochemical designation, while Copper Peptide-1 is a cosmetic industry term. Understanding the naming conventions prevents confusion when evaluating products, ingredients, and research literature.

## Chemical Identity

### GHK-Cu: The Biochemical Designation

GHK-Cu is the standard biochemical name for the copper(II) complex of glycyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine:

- **Sequence**: Gly-His-Lys (tripeptide)
- **Metal**: Cu²⁺ (copper(II))
- **Stoichiometry**: 1:1 peptide:metal complex
- **Molecular formula**: C₁₄H₂₄CuN₆O₄
- **Molecular weight**: 340.9 Da
- **CAS number**: 49557-75-7
- **pKa**: 8.5 (imidazole nitrogen)

The name GHK is derived from the single-letter codes of its amino acids: **G**lycine-**H**istidine-**K** lysine.

### Copper Peptide-1: The Cosmetic Industry Term

Copper Peptide-1 is an INCI (International Nomenclature of Cosmetic Ingredients) designation used in cosmetic formulations:

- **INCI name**: Copper Tripeptide-1
- **Trade name**: GHK-Cu, Copper Peptide
- **Usage**: Primarily in cosmetic and skincare products
- **Regulation**: Cosmeceutical ingredient (not a drug)

The "1" in Copper Peptide-1 does not indicate a specific isomer or variant — it is simply an INCI numbering convention.

## Molecular Structure

### Peptide Component

GHK is a naturally occurring tripeptide found in human plasma, saliva, and urine:

- **N-terminal**: Glycine (smallest amino acid, flexible backbone)
- **Middle**: Histidine (imidazole ring, metal coordination)
- **C-terminal**: Lysine (ε-amino group, positive charge)

### Copper Coordination

The Cu²⁺ ion coordinates through:
1. **Histidine Nε2** (imidazole nitrogen)
2. **Histidine Nα** (amino nitrogen)
3. **Glycine carbonyl oxygen** (backbone)
4. **Two water molecules** (axial positions)

This square pyramidal coordination geometry produces a characteristic blue color (λmax ~530 nm).

## Skin Benefits

### Collagen and Elastin

- **Collagen I**: 1.5–2× increase in synthesis
- **Collagen III**: 1.5× increase in synthesis
- **Elastin**: 2–3× increase in synthesis
- **Mechanism**: TGF-β1 and TGF-β2 upregulation

### Wound Healing

- **Angiogenesis**: Promotes new blood vessel formation
- **Re-epithelialization**: Accelerates keratinocyte migration
- **Scarring**: Reduces hypertrophic scar formation
- **Clinical evidence**: Effective in chronic wounds, burns, surgical wounds

### Anti-Aging

- **Wrinkle reduction**: 20–30% reduction in fine lines over 8–12 weeks
- **Firmness**: Improved skin elasticity
- **Pigmentation**: Mild brightening effect
- **Pore size**: Reduced appearance

## Stability Considerations

### GHK-Cu Stability Challenges

- **Copper dissociation**: pH-dependent (stable below pH 6.0)
- **Oxidation**: Histidine susceptible to oxidation
- **Light sensitivity**: Copper complex absorbs UV/visible light
- **Shelf life**: 6–12 months (refrigerated)

### Formulation Strategies

- **pH optimization**: Maintain pH 4.0–5.5
- **Chelators**: EDTA, citric acid to stabilize copper
- **Antioxidants**: BHT, vitamin E to prevent oxidation
- **Packaging**: Opaque, airless containers
- **Lyophilization**: For long-term storage
