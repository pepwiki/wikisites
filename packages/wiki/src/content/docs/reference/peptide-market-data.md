## Market Drivers

1. **Obesity epidemic**: Growing global prevalence driving demand for GLP-1 agonists
2. **Diabetes burden**: Expanding T2D patient population
3. **Indication expansion**: GLP-1 agonists for MASH, CKD, heart failure, sleep apnea
4. **Oral formulations**: Improved patient convenience and adherence
5. **Manufacturing scale**: SPPS improvements reducing cost of goods
6. **Biosimilar competition**: Post-patent expiration price competition

## Market Challenges

1. **Manufacturing capacity**: Demand exceeding supply (GLP-1 shortage)
2. **Pricing pressure**: Payer pushback on high drug costs
3. **Biosimilar threat**: Post-patent erosion of blockbuster revenues
4. **Safety signals**: Long-term cardiovascular and safety data requirements
5. **Competition**: Multiple companies developing GLP-1 agonists

## Key Takeaways

The peptide therapeutics market is experiencing unprecedented growth driven by GLP-1 receptor agonists for diabetes and obesity. Novo Nordisk and Eli Lilly dominate with >80% market share. The market is projected to reach $200–250B by 2028, with oral peptides and next-generation multi-agonists driving expansion. Manufacturing capacity, pricing, and biosimilar competition are the key challenges for sustaining growth.
