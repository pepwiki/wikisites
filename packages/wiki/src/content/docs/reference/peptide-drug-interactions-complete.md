# Complete Peptide Drug Interactions Reference

A comprehensive, structured database of 100+ known interactions between peptides and conventional drugs, organized by peptide class.

## Severity Classification

| Severity | Meaning | Action Required |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| **Major** | Avoid combination; significant risk of harm | Contraindicated or switch |
| **Moderate** | Use with caution; may require dose adjustment | Monitor, adjust, or consider alternatives |
| **Minor** | Low clinical significance; monitor | No dose change typically needed |

## Mechanism Classification

| Mechanism | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **PK — Absorption** | Altered GI absorption (delayed, enhanced, reduced) |
| **PK — Distribution** | Protein binding displacement, altered Vd |
| **PK — Metabolism** | CYP450 induction/inhibition, peptidase effects |
| **PK — Excretion** | Renal/hepatic clearance alterations |
| **PD — Synergistic** | Additive or enhanced pharmacological effect |
| **PD — Antagonistic** | Opposing pharmacological effects |
| **PD — Additive toxicity** | Overlapping adverse effects |
| **Formulation** | Physical/chemical incompatibility |

## General Principles

1. **Always monitor glucose** when combining any peptide with insulin or oral hypoglycemics
2. **Avoid ergot alkaloids** with oxytocin (hypertensive crisis)
3. **Separate dosing** for chelating agents (tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones) with GHK-Cu
4. **Monitor INR** when combining GLP-1 RAs with warfarin
5. **Monitor electrolytes** with desmopressin + lithium (water intoxication risk)
6. **Avoid triple antiplatelet therapy** unless specifically indicated
7. **Adjust insulin doses** by 20–25% when adding GLP-1 RAs or GH secretagogues
8. **Monitor IGF-1** when combining GH secretagogues
9. **Separate oral medications** by 1–4 hours from GLP-1 RAs (absorption delay)
10. **Consult pharmacist** for any uncertain interaction
