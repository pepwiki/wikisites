# Complete Peptide Reconstitution Reference

Comprehensive reconstitution protocols for all FDA-approved and major investigational peptides.

## Reconstitution Principles

### Solvent Selection

| Solvent | Use Case | Volume | Notes |
|---------|----------|--------|-------|
| Bacteriostatic water (0.9% BA) | Most peptides | Per protocol | Contains preservative; multi-dose |
| Sterile water for injection | Sensitive peptides | Per protocol | Single-use; no preservative |
| Normal saline (0.9% NaCl) | IM/SC injection | Per protocol | Isotonic; compatible with most peptides |
| 0.9% BA in NS | Peptides requiring BA | Per protocol | Contains preservative |

### Reconstitution Volume Calculations

**Standard concentration**: 1 mg/mL (for most research peptides)

| Peptide Amount | Desired Concentration | Add Solvent | Result |
|---------------|----------------------|-------------|--------|
| 1 mg | 1 mg/mL | 1 mL | 1 mg/mL |
| 2 mg | 1 mg/mL | 2 mL | 1 mg/mL |
| 5 mg | 2.5 mg/mL | 2 mL | 2.5 mg/mL |
| 10 mg | 5 mg/mL | 2 mL | 5 mg/mL |
| 10 mg | 2.5 mg/mL | 4 mL | 2.5 mg/mL |
| 10 mg | 1 mg/mL | 10 mL | 1 mg/mL |

**Formula**: Volume (mL) = Amount (mg) ÷ Desired concentration (mg/mL)

## Research Peptide Reconstitution

### General Protocol (Most Lyophilized Peptides)

**Step 1: Gather supplies**
- Lyophilized peptide vial
- Bacteriostatic water or sterile saline
- Bacteriostatic water vial
- 3 mL or 5 mL syringe
- 18G drawing needle
- 27G or 29G injection needle
- Alcohol swabs
- Gloves

**Step 2: Clean tops**
- Wipe peptide vial top with alcohol swab
- Wipe bacteriostatic water vial top with alcohol swab
- Allow to dry (30 seconds)

**Step 3: Draw solvent**
- Attach 18G needle to syringe
- Draw required volume of bacteriostatic water
- Expel air bubbles

**Step 4: Inject solvent into peptide vial**
- Insert needle through vial center
- Slowly inject solvent along vial wall
- Do not spray directly onto powder (causes foaming)

**Step 5: Dissolve**
- Gently swirl vial until powder dissolves
- Do not shake (causes protein denaturation)
- Solution should be clear and colorless
- If cloudy or contains particles, do not use

**Step 6: Verify concentration**
- Confirm total volume matches calculation
- Verify no undissolved material

**Step 7: Label vial**
- Peptide name and amount
- Date and time of reconstitution
- Expiration date (usually 30 days)
- Concentration

**Step 8: Draw dose**
- Attach 27G or 29G injection needle
- Draw correct dose volume
- Expel air bubbles
- Administer

## Common Reconstitution Errors

| Error | Consequence | Prevention |
|-------|------------|-----------|
| Spraying solvent directly onto powder | Foaming, denaturation | Inject along vial wall |
| Shaking vial | Protein denaturation | Gently swirl only |
| Using wrong solvent | Instability, incompatibility | Check peptide compatibility |
| Incorrect volume | Wrong concentration | Calculate carefully |
| Air bubbles in syringe | Dosing error | Tap and expel bubbles |
| Using expired peptide | Reduced potency | Check dates |
| Contamination | Infection risk | Sterile technique |
| Insufficient wait time | Undissolved material | Wait for full dissolution |
| Re-using needles | Dull needle, pain | Fresh needle each time |
| Not labeling vial | Confusion, dosing error | Always label |

## Multi-Dose vs Single-Dose

| Feature | Multi-Dose | Single-Dose |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Preservative | Yes (BA) | No |
| Multi-use | Yes (28 days) | No (one-time) |
| Infection risk | Low (preserved) | Higher |
| Reconstitution | Not needed (liquid) | Required |
| Storage after opening | Room temp or refrigerated | Use immediately |

## Travel and Transport

| Scenario | Recommendation |
|----------|---------------|
| <8 hours travel | Insulated cooler with ice packs |
| 8–24 hours | Insulated cooler with frozen ice packs |
| Air travel | Carry-on luggage; original packaging |
| International | Check customs regulations; letter from prescriber |
| Power outage | Keep in cooler; discard if >24 hours unrefrigerated |
