# Peptide Reconstitution Guide

## Equipment Needed

- Lyophilized peptide vial
- Bacteriostatic water (0.9% benzyl alcohol)
- Sterile syringe (1-3 mL)
- Alcohol swabs
- Clean work surface

## Step-by-Step Protocol

1. **Calculate volume:** Volume (mL) = Mass (mg) / Desired concentration (mg/mL)
2. **Clean vial top** with alcohol swab
3. **Draw BAC water** into syringe
4. **Inject slowly** down the inside wall of the vial (NOT directly onto powder)
5. **Gentle swirling** (NEVER shake)
6. **Wait** for complete dissolution (5-15 minutes)
7. **Inspect** for clarity (no particles, no cloudiness)
8. **Store** at 2-8°C

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Fix |
|---------|------------|-----|
| Shaking vial | Protein aggregation | Gentle swirl only |
| Direct injection onto powder | Mechanical shear damage | Inject down wall |
| Using sterile water | No preservation | Use BAC water |
| Wrong concentration | Dosing error | Use calculator |
| Re-freezing reconstituted | Ice crystal damage | Single-use aliquots |
