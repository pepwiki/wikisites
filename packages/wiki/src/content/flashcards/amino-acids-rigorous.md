id: fc-proline-properties
front: "Why is proline unique among the 20 standard amino acids?"
back: "Proline (Pro, P) is an imino acid where the R-group cyclizes back to the nitrogen, forming a pyrrolidine ring. This constrains the backbone φ angle (~-60°), disrupting α-helices and β-sheets. It facilitates cis-trans isomerization of peptide bonds (X-Pro) and introduces kinks in protein structure. MW = 115.13 Da."
tags: ["proline", "imino-acid", "cyclic", "cis-trans-isomerization", "helix-breaker"]
