id: fc-peptide-merrifield-spps
front: "What was Merrifield's contribution to peptide science?"
back: "Robert Bruce Merrifield introduced solid-phase peptide synthesis (SPPS) in 1963, where peptides are built on an insoluble polymer support (typically Wang or Rink resin). This dramatically simplified purification (simple filtration/washing), enabled automation, and transformed peptide synthesis from a laborious process to a routine laboratory technique. He received the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1984."

front: "What discovery launched the GLP-1 receptor agonist class?"
back: "The discovery that exendin-4, a 39-amino acid peptide from the Gila monster (Heloderma suspectum) venom, was a potent and longer-acting GLP-1 receptor agonist led to exenatide (Byetta, approved 2005). This launched the GLP-1 RA class, now the fastest-growing peptide category with semaglutide and tirzepatide generating over $40 billion annually."

id: fc-peptide-fda-classification
front: "How does the FDA classify peptide drugs versus biologics?"
back: "Synthetic peptides under 40 amino acids are generally regulated as drugs under CDER (Center for Drug Evaluation and Research) via NDA/ANDA pathways. Larger peptides and recombinant proteins fall under CBER (Center for Biologics Evaluation and Research) via BLA pathways. The 2020 BPCIA amendments reclassified certain peptides as biologics, affecting regulatory strategy and exclusivity periods."

back: "1) Variational Autoencoders (VAEs): encode peptides into latent space, generate novel sequences by sampling. 2) Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs): generator creates sequences, discriminator evaluates realism. 3) Diffusion models: iteratively refine random sequences toward desired properties. 4) Autoregressive transformers: generate sequences token-by-token conditioned on properties. 5) Reinforcement learning: optimize sequences via reward functions for binding, stability, selectivity."

back: "1) Limited training data for complex properties (bioavailability, immunogenicity). 2) Accurately predicting proteolytic stability across proteases. 3) Membrane permeability prediction remains unreliable. 4) Oral bioavailability is extremely hard to predict. 5) Off-target effects and selectivity prediction. 6) Experimental validation gap: AI designs often fail wet-lab synthesis or assays. 7) Multi-objective optimization (potency, stability, selectivity simultaneously)."
