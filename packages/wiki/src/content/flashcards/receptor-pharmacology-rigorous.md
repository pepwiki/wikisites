# Peptide Receptor Pharmacology

## Card 1: GLP-1R (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1 Receptor)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** GLP-1R (Class B1 GPCR, family 2)

**Binding Affinity:**
- Endogenous ligands: GLP-1(7-36)amide KD ≈ 0.3-1 nM; GLP-1(7-37) KD ≈ 1-3 nM
- Exendin-4 KD ≈ 0.1-0.5 nM (full agonist)
- Semaglutide KD ≈ 0.1 nM

**Signal Transduction:**
- Primary: Gαs → adenylyl cyclase activation → ↑cAMP → PKA activation
- Secondary: Gαq → PLCβ → IP3/DAG → Ca²⁺ mobilization
- β-arrestin recruitment → receptor internalization
- Epac2 (cAMP-regulated guanine nucleotide exchange factor)

**Tissue Distribution:**
- Pancreatic β-cells (primary site), GI tract (L-cells), brain (hypothalamus, brainstem, hippocampus), heart, kidney, lung, immune cells

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Type 2 diabetes: Exenatide, liraglutide, semaglutide, dulaglutide, tirzepatide (dual GLP-1R/GIPR)
- Obesity: Semaglutide (2.4 mg), liraglutide (3 mg)
- Cardiovascular benefit: LEADER, SUSTAIN-6, SELECT trials
- Side effects: Nausea, vomiting, pancreatitis risk, medullary thyroid carcinoma (rodent signal)
- Nomenclature note: Previously called "GLP1 receptor" in older literature

## Card 2: GIPR (Glucose-Dependent Insulinotropic Polypeptide Receptor)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** GIPR (Class B1 GPCR, family 2)

**Binding Affinity:**
- GIP(1-42) KD ≈ 1-5 nM
- GIP(3-42) KD ≈ 10-50 nM (inactive metabolite)
- Tirzepatide GIPR KD ≈ 0.05 nM

**Signal Transduction:**
- Primary: Gαs → adenylyl cyclase → ↑cAMP → PKA/Epac2
- Secondary: Gαq → PLCβ → IP3/DAG → intracellular Ca²⁺
- β-arrestin recruitment with slower kinetics than GLP-1R
- MAPK/ERK pathway activation

**Tissue Distribution:**
- Pancreatic β-cells, adipose tissue (white and brown), bone (osteoblasts/osteoclasts), brain (hippocampus, hypothalamus), heart, GI tract, adrenal cortex

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Tirzepatide: Dual GLP-1R/GIPR agonist approved for T2DM and obesity (SURMOUNT, SURPASS trials)
- GIPR antagonist hypothesis: Anti-GIPR antibodies (AMG 133) show weight loss
- GIPR polymorphisms: rs1800437 (E354Q) associated with obesity and insulin resistance
- Bone metabolism: GIP promotes bone formation; potential osteoporosis target
- "Incretin effect" contribution: GIP accounts for ~50% of incretin effect

## Card 3: GLP-1R/GIPR Dual Agonism

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** GLP-1R + GIPR (Class B1 GPCRs, family 2)

**Binding Affinity (Tirzepatide):**
- GLP-1R Ki ≈ 0.3 nM (partial agonist, ~50% efficacy vs GLP-1)
- GIPR Ki ≈ 0.05 nM (full agonist)
- Selectivity: ~5-fold preference for GIPR over GLP-1R

**Signal Transduction:**
- Combined cAMP generation from both receptors
- Enhanced β-cell insulin secretion synergy
- Additive/synergistic effects on appetite suppression
- Potential receptor heterodimerization (under investigation)

**Tissue Distribution:**
- Overlap: Pancreatic β-cells, brain, GI tract
- GIPR-specific: Adipose tissue, bone
- GLP-1R-specific: Heart, kidney

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Tirzepatide (Mounjaro®): 5/10/15 mg SC weekly
- SURPASS-2: Superior HbA1c reduction vs semaglutide 1 mg (-2.09% vs -1.46%)
- SURMOUNT-1: Mean weight loss -15% (5 mg), -19.5% (10 mg), -20.9% (15 mg)
- Mechanism hypothesis: GIP agonism may counteract GLP-1R desensitization
- Adipose tissue effects: GIPR activation may improve lipid metabolism
- Retatrutide: Triple agonist (GLP-1R/GIPR/GCGR) in phase 3

## Card 4: Insulin Receptor (IR)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** Insulin receptor (RTK family, EC 2.7.10.1)

**Binding Affinity:**
- Insulin KD ≈ 0.1-1 nM (isoform-dependent)
- IR-A (fetal): Higher affinity for insulin, binds IGF-2
- IR-B (adult): Higher affinity for IGF-1
- Proinsulin: ~5-10% potency of mature insulin

**Signal Transduction:**
- Receptor autophosphorylation (Tyr1158, Tyr1162, Tyr1163)
- IRS-1/2 recruitment and phosphorylation
- PI3K/Akt pathway → GLUT4 translocation, glycogen synthesis
- Ras/MAPK pathway → gene expression, cell growth
- Grb2/SOS → Ras activation
- SHP-2 phosphatase recruitment

**Tissue Distribution:**
- Ubiquitous: Liver, skeletal muscle, adipose tissue (high expression)
- Brain (hippocampus, hypothalamus), kidney, heart, pancreatic β-cells
- Isoform ratio tissue-specific: IR-A in fetal/cancer tissues, IR-B in liver/muscle

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Diabetes: Type 1 (autoimmune destruction), Type 2 (insulin resistance)
- Insulin therapy: Lispro, aspart, glargine, degludec
- Insulin resistance: Serine phosphorylation of IRS-1 (inhibitory)
- Cancer risk: Hyperinsulinemia → IR-A activation → mitogenic signaling
- Donohue syndrome (leprechaunism): Homozygous IR mutations
- Rabson-Mendenhall syndrome: Severe insulin resistance

## Card 5: IGF-1R (Insulin-Like Growth Factor-1 Receptor)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** IGF1R (RTK family, EC 2.7.10.1)

**Binding Affinity:**
- IGF-1 KD ≈ 0.2-1 nM
- IGF-2 KD ≈ 2-5 nM (lower affinity)
- Insulin KD ≈ 100-1000 nM (very low affinity)
- IGF-1R/IR hybrid receptors: Variable affinities

**Signal Transduction:**
- Receptor autophosphorylation (Tyr1131, Tyr1135, Tyr1136)
- IRS-1/2 → PI3K/Akt → survival, protein synthesis
- Shc → Ras/MAPK → proliferation
- 14-3-3 protein binding
- mTORC1 activation via Akt/TSC1/2

**Tissue Distribution:**
- Ubiquitous: High expression in liver, muscle, bone (chondrocytes), brain
- Fetal tissues: High expression during development
- Cancer cells: Often overexpressed

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Laron syndrome: GH receptor mutation → low IGF-1 → growth failure
- Cancer: IGF-1R overexpression in breast, prostate, lung cancers
- Anti-IGF-1R therapies: Figitumumab, ganitumab (limited clinical success)
- IGF-1R inhibitors: Linsitinib (OSI-906), BMS-754807
- Growth disorders: IGF-1 therapy (mecasermin) for severe IGF-1 deficiency
- Acromegaly: Elevated GH/IGF-1 axis

## Card 6: OXTR (Oxytocin Receptor)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** OXTR (Class A GPCR, rhodopsin family)

**Binding Affinity:**
- Oxytocin Ki ≈ 1-5 nM
- Vasopressin Ki ≈ 100-1000 nM (low affinity)
- Atosiban Ki ≈ 10-50 nM (antagonist)
- Carbetocin Ki ≈ 5-10 nM (agonist)

**Signal Transduction:**
- Primary: Gαq/11 → PLCβ → IP3/DAG → Ca²⁺ mobilization
- Gαi/o → inhibition of adenylyl cyclase
- MAPK/ERK pathway
- β-arrestin recruitment → receptor desensitization
- RhoA/ROCK pathway in myometrium

**Tissue Distribution:**
- Uterus (myometrium, decidua), mammary gland (myoepithelial cells)
- Brain: Hypothalamus (PVN, SON), amygdala, nucleus accumbens, hippocampus
- Heart, kidney, pancreas, endothelium, immune cells

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Labor induction: Synthetic oxytocin (Pitocin®)
- Postpartum hemorrhage: Oxytocin first-line treatment
- Preterm labor: Atosiban (OXTR antagonist, Europe)
- Autism spectrum disorder: Oxytocin intranasal trials (mixed results)
- Social behavior: Pair bonding, trust, empathy modulation
- Carbetocin: Long-acting OXTR agonist for PPH prevention (CHAMPION trial)
- Psychiatric disorders: Oxytocin trials in schizophrenia, PTSD, depression

## Card 7: AVPR1A (Vasopressin V1a Receptor)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** AVPR1A (Class A GPCR, rhodopsin family)

**Binding Affinity:**
- Arginine vasopressin (AVP) Ki ≈ 1-2 nM
- Oxytocin Ki ≈ 100-500 nM
- [Phe²,Ile³,Orn⁸]vasopressin Ki ≈ 5-10 nM (antagonist)
- Relcovaptan Ki ≈ 1-2 nM (selective antagonist)

**Signal Transduction:**
- Primary: Gαq/11 → PLCβ → IP3/DAG → intracellular Ca²⁺ ↑
- Gα12/13 → RhoA/ROCK → cytoskeletal rearrangement
- MAPK pathway activation (ERK1/2)
- Prostaglandin synthesis (COX-2 induction)

**Tissue Distribution:**
- Vascular smooth muscle (primary site), liver, brain (septum, hippocampus, cortex)
- Platelets, uterus, testes (Sertoli cells), adrenal cortex
- Kidney (medullary interstitial cells)

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Vasodilatory shock: Vasopressin adjunctive therapy
- Hepatorenal syndrome: Terlipressin (V1a agonist)
- Raynaud's disease: V1a antagonists investigated
- Anxiety/social behavior: AVP-AVPR1A axis in social cognition
- SRX246: V1a antagonist in clinical trials for aggression in ALS
- Bleeding esophageal varices: Vasopressin (non-selective)
- RS1 receptor polymorphism: Associated with social behavior traits

## Card 8: AVPR1B (Vasopressin V1b Receptor)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** AVPR1B (Class A GPCR, rhodopsin family)

**Binding Affinity:**
- AVP Ki ≈ 1-2 nM
- Oxytocin Ki ≈ 200-500 nM
- Desmopressin Ki ≈ 10-50 nM (partial agonist)
- SSR149415 Ki ≈ 2-5 nM (selective antagonist)
- Lixivaptan Ki ≈ 50-100 nM (V1b/V2 antagonist)

**Signal Transduction:**
- Primary: Gαq/11 → PLCβ → IP3/DAG → Ca²⁺ mobilization
- Gαs → adenylyl cyclase → ↑cAMP (in some cell types)
- MAPK/ERK pathway activation
- ACTH release from anterior pituitary corticotrophs

**Tissue Distribution:**
- Anterior pituitary (corticotrophs, primary site), brain (hippocampus, hypothalamus, amygdala)
- Pancreatic islets (β-cells), adrenal medulla
- Kidney (inner medullary collecting duct)

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Cushing's disease: V1bR overexpression in corticotroph adenomas
- Depression: V1b antagonists (SSR149415) anxiolytic in preclinical models
- ACTH regulation: CRH + AVP synergistic release of ACTH
- Stress response: V1bR mediates HPA axis activation
- ACTH-dependent Cushing's: V1bR as therapeutic target
- Reliance on V1b antagonists for treatment-resistant depression (clinical trials)
- Osilodrostat: Inhibits cortisol synthesis (not V1bR antagonist, but related Cushing's therapy)

## Card 9: AVPR2 (Vasopressin V2 Receptor)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** AVPR2 (Class A GPCR, rhodopsin family)

**Binding Affinity:**
- AVP Ki ≈ 1-2 nM
- Desmopressin (dDAVP) Ki ≈ 0.5-1 nM (selective agonist)
- Tolvaptan Ki ≈ 0.5-1 nM (selective antagonist)
- Lixivaptan Ki ≈ 1-2 nM (antagonist)
- Conivaptan Ki ≈ 5-10 nM (V1a/V2 antagonist)

**Signal Transduction:**
- Primary: Gαs → adenylyl cyclase → ↑cAMP → PKA activation
- AQP2 phosphorylation (Ser256) → apical membrane insertion
- UT-A1 urea transporter phosphorylation
- ENaC sodium channel activation
- Epac2 pathway

**Tissue Distribution:**
- Renal collecting duct (principal cells, primary site), thick ascending limb
- Inner medullary collecting duct (high expression)
- Vascular endothelium (VWF release), hepatocytes
- Brain (choroid plexus, ependymal cells)

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Nephrogenic diabetes insipidus (NDI): AVPR2 mutations (X-linked)
- Tolvaptan (Samsca®): Hyponatremia (SIADH, CHF)
- Conivaptan: V1a/V2 antagonist for euvolemic/hypervolemic hyponatremia
- Polycystic kidney disease: Tolvaptan (REPRISE trial) slows cyst growth
- Desmopressin: Nocturia, von Willebrand disease, hemophilia A
- Aquaporin-2 mutations: Autosomal NDI (AVPR2 normal)
- Lixivaptan: V2 antagonist in development for hyponatremia

## Card 10: GNRHR (Gonadotropin-Releasing Hormone Receptor)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** GNRHR (Class A GPCR, rhodopsin family)

**Binding Affinity:**
- GnRH (LHRH) Ki ≈ 0.5-2 nM
- Leuprolide Ki ≈ 0.1-0.5 nM (agonist)
- Cetrorelix Ki ≈ 0.1-0.5 nM (antagonist)
- Degarelix Ki ≈ 0.01-0.05 nM (antagonist)
- Triptorelin Ki ≈ 0.2-1 nM (agonist)

**Signal Transduction:**
- Primary: Gαq/11 → PLCβ → IP3/DAG → Ca²⁺ mobilization
- PKC activation → LH/FSH release
- MAPK/ERK pathway
- β-arrestin-mediated signaling (sustained)
- Receptor downregulation with chronic agonist exposure

**Tissue Distribution:**
- Anterior pituitary gonadotrophs (primary site), brain (hippocampus, amygdala)
- Ovarian granulosa cells, testicular Leydig cells
- Placenta, endometrium, breast tissue
- Prostate cancer cells (variable expression)

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Prostate cancer: GnRH agonists (leuprolide) for androgen deprivation therapy
- Breast cancer: GnRH agonists/antagonists for premenopausal women
- Endometriosis: GnRH agonists with add-back therapy
- Infertility: GnRH antagonist protocol (cetrorelix, ganirelix)
- GnRH pump therapy: Pulsatile GnRH for hypothalamic amenorrhea
- Degarelix: GnRH antagonist for prostate cancer (no flare)
- Relugolix: Oral GnRH antagonist (PROCISION trial)

## Card 11: FSHR (Follicle-Stimulating Hormone Receptor)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** FSHR (Class A GPCR, rhodopsin family)

**Binding Affinity:**
- FSH KD ≈ 0.1-1 nM
- FSH (recombinant) KD ≈ 0.2-0.5 nM
- Follitropin alfa/beta: Equivalent to endogenous FSH
- Small molecule FSHR agonists (thienopyrimidines): Ki ~100 nM
- FSHR antagonists: Under development

**Signal Transduction:**
- Primary: Gαs → adenylyl cyclase → ↑cAMP → PKA activation
- Gαi (at high FSH concentrations) → inhibitory
- β-arrestin-mediated ERK1/2 activation
- Akt/PI3K pathway
- Steroidogenic acute regulatory protein (StAR) induction

**Tissue Distribution:**
- Ovarian granulosa cells (primary site), testicular Sertoli cells
- Ovarian surface epithelium (pathological in cancer)
- Prostate, breast, endometrium (low expression)
- Bone (osteoblasts/osteoclasts)

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Infertility: Follitropin alfa/beta for controlled ovarian stimulation
- FSHR polymorphisms: Ser680Asn affects FSH sensitivity
- Ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS): Excessive FSH stimulation
- FSHR mutations: Inactivating (46,XY sex reversal) vs activating (ovarian hyperstimulation)
- Cancer: FSHR expression in ovarian, breast, prostate cancers
- Corifollitropin alfa: Long-acting FSH (Elonva®)
- Small molecule FSHR agonists: Non-peptide alternatives in development

## Card 12: LHCGR (Luteinizing Hormone/Choriogonadotropin Receptor)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** LHCGR (Class A GPCR, rhodopsin family)

**Binding Affinity:**
- LH KD ≈ 0.1-0.5 nM
- hCG KD ≈ 0.05-0.2 nM (higher affinity)
- Ovine LH: ~10% potency of hCG
- Small molecule LHCGR agonists: Under development

**Signal Transduction:**
- Primary: Gαs → adenylyl cyclase → ↑cAMP → PKA
- Gαq → PLCβ → IP3/DAG → Ca²⁺ ↑
- β-arrestin/ERK1/2 pathway
- StAR protein activation → steroidogenesis
- Desensitization via GRK-mediated phosphorylation

**Tissue Distribution:**
- Ovarian theca cells, granulosa cells (LH surge), corpus luteum
- Testicular Leydig cells (primary site in males)
- Uterus (myometrium, endometrium), fallopian tube
- Adrenal cortex, thyroid, breast, prostate

**Clinical Relevance:**
- hCG trigger: Ovulation induction in IVF (Ovidrel®, Pregnyl®)
- hCG for male hypogonadism: Stimulates testosterone production
- LHCGR mutations: Activating (familial male-limited precocious puberty)
- LHCGR inactivating mutations: Leydig cell hypoplasia (46,XY)
- Pregnancy detection: hCG assays (β-hCG specific)
- Hyperstimulation: hCG trigger + high OHSS risk → GnRH agonist trigger
- Recombinant LH (lutropin alfa): For LH-deficient hypogonadotropic hypogonadism

## Card 13: TSHR (Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone Receptor)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** TSHR (Class A GPCR, rhodopsin family)

**Binding Affinity:**
- TSH KD ≈ 0.1-0.5 nM
- Thyroid-stimulating antibodies (TSAb): Variable affinity
- Thyroid-blocking antibodies (TBAb): Variable affinity
- Small molecule TSHR agonists (ML-171): Ki ~1 μM

**Signal Transduction:**
- Primary: Gαs → adenylyl cyclase → ↑cAMP → PKA (thyroid hormone synthesis)
- Gαq → PLCβ → IP3/DAG → Ca²⁺ ↑
- Gα12/13 → RhoA pathway
- β-arrestin signaling
- TSHR cleavage → A-subunit shedding (unique among GPCRs)

**Tissue Distribution:**
- Thyroid follicular cells (primary site), thyroid C-cells
- Orbital fibroblasts (Graves' ophthalmopathy)
- Adipocytes (bone marrow, visceral), osteoblasts
- Brain (neurons), kidney, testes, heart

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Graves' disease: TSHR-stimulating antibodies → hyperthyroidism
- Graves' ophthalmopathy: Teprotumumab (anti-IGF-1R antibody)
- TSHR mutations: Activating (toxic adenoma), inactivating (hypothyroidism)
- Congenital hyperthyroidism: Germline activating TSHR mutations
- TSHR autoantibodies: Predict relapse/remission in Graves'
- Thyroid cancer: TSHR expression for radioiodine uptake
- TSHR-LH/CGR chimeric receptors: Used in research assays

## Card 14: PTHR1 (Parathyroid Hormone Receptor 1)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** PTH1R (Class B1 GPCR, family 2)

- PTH(1-34) KD ≈ 1-5 nM
- PTHrP(1-36) KD ≈ 1-5 nM
- Teriparatide (PTH 1-34) KD ≈ 2 nM
- Abaloparatide (PTHrP analog) KD ≈ 0.5-1 nM

**Signal Transduction:**
- Primary: Gαs → adenylyl cyclase → ↑cAMP → PKA
- Gαq → PLCβ → IP3/DAG → Ca²⁺ mobilization
- β-arrestin-mediated signaling (sustained PTH exposure)

- Jansen metaphyseal chondrodysplasia: Activating PTH1R mutations
- Blomstrand chondrodysplasia: Inactivating PTH1R mutations
- Anabolic vs catabolic: Intermittent PTH = anabolic, continuous = catabolic

## Card 15: CALCR (Calcitonin Receptor)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** CALCR (Class B GPCR, family 2; CT(a) receptor)

- Salmon calcitonin KD ≈ 0.1-0.5 nM
- Human calcitonin KD ≈ 5-10 nM
- Amylin KD ≈ 1-10 nM (when complexed with RAMP)

- Osteoclasts (primary site), kidney (medulla), brain (hypothalamus, area postrema)

- Peripheral nerves, adrenal medulla

- Pramlintide (Symlin®): Amylin analog for diabetes (acts on AMY receptors)
- Olcalcitonin: Long-acting calcitonin analogs in development
- Bone metastases: Calcitonin for pain management

## Card 16: SSTR1-5 (Somatostatin Receptors)

**IUPHAR Nomenclature:** SSTR1, SSTR2, SSTR3, SSTR4, SSTR5 (Class A GPCRs)

| Receptor | SST-14 | SST-28 | Octreotide | Lanreotide | Pasireotide |
|----------|--------|--------|------------|------------|-------------|
| SSTR1 | 0.1-2 | 0.1-1 | >1000 | >1000 | >1000 |

- β-arrestin-mediated internalization (SSTR2 > SSTR5)

- SSTR4: Brain, lung, heart, spleen, immune cells

**Clinical Relevance:**
- Acromegaly: Octreotide (SSTR2-preferring), lanreotide, pasireotide (SSTR5 > SSTR2)

- Carcinoid syndrome: Octreotide LAR, lanreotide autogel
- Cushing's disease: Pasireotide (Signifor®) - SSTR5 > SSTR2
- TSH-secreting adenomas: Octreotide (SSTR2 mediated)
- VIPomas: Octreotide for secretory diarrhea
- Small molecule SSTR agonists: Under development
