id: quiz-nephrology-renal-peptide-handling-001
question: "What is the primary mechanism by which small peptides (< 5 kDa) are processed by the kidney?"
options:
  - "Active secretion into the proximal tubule lumen via P-glycoprotein"
  - "Glomerular filtration followed by proximal tubular reabsorption and lysosomal degradation"
  - "Filtration at the glomerulus with no tubular reabsorption"
  - "Hepatic extraction before reaching the renal circulation"
correctIndex: 1
explanation: "Small peptides below approximately 5 kDa are freely filtered at the glomerulus. The proximal tubular cells then reabsorb them via megalin/cubilin receptor-mediated endocytosis. Once internalized, they are transported to lysosomes where proteolytic enzymes degrade them into amino acids, which are returned to the circulation. This process is highly efficient, so very little intact peptide appears in the final urine."

id: quiz-nephrology-glomerular-filtration-001
question: "Which factor most critically determines the glomerular filtration of a peptide?"
options:
  - "The peptide's isoelectric point"
  - "The molecular weight and effective radius relative to the glomerular filtration barrier pore size"
  - "The peptide's lipophilicity"
  - "The concentration of the peptide in the efferent arteriole"
correctIndex: 1
explanation: "The glomerular filtration barrier consists of fenestrated endothelium, the glomerular basement membrane, and podocyte slit diaphragms. Peptide filtration is governed primarily by molecular size and charge. Peptides under ~5 kDa are freely filtered, those between 5-60 kDa are partially filtered in inverse proportion to their size, and peptides above ~60 kDa (like albumin at 67 kDa) are largely excluded. The effective molecular radius is the key determinant."

question: "Which receptor complex on proximal tubular epithelial cells mediates the reabsorption of filtered peptides and low-molecular-weight proteins?"
options:
  - "SGLT2 / sodium-glucose transporter"
  - "Megalin / cubilin receptor complex"
  - "Tamm-Horsfall protein / uromodulin"
  - "Aquaporin-1 / water channel"
correctIndex: 1
explanation: "Megalin (LRP2) and cubilin form a multi-ligand receptor complex on the apical brush border of proximal tubular cells. Megalin is a large transmembrane glycoprotein that binds and endocytoses a wide range of filtered ligands including peptides, proteins, vitamin-binding proteins, and lipoproteins.   - "Preferential dilation of the efferent arteriole, reducing intraglomerular pressure and proteinuria"
  - "Dilation of both afferent and efferent arterioles equally"
  - "Constriction of the efferent arteriole to maintain GFR"

explanation: "ACE inhibitors block angiotensin II formation, which preferentially relaxes the efferent arteriole (angiotensin II constricts efferent > afferent arteriole). Ramipril and enalapril are well-studied for renoprotection (REIN, REIN-2 trials)."
