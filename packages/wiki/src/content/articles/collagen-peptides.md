# Collagen Peptides

## What Is Collagen?

Collagen is the most abundant protein in the human body, comprising approximately 30 percent of total protein. It provides structural support to skin, bones, tendons, ligaments, and cartilage. Collagen molecules are long, triple-helical structures composed of three polypeptide chains wound around each other.

The repeating sequence motif Gly-X-Y (where X is often proline and Y is often hydroxyproline) is essential for triple helix formation. Glycine, the smallest amino acid, fits in the interior of the helix where larger residues cannot.

## Collagen Structure

Each collagen chain is a left-handed helix. Three chains twist together into a right-handed triple helix called tropocollagen. These tropocollagen molecules assemble into fibrils through cross-linking, creating the tensile strength that characterizes connective tissue.

**Mnemonic:** Remember "GPH" for the signature collagen amino acids: **G**lycine, **P**roline, **H**ydroxyproline. These three residues dominate the collagen sequence and are essential for its structure.

## Hydrolyzed Collagen (Collagen Peptides)

Hydrolyzed collagen is produced by breaking native collagen into smaller peptide fragments (2 to 10 kDa) through enzymatic or acid hydrolysis. Unlike gelatin (partially hydrolyzed collagen), collagen peptides are fully soluble in cold water and are more readily absorbed in the digestive tract.

**Production process:**

1. Source material (bovine, marine, or porcine skin and bones) is cleaned and processed
2. Acid or alkali treatment removes minerals and non-collagen proteins
3. Enzymatic hydrolysis cleaves the triple helix into small peptides
4. Purification and spray-drying yields the final supplement

## Evidence for Skin Health

Multiple randomized controlled trials support collagen peptide supplementation for skin health:

- A 2019 meta-analysis of 11 studies found that oral collagen supplements (2.5 to 10 grams daily for 8 to 24 weeks) significantly improved skin elasticity, hydration, and wrinkle depth
- Hydroxyproline-containing dipeptides (Pro-Hyp) are absorbed intact and may stimulate fibroblasts to increase collagen synthesis
- Results typically appear after 4 to 8 weeks of consistent supplementation

## Evidence for Joint Health

Collagen peptides also show promise for joint health:

- A 2019 systematic review found moderate evidence for reduced joint pain in athletes and osteoarthritis patients
- The proposed mechanism involves providing substrate for cartilage repair and stimulating chondrocyte activity
- Doses of 10 grams daily for 6 months showed significant improvements in joint comfort scores

## Practical Considerations

- **Dosage:** 5 to 15 grams daily is the typical range used in clinical studies
- **Timing:** Morning or between meals may optimize absorption
- **Source:** Marine collagen peptides have smaller molecular weight and may be better absorbed; bovine collagen provides higher type I collagen content
- **Type I vs type II:** Type I collagen (bovine, marine) supports skin and bones; type II collagen (chicken sternum) specifically targets joint cartilage
- **Safety:** Collagen peptides are generally well tolerated; common side effects include mild gastrointestinal discomfort

## Learning Tip

When evaluating collagen supplements, check for clinical evidence at the specific dose provided. Many products contain sub-therapeutic amounts. Look for studies using the same dose and source as the product you are considering.

## Key Takeaways

- Collagen is the most abundant body protein, with a unique triple-helical structure
- Hydrolyzed collagen peptides are small fragments optimized for absorption
- Clinical evidence supports benefits for skin elasticity, hydration, and joint comfort
- Typical effective doses range from 5 to 15 grams daily for 8 to 24 weeks
