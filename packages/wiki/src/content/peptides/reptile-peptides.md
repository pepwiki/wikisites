# Reptile Peptides Database

A structured database of bioactive peptides from reptilian species, organized by taxonomic group. Reptile peptides represent a vast and largely untapped resource for drug discovery, with applications spanning neurology, metabolic disease, infectious disease, and biotechnology.

---

## Snake Venom Peptides

### 1. α-Bungarotoxin

```yaml
id: alpha-bungarotoxin
name: α-Bungarotoxin
sequence: IVCHTTATSPISAVTCPPGENLCYKMWRCDWRPDCCRGNVCCKHWFGYCGPNGICGCP
length: 74 aa
molecular_weight: 7984 Da
source_reptile: Bungarus multicinctus (many-banded krait)
reptile_family: Elapidae
target: Nicotinic acetylcholine receptor (nAChR)
mechanism: Irreversible competitive antagonist at postsynaptic α1-nAChR
bioactivity: Neuromuscular blockade, flaccid paralysis
therapeutic_potential: Research tool for nAChR mapping; template for muscle relaxant design
category: Snake Venom Peptide
```

α-Bungarotoxin is the prototypical postsynaptic neurotoxin from the many-banded krait. It binds irreversibly to the α-subunit of muscle-type nicotinic acetylcholine receptors at the neuromuscular junction, preventing acetylcholine binding and causing flaccid paralysis. Widely used in neuroscience research to map nAChR distribution and study neuromuscular transmission.

## Lizard Peptides

### 11. Exendin-4

```yaml
id: exendin-4
name: Exendin-4 (Exenatide)
sequence: HGEGTFTSDLSKQMEEEAVRLFIEWLKNGGPSSGAPPPS
length: 39 aa
molecular_weight: 4187 Da
source_reptile: Heloderma suspectum (Gila monster)
reptile_family: Helodermatidae
target: GLP-1 receptor (GLP-1R)
mechanism: Long-acting GLP-1R agonist, resistant to DPP-4 degradation
bioactivity: Glucose-dependent insulin secretion, glucagon suppression, gastric emptying delay
therapeutic_potential: FDA-approved (Byetta/Bydureon) for type 2 diabetes; obesity treatment
category: Lizard Peptide
```

Exendin-4 is the most therapeutically successful reptile peptide to date. Discovered in Gila monster saliva, it is a potent GLP-1 receptor agonist with a much longer half-life than native GLP-1 due to resistance to DPP-4 degradation. It was developed as exenatide (Byetta, Bydureon) for type 2 diabetes and has shown remarkable efficacy in glycemic control and weight loss.

## Key Therapeutic Areas

1. **Metabolic Disease**: Exendin-4 (exenatide) is the most successful reptile peptide drug, approved for type 2 diabetes
2. **Antimicrobials**: Crocodilian, turtle, gecko, and chameleon peptides offer novel anti-infective leads
3. **Neurology**: Snake venom neurotoxins serve as research tools and templates for neurological drugs
4. **Cardiovascular**: Sarafotoxins provide models for endothelin system research
5. **Drug Delivery**: Crotamine's cell-penetrating properties enable intracellular drug delivery
