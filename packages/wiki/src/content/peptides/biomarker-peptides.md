## Cardiac Biomarkers

### BNP (B-Type Natriuretic Peptide)

**ID:** BIO-001 | **Sequence:** 32-aa mature hormone (proBNP 108 → NT-proBNP 76 + BNP 32) | **Disease:** Heart failure; acute coronary syndrome

**Clinical Use:** Diagnosis and severity assessment of heart failure; guides therapy (sacubitril/valsartan); prognostic marker

**Cutoff Values:** <100 pg/mL (acute HF unlikely); 100–400 pg/mL (gray zone); >400 pg/mL (acute HF likely). Age-adjusted: >50 yrs >450; >75 yrs >900 pg/mL (NT-proBNP).

Secreted by ventricular cardiomyocytes in response to wall stretch. Active hormone with vasodilatory, natriuretic, and diuretic effects. Half-life ~20 min.

---

### NT-proBNP (N-Terminal Pro-BNP)

**ID:** BIO-002 | **Sequence:** 76 amino acids (inactive cleavage product) | **Disease:** Heart failure; pulmonary embolism; right heart strain

**Clinical Use:** Diagnosis/prognosis of heart failure; risk stratification in ACS; monitoring treatment response

**Cutoff Values:** <300 pg/mL (rule out acute HF); <125 pg/mL (rule out chronic HF, age-independent ESC). Varies by age and renal function.

Inactive fragment with longer half-life (~120 min) and higher plasma levels than BNP. More stable analyte; less affected by sacubitril therapy. Correlates with renal function.

---

### Troponin I (Cardiac)

**ID:** BIO-003 | **Sequence:** 209 amino acids (cardiac isoform) | **Disease:** Acute myocardial infarction (AMI); myocardial injury

**Clinical Use:** Diagnosis of AMI; detection of myocardial injury; risk stratification; rule-in/rule-out protocols

**Cutoff Values:** High-sensitivity (hs-cTnI): 99th percentile URL ~26 ng/L (varies by assay/sex). Rule-out: <5 ng/L at presentation (0/1h ESC algorithm).

Cardiac-specific isoform; absent from skeletal muscle. hs-cTnT/I assays detect 10-fold lower concentrations than conventional assays. Sex-specific cutoffs recommended (lower in women).

---

### Troponin T (Cardiac)

**ID:** BIO-004 | **Sequence:** 288 amino acids (cardiac isoform) | **Disease:** AMI; myocardial injury; chronic kidney disease (re-expression)

**Clinical Use:** Diagnosis of AMI; prognostic in HF, sepsis, pulmonary embolism; serial delta-change protocols

**Cutoff Values:** hs-cTnT: 99th percentile URL = 14 ng/L (single sex-independent cutoff for Roche assay). Rule-out: <5 ng/L at presentation.

Re-expressed in skeletal muscle in chronic kidney disease and muscular dystrophies (less specific than cTnI in these populations). hs-cTnT is the most widely validated assay globally.

---

### D-dimer

**ID:** BIO-005 | **Sequence:** Fragment of cross-linked fibrin (two D domains joined by cross-links) | **Disease:** Venous thromboembolism (VTE); disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC); aortic dissection

**Clinical Use:** Rule-out VTE (DVT/PE) in low-to-moderate probability patients; DIC diagnosis; aortic dissection screening

**Cutoff Values:** Standard: <500 ng/mL FEU (age-adjusted: age × 10 ng/mL for patients >50 years). High sensitivity assays: <250 ng/mL for rule-out.

Fibrin degradation product. High sensitivity (~95%) but low specificity for VTE. Elevated in inflammation, malignancy, pregnancy, trauma, and elderly. Age-adjusted cutoff improves specificity in elderly.

## Sepsis / Infection Biomarkers

### Procalcitonin (PCT)

**ID:** BIO-006 | **Sequence:** 116 amino acids (precursor of calcitonin) | **Disease:** Bacterial sepsis; lower respiratory tract infections; meningitis

**Clinical Use:** Differentiate bacterial from viral infection; guide antibiotic initiation/discontinuation; prognostic in sepsis

**Cutoff Values:** <0.1 μg/L (bacterial infection unlikely); 0.1–0.25 μg/L (possible); 0.25–0.5 μg/L (likely); >0.5 μg/L (strongly suggestive); >2 μg/L (sepsis likely). CRP comparison: PCT more specific for bacterial infection.

Precursor of calcitonin; normally produced in C-cells of thyroid. In bacterial infection, ectopic expression in neuroendocrine cells and macrophages drives systemic release. Not elevated in viral infections, autoimmune flares, or most malignancies.

## Neurodegenerative Disease Biomarkers

### Amyloid-beta 42 (Aβ42)

**ID:** BIO-007 | **Sequence:** DAEFRHDSGYEVHHQKLVFFAEDVGSNKGAIIGLMVGGVVIA (42 residues) | **Disease:** Alzheimer's disease (AD); cerebral amyloid angiopathy

**Clinical Use:** CSF biomarker for AD diagnosis (ATN classification); amyloid PET correlation; clinical trial eligibility; blood-based screening (p-tau217/Aβ42 ratio)

**Cutoff Values:** CSF Aβ42: <800–1000 pg/mL (depending on assay) indicates amyloid pathology. Aβ42/40 ratio <0.06–0.08 (assay-dependent). Plasma Aβ42/40 ratio: <0.06 (mass spec) suggests amyloid positivity.

Reduced CSF Aβ42 reflects brain amyloid deposition (sequestered in plaques). Combined with p-tau for ATN staging. Blood-based assays (p-tau217/Aβ42 ratio) approaching CSF/PET accuracy for screening.

---

### Alpha-Synuclein (α-Syn)

**ID:** BIO-008 | **Sequence:** 140 amino acids (native unfolded) | **Disease:** Parkinson's disease (PD); dementia with Lewy bodies (DLB); multiple system atrophy (MSA)

**Clinical Use:** CSF/blood seed amplification assay (SAA) for synucleinopathies; differential diagnosis of PD vs. atypical parkinsonism; prodromal detection

**Cutoff Values:** SAA: qualitative (positive/negative). CSF total α-syn: ~1000–2000 pg/mL (variable); reduced in PD. Phospho-Ser129 (pS129): elevated in PD CSF.

Seed amplification assay (SAA/RT-QuIC) detects pathological α-syn aggregates with ~95% sensitivity and ~95% specificity for PD. Can detect prodromal disease (REM sleep behavior disorder). Distinguishes synucleinopathies from tauopathies.

---

### Tau (Phosphorylated)

**ID:** BIO-009 | **Sequence:** 441 amino acids (longest CNS isoform); multiple phosphorylation sites (Thr181, Thr217, Thr231, Ser181, Ser396) | **Disease:** Alzheimer's disease; frontotemporal dementia; traumatic brain injury (CTE)

**Clinical Use:** CSF p-tau for AD diagnosis (ATN staging); blood-based p-tau217 for screening; prediction of progression from MCI to AD; clinical trial enrichment

**Cutoff Values:** CSF p-tau181: >20–30 pg/mL (assay-dependent). Plasma p-tau217: >0.25 pg/mL (Lumipulse) or ratio-based cutoffs. p-tau217: highest single-marker accuracy for amyloid and tau PET positivity.

Phosphorylated tau reflects tangle pathology. p-tau217 shows highest accuracy among blood biomarkers for both amyloid and tau PET positivity. p-tau181 most widely validated CSF marker. p-tau231 shows earliest changes in preclinical AD.

---

### Prion Protein (PrP)

**ID:** BIO-010 | **Sequence:** 209 amino acids (PrP^C, normal cellular form); 208 amino acids after signal peptide cleavage | **Disease:** Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease (CJD); fatal familial insomnia; Gerstmann-Sträussler-Scheinker; variant CJD

**Clinical Use:** CSF real-time quaking-induced conversion (RT-QuIC) for CJD diagnosis; surrogate endpoint in therapeutic trials; genetic prion disease screening

**Cutoff Values:** RT-QuIC: qualitative (positive/negative); sensitivity ~90–95% for sCJD; specificity ~99%. CSF 14-3-3 and tau are supportive but less specific.

RT-QuIC for PrP^Sc detection is the gold standard for antemortem CJD diagnosis. CSF 14-3-3 protein, total tau, and NSE are older, less specific markers. Genetic sequencing for PRNP mutations in familial cases.

## Cancer Biomarkers

### Chromogranin A (CgA)

**ID:** BIO-011 | **Sequence:** 439 amino acids (precursor processed to multiple fragments) | **Disease:** Neuroendocrine tumors (NETs); pheochromocytoma; small cell lung cancer; carcinoid syndrome

**Clinical Use:** Screening/diagnosis of NETs; monitoring treatment response; recurrence detection; prognostic in heart failure

**Cutoff Values:** <100 ng/mL (normal, assay-dependent). Elevated: >300–400 ng/mL suggestive of NET. PPIs, renal failure, atrophic gastritis, and chronic HF can cause false elevations.

Most widely used circulating NET marker. Secreted by neuroendocrine cells and co-released with hormones. Chromogranin fragments (WE-14, catestatin, vasostatin) have independent bioactivity. Multiple assays exist with varying cutoffs.

---

### PSA (Prostate-Specific Antigen)

**ID:** BIO-012 | **Sequence:** 237 amino acids (kallikrein-3, KLK3) | **Disease:** Prostate cancer; benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH); prostatitis

**Clinical Use:** Prostate cancer screening (controversial); post-treatment monitoring; recurrence detection; risk stratification (free/total PSA ratio; PSA density; PSA velocity)

**Cutoff Values:** Traditional: <4.0 ng/mL (high false-positive rate). Age-specific: 40–49 <1.5; 50–59 <2.5; 60–69 <3.5; 70–79 <4.5 ng/mL. Free/total PSA ratio <0.15 suggests malignancy (in 4–10 ng/mL gray zone).

Serine protease that liquefies seminal coagulum. Elevated in BPH, prostatitis, and after DRE/ejaculation. The Prostate Health Index (PHI) and 4Kscore improve specificity. PCA3 and SelectMDx are tissue-based alternatives.

---

### CA-125 (MUC16)

**ID:** BIO-013 | **Sequence:** Large glycoprotein (~22,000 amino acids, heavily O-glycosylated) | **Disease:** Epithelial ovarian cancer; endometriosis; pelvic inflammatory disease; peritoneal/pleural effusions

**Clinical Use:** Ovarian cancer monitoring (not screening); ROMA score (with HE4); treatment response; recurrence detection; risk of malignancy index (RMI)

**Cutoff Values:** <35 U/mL (normal). ROMA score combines CA-125, HE4, and menopausal status for risk stratification. Elevated in endometriosis (~50%), cirrhosis, CHF, pregnancy (1st trimester).

Mucin family glycoprotein expressed on coelomic epithelium (peritoneum, pleura, pericardium, Müllerian duct derivatives). Not suitable for population screening due to low sensitivity in early-stage disease. Combined with transvaginal ultrasound for high-risk surveillance.

---

### CEA (Carcinoembryonic Antigen)

**ID:** BIO-014 | **Sequence:** 668 amino acids (glycosylated, GPI-anchored) | **Disease:** Colorectal cancer; medullary thyroid cancer; pancreatic, gastric, lung, breast cancer (non-specific)

**Clinical Use:** Colorectal cancer monitoring; post-surgical recurrence detection (serial measurements); treatment response assessment; not useful for screening

**Cutoff Values:** <2.5 ng/mL (non-smokers); <5.0 ng/mL (smokers). Levels >10 ng/mL suggest advanced/metastatic disease.

Glycoprotein of the immunoglobulin superfamily. Oncofetal antigen normally expressed in fetal gut. Non-specific elevations in smokers, IBD, pancreatitis, liver disease. Serial monitoring more useful than single values.

---

### AFP (Alpha-Fetoprotein)

**ID:** BIO-015 | **Sequence:** 591 amino acids (glycoprotein) | **Disease:** Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC); germ cell tumors (yolk sac, mixed); neural tube defects (amniotic fluid)

**Clinical Use:** HCC screening in cirrhosis (with ultrasound every 6 months); germ cell tumor marker; treatment monitoring; obstetric screening

**Cutoff Values:** <10–20 ng/mL (normal adult). HCC: >200 ng/mL in cirrhosis highly suggestive. Germ cell tumors: >1000 ng/mL common with yolk sac component. Pregnancy: varies by gestational age.

Glycoprotein normally produced by fetal liver and yolk sac. AFP-L3% (Lens culinaris lectin-reactive fraction) >10% increases specificity for HCC. DCP (PIVKA-II) is a complementary HCC marker.

## Endocrine Biomarkers

### Calcitonin

**ID:** BIO-016 | **Sequence:** 32 amino acids (mature hormone) | **Disease:** Medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC); C-cell hyperplasia; thyroid nodules; chronic kidney disease

**Clinical Use:** MTC screening in thyroid nodules; post-thyroidectomy monitoring; pentagastrin/calcium stimulation test for C-cell hyperplasia; MEN2 screening

**Cutoff Values:** Basal: <10 pg/mL (normal). >100 pg/mL highly suspicious for MTC. Stimulated (pentagastrin): >100 pg/mL suggests C-cell hyperplasia. Sex-specific: males have higher baseline. PPIs can cause false elevation.

Calcium-regulating hormone from thyroid C-cells. Controversial as routine thyroid nodule screening (high false-positive rate); recommended when MTC suspicion or family history of MEN2. Preoperative calcitonin >500 pg/mL correlates with lymph node metastasis.

---

### PTH (Parathyroid Hormone)

**ID:** BIO-017 | **Sequence:** 84 amino acids (full-length PTH); active fragment PTH(1-34) | **Disease:** Primary hyperparathyroidism; secondary hyperparathyroidism (CKD); hypoparathyroidism; vitamin D deficiency

**Clinical Use:** Differential diagnosis of hypercalcemia; monitoring CKD-MBD; intraoperative parathyroid localization; guiding parathyroidectomy

**Cutoff Values:** Intact PTH: 10–65 pg/mL (normal, assay-dependent). Primary HPT: elevated or inappropriately normal with hypercalcemia. CKD stage 3–5: KDIGO targets 2–9× upper normal. Intraoperative: >50% drop at 10 min predicts successful removal.

Calcium homeostasis regulator. Intact PTH assay (1-84) standard; PTH(7-84) fragments detected by some assays. ioPTH monitoring during parathyroidectomy guides surgical completeness. Two-site immunoradiometric/immunochemiluminometric assays (IRMA/ICMA).

---

### Gastrin

**ID:** BIO-018 | **Sequence:** G-17 (17 amino acids, major circulating form); G-34 (big gastrin) | **Disease:** Zollinger-Ellison syndrome (gastrinoma); pernicious anemia; atrophic gastritis; *H. pylori* infection; MEN1

**Clinical Use:** Diagnosis of Zollinger-Ellison syndrome; differential of hypergastrinemia; monitoring after gastrinoma resection; evaluation of achlorhydria

**Cutoff Values:** Fasting G-17: <10 pmol/L (normal). >100 pmol/L suggests gastrinoma. Stimulated (>1000 after secretin) confirms ZES. PPIs must be withheld 2 weeks prior for accurate measurement.

G-17 is the major bioactive form produced by antral G-cells. Hypergastrinemia differential: (1) gastric acid hypersecretion (ZES, G-cell hyperplasia); (2) gastric hypoacidity (atrophic gastritis, PPI use). Secretin stimulation test is gold standard for ZES.

---

### Insulin

**ID:** BIO-019 | **Sequence:** 51 amino acids (A-chain 21 + B-chain 30, disulfide-linked) | **Disease:** Diabetes mellitus (type 1, type 2); insulinoma; metabolic syndrome; insulin resistance; PCOS

**Clinical Use:** Insulinoma diagnosis (72h fast); C-peptide-based assessment of endogenous insulin; HOMA-IR calculation; insulin resistance screening

**Cutoff Values:** Fasting insulin: 2–20 μIU/mL (normal). HOMA-IR = (fasting insulin × fasting glucose)/405; >2.5 suggests insulin resistance. Insulinoma: insulin >3 μIU/mL during hypoglycemia (glucose <45 mg/dL) at 72h fast.

Exogenous insulin assays must distinguish from endogenous. Insulin analogs (lispro, glargine) show variable cross-reactivity. Proinsulin measured separately in insulinoma workup. Use C-peptide to distinguish endogenous vs. exogenous hyperinsulinemia.

---

### C-Peptide

**ID:** BIO-020 | **Sequence:** 31 amino acids (connecting peptide of proinsulin) | **Disease:** Diabetes mellitus (differentiate type 1 vs. type 2); insulinoma; factitious hypoglycemia; monitoring residual beta-cell function

**Clinical Use:** Assessment of endogenous insulin secretion; differentiate T1DM from T2DM; insulinoma localization; monitor beta-cell function post-transplant; detect factitious insulin use

**Cutoff Values:** Fasting: 0.8–3.9 ng/mL (normal). Stimulated (glucagon): >1.8 ng/mL indicates adequate reserve. <0.6 ng/mL suggests T1DM or advanced T2DM. Factitious hypoglycemia: low C-peptide with exogenous insulin detected.

Equimolarly secreted with insulin but has longer half-life (~30 min vs. 5 min for insulin) and is not cleared by liver. More reliable marker of endogenous insulin secretion. Elevated in insulin resistance and insulinoma; undetectable in exogenous insulin administration.
