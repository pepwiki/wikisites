# Peptide Drug Conjugates (PDCs) Database

A comprehensive database of peptide drug conjugates (PDCs) including radiolabeled therapeutics, cytotoxic conjugates, immunomodulatory conjugates, and diagnostic agents.

PDCs are targeted drug delivery systems consisting of a peptide (targeting moiety) covalently linked to a drug payload via a linker. Compared to antibody drug conjugates (ADCs), PDCs offer smaller size, better tumor penetration, faster clearance, lower immunogenicity, and easier synthesis.

## Key Advantages of PDCs Over ADCs

| Property | PDCs | ADCs |
|----------|------|------|
| **Molecular Weight** | 1,000-20,000 Da | ~150,000 Da |
| **Tumor Penetration** | Excellent (small size) | Limited (large size) |
| **Clearance** | Rapid renal | Slow hepatic |
| **Immunogenicity** | Low | Moderate-High |
| **Synthesis** | Fully synthetic, scalable | Requires biological production |
| **Batch Consistency** | High | Variable |
| **Cost of Goods** | Lower | Higher |
| **Half-Life** | Short (minutes-hours) | Long (days-weeks) |
