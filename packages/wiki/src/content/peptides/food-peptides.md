## Milk-Derived Peptides

### VPP

**ID:** FOOD-001 | **Sequence:** Val-Pro-Pro | **Source:** Casein (β/κ)

**Function:** ACE inhibitor; antihypertensive

**IC50/Activity:** IC50 ~9 μM (ACE inhibition)

Tripeptide released during fermentation of milk by *Lactobacillus helveticus*. Commercially used in Calpis™ fermented milk. One of the most clinically studied food-derived peptides with demonstrated blood pressure-lowering effects in hypertensive subjects.

---

### IPP

**ID:** FOOD-002 | **Sequence:** Ile-Pro-Pro | **Source:** Casein (β/κ)

**Function:** ACE inhibitor; antihypertensive

**IC50/Activity:** IC50 ~5 μM (ACE inhibition)

Tripeptide co-released with VPP during fermentation. Synergistic antihypertensive effect when combined with VPP. Absorbed intact across intestinal epithelium.

---

### α-Lactorphin

**ID:** FOOD-003 | **Sequence:** Tyr-Gly-Leu-Phe | **Source:** α-Lactalbumin

**Function:** ACE inhibitor; opioid agonist (μ-receptor)

**IC50/Activity:** IC50 ~730 μM (ACE); Ki ~12 μM (μ-opioid)

Tetrapeptide from α-lactalbumin hydrolysis. Dual functionality as both ACE inhibitor and mild opioid receptor agonist.

---

### β-Lactorphin

**ID:** FOOD-004 | **Sequence:** Tyr-Leu-Leu-Phe | **Source:** β-Lactoglobulin

**Function:** ACE inhibitor; opioid agonist (μ-receptor)

**IC50/Activity:** IC50 ~170 μM (ACE); Ki ~54 μM (μ-opioid)

Derived from pepsin hydrolysis of β-lactoglobulin. More potent ACE inhibitor than α-lactorphin but weaker opioid activity.

---

### Lactoferricin B

**ID:** FOOD-005 | **Sequence:** Lys-Cys-Arg-Arg-Trp-Gln-Trp-Arg-Met-Lys-Lys-Leu-Gly-Ala-Pro-Ser-Ile-Thr-Cys-Val-Arg-Arg-Ala-Phe (LfcinB, residues 17–41 of lactoferrin) | **Source:** Bovine lactoferrin

**Function:** Antimicrobial; antifungal; antitumor; immunomodulatory

**IC50/Activity:** MIC 1.5–6.2 μM (gram-negative bacteria)

25-residue peptide released by pepsin digestion. Contains a disulfide-bonded loop in the parent protein but the bioactive linear fragment folds into an amphipathic β-sheet. Membrane-disrupting mechanism.

---

### Casoxin C

**ID:** FOOD-006 | **Sequence:** Tyr-Ile-Pro-Ile-Gln-Tyr-Val-Leu-Ser-Arg | **Source:** κ-Casein

**Function:** Opioid antagonist (μ-receptor); ACE inhibitor

**IC50/Activity:** IC50 ~40 μM (ACE); Ki ~30 μM (μ-opioid antagonist)

Released from κ-casein by pepsin digestion. One of few food-derived opioid antagonists.

---

### Casomorphin

**ID:** FOOD-007 | **Sequence:** Tyr-Pro-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro-Ile (β-casomorphin-7) | **Source:** β-Casein (A1/A2 variants)

**Function:** Opioid agonist (μ-receptor); immunomodulatory

**IC50/Activity:** Ki ~1 μM (μ-opioid)

Exorphin from β-casein. The BCM-7 variant (from A1 β-casein) has been linked to GI inflammation in epidemiological studies. Resistant to digestive proteases.

## Plant-Derived Peptides

### Soymorphin-5

**ID:** FOOD-008 | **Sequence:** Tyr-Pro-Phe-Val-Val | **Source:** Glycinin (soy protein)

**Function:** Opioid agonist (μ-receptor); ACE inhibitor

**IC50/Activity:** Ki ~15 μM (μ-opioid); IC50 ~68 μM (ACE)

Derived from soy glycinin hydrolysis by pepsin. Mild μ-opioid agonist with anxiolytic-like activity in animal models.

---

### Rubiscolin

**ID:** FOOD-009 | **Sequence:** Tyr-Pro-Leu (rubiscolin-6: Tyr-Pro-Leu-Trp-Leu-Ser) | **Source:** Rubisco (spinach, *Chenopodium*)

**Function:** Opioid agonist (δ-receptor); memory enhancement

**IC50/Activity:** Ki ~8.4 μM (δ-opioid)

First plant-derived δ-opioid peptide. The hexapeptide form shows memory-improving effects in mice via δ-receptor activation.

---

### Gluten Exorphin

**ID:** FOOD-010 | **Sequence:** Tyr-Gly-Gly-Trp-Leu (exorphin C5); H-Tyr-Gly-Gly-Trp-OH (exorphin A5) | **Source:** Wheat gluten

**Function:** Opioid agonist (μ/δ-receptor)

**IC50/Activity:** Ki ~24 μM (μ-opioid, exorphin C5)

Released during gastrointestinal digestion of wheat gluten. May contribute to "bread addiction" hypotheses. Crosses blood-brain barrier in animal models.

---

### Gliadorphin

**ID:** FOOD-011 | **Sequence:** Tyr-Pro-Gln-Pro-Gln-Pro-Phe (gliadorphin-7) | **Source:** Gliadin (wheat)

**Function:** Opioid agonist (μ-receptor); immunomodulatory

**IC50/Activity:** Ki ~40 μM (μ-opioid)

Gliadin-derived exorphin resistant to proteolysis. Investigated in celiac disease and autism spectrum hypotheses due to opioid-like effects on gut and CNS.

---

## Endogenous/Dietary Antioxidant Peptides

### Carnosine

**ID:** FOOD-012 | **Sequence:** β-Alanyl-L-histidine | **Source:** Skeletal muscle (beef, poultry, fish); endogenous

**Function:** Antioxidant; anti-glycation; pH buffer; neuroprotective

**IC50/Activity:** IC50 ~25 μM (lipid peroxidation inhibition)

Dipeptide concentrated in excitable tissues (1–20 mM in muscle). Chelates divalent metals, scavenges ROS/RNS, and inhibits AGE formation. Substrate for carnosinase (CNDP1); genetic variants affect diabetic nephropathy risk.

---

### Anserine

**ID:** FOOD-013 | **Sequence:** β-Alanyl-1-methyl-L-histidine | **Source:** Poultry muscle, fish, brain

**Function:** Antioxidant; anti-inflammatory; pH buffer

**IC50/Activity:** IC50 ~30 μM (lipid peroxidation)

Methylated analog of carnosine with similar but distinct antioxidant profile. More resistant to carnosinase degradation than carnosine.

---

### Glutathione

**ID:** FOOD-014 | **Sequence:** γ-L-Glutamyl-L-cysteinylglycine (GSH) | **Source:** Ubiquitous in foods; endogenous tripeptide

**Function:** Antioxidant; detoxification cofactor; redox homeostasis

**IC50/Activity:** Intracellular concentration 1–10 mM; ratio GSH/GSSG >100:1 (healthy cells)

Master intracellular antioxidant. Cofactor for glutathione peroxidase and glutathione S-transferase. Redox-sensitive; ratio GSH:GSSG is a cellular health marker.

## Broad-Functional Peptides

### ACE-Inhibitory Peptides (General)

**ID:** FOOD-015 | **Sequence:** Various (see individual entries) | **Source:** Casein, whey, soy, fish, egg, plant proteins

**Function:** ACE inhibition; antihypertensive

**IC50/Activity:** IC50 range 0.6–1000 μM depending on peptide

Structure-activity: C-terminal dipeptides with hydrophobic residues (Trp, Phe, Tyr, Leu, Ile, Pro) preferred by ACE active site. Tripeptides often more potent than longer analogs.

---

### Antioxidant Peptides (General)

**ID:** FOOD-016 | **Sequence:** Various (see individual entries) | **Source:** Muscle, milk, soy, cereal, marine proteins

**Function:** ROS scavenging; metal chelation; lipid peroxidation inhibition

**IC50/Activity:** ORAC values 0.5–50 μmol TE/mg

Mechanisms: (1) direct radical scavenging via His, Tyr, Trp, Met, Cys residues; (2) Fe²⁺/Cu²⁺ chelation via His, Glu, Asp; (3) quenching of singlet oxygen. His-containing peptides typically most active.

---

### Mineral-Binding Peptides

**ID:** FOOD-017 | **Sequence:** Caseinophosphopeptides (CPP): Ser(P)-Ser(P)-Ser(P)-Glu-Glu (cluster sequence) | **Source:** Casein, egg yolk phosvitin

**Function:** Calcium, iron, zinc binding; mineral bioavailability enhancement

**IC50/Activity:** Ca²⁺ binding capacity ~4 mol/mol CPP

Caseinophosphopeptides (CPPs) form soluble organo-phosphopeptide mineral complexes that prevent precipitation in the small intestine, enhancing absorption. Used in functional foods and supplements. Phosphoserine clusters are essential for binding.
