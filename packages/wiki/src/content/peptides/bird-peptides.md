# Bird Peptides Database

A structured database of bioactive peptides from avian species, organized by functional category. Bird peptides represent important sources of antimicrobial agents, neuropeptides, peptide hormones, and structural proteins with applications spanning veterinary medicine, agriculture, and human therapeutics.

Avian beta-defensin 1 is constitutively expressed in chicken epithelial tissues including the trachea, bursa of Fabricius, and skin. ```

Gallinacins constitute the major beta-defensin gene cluster in chickens, comprising at least 14 distinct peptides (Gal-1 to Gal-14). They are encoded in a gene cluster on chromosome 3 and show tissue-specific expression patterns. Gallinacins are the primary antimicrobial peptides in chicken heterophils, the avian equivalent of mammalian neutrophils, and are released upon degranulation to kill phagocytosed pathogens.

### 31. Pituitary Extract Peptides (Pigeon)

```yaml
id: pigeon-pituitary-extract
name: Pigeon Pituitary Peptide Extract
sequence: Complex mixture of anterior pituitary hormones
length: Variable (multiple peptide components)
molecular_weight: Variable (500-45,000 Da range)
source_bird: Columba livia (Pigeon)
bird_order: Columbiformes
target: Multiple endocrine receptors
mechanism: Various hormonal signaling pathways (GH, PRL, TSH, LH, FSH, ACTH)
bioactivity: Endocrine stimulation, growth promotion, reproductive regulation
therapeutic_potential: Historical use in endocrinology research; comparative endocrinology studies
category: Avian Specialized Peptide
```

Pigeon pituitary extracts were historically important in endocrinology research before the availability of purified recombinant hormones. These extracts contain a complex mixture of anterior pituitary hormones including growth hormone, prolactin, thyroid-stimulating hormone, and gonadotropins. Pigeon pituitary glands were particularly valued for studying prolactin, as pigeons have exceptionally high prolactin levels related to crop milk production during incubation.

Avian serum contains a diverse array of bioactive peptides including complement-derived fragments, antimicrobial peptide sequences released from serum proteins, and acute-phase mediators. These peptides contribute to the systemic immune defense of birds and are upregulated during infection. Studying serum peptide profiles can provide valuable biomarkers for monitoring avian health and disease status in commercial flocks.

### 33. Feather Keratin Peptides

```yaml
id: feather-keratin-peptides
name: Feather Keratin-Derived Peptides
sequence: Derived from beta-keratin hydrolysis; rich in glycine, alanine, serine, and proline
length: 3-30 aa (hydrolysate fragments)
molecular_weight: 250-3500 Da
source_bird: Various avian species
bird_order: Multiple orders
target: Skin and connective tissue cells
mechanism: Collagen synthesis stimulation, antioxidant activity, moisture retention
bioactivity: Skin health promotion, antioxidant protection, UV absorption
therapeutic_potential: Cosmetic ingredients; wound healing applications; sustainable protein source from feather waste
category: Avian Specialized Peptide
```

Feather keratin peptides are derived from the enzymatic or chemical hydrolysis of feather beta-keratin, one of nature's most abundant structural proteins. Feathers are produced in massive quantities as agricultural waste, making keratin peptides an attractive sustainable resource. Bioactive peptides from feather keratin have demonstrated antioxidant, UV-absorptive, and skin-hydrating properties, with applications in cosmetics and wound care. They also have potential as a sustainable protein supplement in animal feed.

### 34. Eggshell Matrix Peptides

```yaml
id: eggshell-matrix-peptides
name: Eggshell Matrix Peptides
sequence: Derived from eggshell matrix proteins including ovocleidin-17, ovocleidin-116, and osteopontin
length: 5-40 aa (bioactive fragments)
molecular_weight: 500-5000 Da
source_bird: Gallus gallus (Chicken)
bird_order: Galliformes
target: Calcium carbonate crystal formation; osteoblasts and osteoclasts
mechanism: Modulation of crystal nucleation and growth; bone cell signaling
bioactivity: Biomineralization regulation, bone health promotion, calcium-binding
therapeutic_potential: Bone health nutraceuticals; understanding of eggshell formation; calcium metabolism research
category: Avian Specialized Peptide
```

The eggshell matrix contains a complex mixture of proteins and peptides that regulate the biomineralization of calcium carbonate during eggshell calcification. Bioactive peptides derived from matrix proteins such as ovocleidin-17, ovocleidin-116, and osteopontin can influence calcium carbonate crystallization and have been shown to promote osteoblast activity and inhibit osteoclast differentiation. These peptides have potential as nutraceuticals for bone health and osteoporosis prevention.

### 35. Crop Milk Peptides

```yaml
id: crop-milk-peptides
name: Pigeon Crop Milk Peptides
sequence: Derived from crop milk proteins including pigeon milk-specific proteins and immunoglobulins
length: 3-25 aa (bioactive fragments)
molecular_weight: 300-3000 Da
source_bird: Columba livia (Pigeon)
bird_order: Columbiformes
target: Squab immune system and gut epithelium
mechanism: Growth factor activity, immunoglobulin transfer, gut microbiome modulation
bioactivity: Neonatal immune protection, growth promotion, gut health maintenance
therapeutic_potential: Understanding avian neonatal immunity; probiotic and prebiotic development; comparative lactation biology
category: Avian Specialized Peptide
```

Crop milk is a unique secretion produced by both male and female pigeons and doves in their crop glands during the breeding season. This nutrient-rich substance contains proteins, lipids, and bioactive peptides that support the rapid growth of squabs. Crop milk also contains immunoglobulins and antimicrobial peptides that provide passive immunity to hatchlings before their own immune systems mature. Research on crop milk peptides contributes to understanding the evolution of parental nutrient provisioning in birds.
