## Amino Acids

### Residue

**Category:** Amino Acids | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** amino-acid, peptide-bond, backbone

An individual amino acid unit within a peptide chain, connected by peptide bonds. Named because what remains after water is lost during condensation.

---

### Side Chain

**Category:** Amino Acids | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** amino-acid, chirality, isoelectric-point

The variable R-group attached to the alpha-carbon of an amino acid. Determines chemical properties including charge, hydrophobicity, and reactivity.

---

### Backbone

**Category:** Amino Acids | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** residue, peptide-bond, phi-psi-angles

The repeating N-Calpha-C(=O) unit of a peptide chain. Provides structural framework while side chains project outward.

---

### Chirality

**Category:** Amino Acids | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** alpha-carbon, stereoisomer, enantiomer

The property of a molecule being non-superimposable on its mirror image. Natural amino acids are L-configured. Critical for enzyme recognition and drug activity.

---

### Zwitterion

**Category:** Amino Acids | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** isoelectric-point, pKa, amino-acid

A molecule with both positive and negative charges but net charge zero. Amino acids exist as zwitterions at physiological pH.

---

## Peptide Bonds

### Condensation

**Category:** Peptide Bonds | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** hydrolysis, peptide-bond, dehydration-synthesis

A reaction joining two molecules with loss of water. Peptide bond formation is condensation between carboxyl and amino groups.

---

### Hydrolysis

**Category:** Peptide Bonds | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** condensation, peptide-bond, protease

Breaking a bond by adding water. Reverse of condensation. Peptide bonds are hydrolyzed by proteases or strong acid/base.

---

### Amide Bond

**Category:** Peptide Bonds | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** peptide-bond, resonance, planarity

A covalent bond between a carbonyl carbon and nitrogen. The peptide bond is a specific amide bond between amino acids.

---

### Planarity

**Category:** Peptide Bonds | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** resonance, amide-bond, phi-psi-angles

The peptide bond group (C=O, N-H, and connecting atoms) is planar due to partial double-bond character from resonance. Limits conformational flexibility.

---

### Resonance

**Category:** Peptide Bonds | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** planarity, amide-bond, peptide-bond

Delocalization of electrons across multiple atoms. In peptide bonds, resonance between C=O and C-N forms gives partial double-bond character and planarity.

---

## Structure

### Primary Structure

**Category:** Structure | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** secondary-structure, amino-acid-sequence, N-terminus

The linear sequence of amino acids in a peptide chain, read from N-terminus to C-terminus. Encoded by DNA and determines all higher-order structure.

---

### Secondary Structure

**Category:** Structure | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** alpha-helix, beta-sheet, hydrogen-bond

Local folding patterns stabilized by hydrogen bonds between backbone atoms. Common types: alpha-helix and beta-sheet.

---

### Tertiary Structure

**Category:** Structure | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** quaternary-structure, fold, disulfide-bond

The overall 3D shape of a single peptide chain. Stabilized by hydrophobic interactions, hydrogen bonds, ionic bonds, and disulfide bridges.

---

### Quaternary Structure

**Category:** Structure | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** tertiary-structure, subunit, oligomer

The arrangement of multiple peptide subunits into a functional complex. Stabilized by same forces as tertiary structure. Example: hemoglobin (4 subunits).

---

### Fold

**Category:** Structure | **Difficulty:** Advanced
**Related Terms:** domain, tertiary-structure, protein-folding

A distinctive 3D arrangement of secondary structural elements. Proteins with same fold may have different sequences. Classification by SCOP/CATH databases.

---

## Synthesis

### Solid-Phase Peptide Synthesis (SPPS)

**Category:** Synthesis | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** fmoc, boc, resin, coupling

Method for synthesizing peptides on insoluble resin support. Amino acids added one at a time from C-to-N terminus. Enables automation and rapid purification.

---

### Fmoc

**Category:** Synthesis | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** spps, protecting-group, boc

Fluorenylmethyloxycarbonyl. A base-labile protecting group for amino groups in SPPS. Removed by piperidine. Preferred for its mild conditions.

---

### Boc

**Category:** Synthesis | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** spps, protecting-group, fmoc

tert-Butyloxycarbonyl. An acid-labile protecting group for amino groups. Removed by trifluoroacetic acid. Used in Boc-SPPS strategy.

---

### Resin

**Category:** Synthesis | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** spps, linker, cleavage

Insoluble polymer support for SPPS. Common types: Wang, Rink, Merrifield resin. Allows washing away reagents without losing peptide.

---

### Coupling

**Category:** Synthesis | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** spps, coupling-reagent, activation

Formation of peptide bond between activated amino acid and growing chain on resin. Coupling reagents: HATU, HBTU, DIC/HOBt. Efficiency critical for yield.

---

## Pharmacology

### Bioavailability

**Category:** Pharmacology | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** adme, half-life, first-pass-metabolism

The fraction of administered drug that reaches systemic circulation unchanged. Oral bioavailability of peptides is typically <2% due to degradation and poor absorption.

---

### Half-life

**Category:** Pharmacology | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** clearance, pharmacokinetics, elimination

Time required for drug concentration to decrease by 50%. Influenced by distribution, metabolism, and excretion. Peptide half-lives range from minutes to days.

---

### Clearance

**Category:** Pharmacology | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** half-life, volume-of-distribution, pharmacokinetics

Volume of plasma cleared of drug per unit time. Total clearance = hepatic + renal clearance. Determines dosing requirements.

---

### ADME

**Category:** Pharmacology | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** bioavailability, pharmacokinetics, clearance

Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion. Framework for understanding drug pharmacokinetics. Each phase affects drug concentration at target site.

---

### Pharmacokinetics

**Category:** Pharmacology | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** adme, pharmacodynamics, bioavailability

Study of drug absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion over time. Answers: what does the body do to the drug? Contrast with pharmacodynamics.

---

## Signaling

### Hormone

**Category:** Signaling | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** neurotransmitter, receptor, endocrine

Chemical messenger produced by endocrine glands, transported via blood to target organs. Peptide hormones include insulin, glucagon, and growth hormone.

---

### Neurotransmitter

**Category:** Signaling | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** hormone, synapse, neuropeptide

Chemical messenger released at synapses to transmit nerve signals. Peptide neurotransmitters: substance P, endorphins, neuropeptide Y.

---

### Receptor

**Category:** Signaling | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** ligand, agonist, antagonist

Protein that binds specific molecules (ligands) and triggers cellular response. Types: G-protein coupled, ion channel, enzyme-linked, nuclear.

---

### Agonist

**Category:** Signaling | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** antagonist, receptor, ligand

Molecule that binds receptor and activates it, producing biological response. Full agonists produce maximum response; partial agonists produce submaximal response.

---

### Antagonist

**Category:** Signaling | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** agonist, receptor, inhibitor

Molecule that binds receptor without activating it, blocking agonist binding. Competitive antagonists can be overcome by increasing agonist concentration.

---

## Analysis

### HPLC

**Category:** Analysis | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** purification, reverse-phase, mass-spectrometry

High-Performance Liquid Chromatography. Separation technique for peptides using stationary phase and mobile phase under high pressure. Used for purification and analysis.

---

### Mass Spectrometry

**Category:** Analysis | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** hplc, maldi, electrospray

Analytical technique measuring mass-to-charge ratio of ionized molecules. Essential for peptide identification, sequencing, and post-translational modification analysis.

---

### NMR Spectroscopy

**Category:** Analysis | **Difficulty:** Advanced
**Related Terms:** circular-dichroism, structure-determination, chemical-shift

Nuclear Magnetic Resonance. Uses magnetic fields to probe atomic environments. Provides 3D structure information and dynamics data for peptides in solution.

---

### Circular Dichroism

**Category:** Analysis | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** nmr, secondary-structure, thermal-stability

Difference in absorption of left and right circularly polarized light. Used to assess secondary structure content (alpha-helix, beta-sheet) and thermal stability.

---

### Edman Degradation

**Category:** Analysis | **Difficulty:** Advanced
**Related Terms:** mass-spectrometry, sequencing, primary-structure

Sequential method for determining amino acid sequence from N-terminus. Each cycle removes and identifies one residue. Limited to ~50 residues.

---

## Drug Delivery

### Nanoparticle

**Category:** Drug Delivery | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** liposome, drug-delivery, encapsulation

Particle 1-100 nm in size used for drug delivery. Types: polymeric, lipid, metallic. Can protect peptides from degradation and enable targeted delivery.

---

### Liposome

**Category:** Drug Delivery | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** nanoparticle, pegylated-liposome, drug-carrier

Spherical vesicle with phospholipid bilayer. Can encapsulate hydrophilic drugs in core and hydrophobic drugs in membrane. Used for peptide delivery.

---

### PEGylation

**Category:** Drug Delivery | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** half-life, clearance, polyethylene-glycol

Attachment of polyethylene glycol (PEG) to peptides/proteins. Increases half-life by reducing renal clearance and proteolytic degradation. May reduce immunogenicity.

---

### Prodrug

**Category:** Drug Delivery | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** bioavailability, drug-metabolism, activation

Inactive compound converted to active drug in vivo. Peptide prodrugs may improve oral bioavailability by protecting from degradation or enhancing absorption.

---

### Enteric Coating

**Category:** Drug Delivery | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** oral-delivery, gastric-stability, drug-formulation

pH-sensitive polymer coating that prevents drug release in stomach but allows release in intestine. Protects acid-labile peptides from gastric degradation.

---

## Clinical

### Clinical Trial

**Category:** Clinical | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** fda, ind, gmp

Research study in human subjects to evaluate medical intervention. Phases: I (safety), II (efficacy), III (confirmation), IV (post-market). Required for drug approval.

---

### FDA

**Category:** Clinical | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** clinical-trial, nda, ind

U.S. Food and Drug Administration. Regulatory agency responsible for drug approval, food safety, and medical device regulation.

---

### GMP

**Category:** Clinical | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** fda, quality-control, manufacturing

Good Manufacturing Practice. Quality assurance guidelines ensuring drugs are consistently produced and controlled. Required for pharmaceutical manufacturing.

---

### NDA

**Category:** Clinical | **Difficulty:** Advanced
**Related Terms:** fda, clinical-trial, drug-approval

New Drug application. Submitted to FDA for marketing approval after clinical trials. Contains all efficacy, safety, and manufacturing data.

---

### Bioequivalence

**Category:** Clinical | **Difficulty:** Advanced
**Related Terms:** bioavailability, generic-drug, pharmacokinetics

Similarity in bioavailability between test and reference drug products. Criteria: 90% CI of AUC and Cmax ratios within 80-125%. Required for generic drugs.

---

## Disease

### Diabetes

**Category:** Disease | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** insulin, glp-1, blood-glucose

Metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar. Type 1: autoimmune destruction of beta cells. Type 2: insulin resistance. GLP-1 analogs used in treatment.

---

### Obesity

**Category:** Disease | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** glp-1, appetite-regulation, metabolic-syndrome

Excessive body fat accumulation (BMI ≥30). Associated with increased risk of diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and certain cancers. GLP-1 agonists used for treatment.

---

### Cancer

**Category:** Disease | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** peptide-vaccine, targeted-therapy, tumor

Uncontrolled cell growth and division. Peptide-based approaches: targeted therapy, immunotherapy, peptide vaccines, and drug conjugates.

---

### Neurodegeneration

**Category:** Disease | **Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Related Terms:** amyloid, neuroprotection, blood-brain-barrier

Progressive loss of neuronal structure and function. Examples: Alzheimer's, Parkinson's. Peptide-based therapeutics under investigation for neuroprotection.

---

### Inflammation

**Category:** Disease | **Difficulty:** Beginner
**Related Terms:** bradykinin, substance-p, cytokine

Immune response to injury or infection. Peptide mediators: bradykinin, substance P. Anti-inflammatory peptides under development for chronic inflammation.
