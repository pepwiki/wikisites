# Drug Delivery Systems

## Introduction

Peptide drug delivery faces unique challenges due to the inherent instability of peptides in biological environments. This chapter covers advanced delivery technologies that overcome limitations of poor oral bioavailability, short half-life, and rapid clearance.

## PEGylation

**Polyethylene glycol (PEG) conjugation** is the most established technology for extending peptide half-life.

### Mechanism of Action

**Steric Shielding:**

- PEG chains create hydrodynamic barrier
- Protect from protease degradation
- Reduce renal clearance
- Mask immunogenic epitopes

**Increased Hydrodynamic Radius:**

- PEG (5 kDa) increases apparent MW by 5-10x
- Glomerular filtration threshold: ~60 kDa
- PEGylation effectively increases circulating half-life

### PEG Types

| PEG Type      | MW (kDa) | Application      | Half-life Extension |
| ------------- | -------- | ---------------- | ------------------- |
| Linear PEG    | 2-40     | Most peptides    | Moderate            |
| Branched PEG  | 10-40    | Complex peptides | High                |
| Y-shaped PEG  | 20-40    | High MW peptides | Very high           |
| Multi-arm PEG | 40-100   | Proteins         | Maximum             |

### Site-Specific PEGylation

**Strategies:**

- N-terminal PEGylation
- Lysine side chain PEGylation
- Cysteine PEGylation (site-selective)
- Enzymatic PEGylation (transglutaminase)

**Challenges:**

- Heterogeneous products
- Reduced activity at PEGylation site
- Immunogenicity of PEG

### Clinical Examples

| Drug                       | PEG Size | Indication  | Half-life   |
| -------------------------- | -------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| PEGylated interferon alpha | 40 kDa   | Hepatitis C | 7-10 days   |
| PEGylated G-CSF            | 20 kDa   | Neutropenia | 15-80 hours |
| PEG-asparaginase           | 5 kDa    | Leukemia    | 5.7 days    |

## Lipid Nanoparticles

### Liposomes

**Structure:** Phospholipid bilayer vesicles
**Size:** 50-1000 nm
**Applications:**

- Encapsulation of hydrophilic peptides (aqueous core)
- Encapsulation of hydrophobic peptides (bilayer)
- Targeted delivery via surface ligands

**Formulation Components:**

- Phospholipids (DSPC, DPPC)
- Cholesterol (stability)
- PEG-lipids (stealth properties)
- Targeting ligands (antibodies, peptides)

**Types:**

- Conventional liposomes
- Stealth liposomes (PEGylated)
- Targeted liposomes
- Thermostensitive liposomes

### Solid Lipid Nanoparticles (SLN)

**Structure:** Solid lipid matrix
**Advantages:**

- Better stability than liposomes
- Scalable production
- Controlled release possible

### Nanostructured Lipid Carriers (NLC)

**Structure:** Mixed solid/liquid lipid matrix
**Advantages:**

- Higher drug loading
- Better stability
- Controlled release

## Cyclodextrin Complexation

**Cyclodextrins** are cyclic oligosaccharides that form inclusion complexes with hydrophobic molecules.

### Structure

**Types:**

- α-cyclodextrin: 6 glucose units
- β-cyclodextrin: 7 glucose units
- γ-cyclodextrin: 8 glucose units
- Hydroxypropyl-β-cyclodextrin (HPβCD): Modified, improved solubility

### Mechanism

**Inclusion Complex Formation:**

- Hydrophobic cavity accommodates guest molecules
- Hydrophilic exterior improves solubility
- Reduces protease access
- Improves stability

### Applications

- Solubility enhancement
- Stability improvement
- Taste masking
- Controlled release

## Depot Formulations

### Microspheres

**PLGA (Poly(lactic-co-glycolic acid)) Microspheres:**

- Biodegradable polymer
- Controlled release over weeks to months
- FDA-approved for several peptide drugs

**Mechanism:**

1. Peptide encapsulation in polymer matrix
2. Initial burst release
3. Diffusion-controlled release
4. Polymer degradation release

**Clinical Examples:**

- Leuprolide microspheres (1-6 months)
- Octreotide microspheres (1 month)

### In Situ Forming Depots

**PLGA-PEG-PLGA Systems:**

- Injectable solution
- Forms gel depot in situ
- Controlled release

**Advantages:**

- Ease of administration
- No surgical implantation
- Tunable release profile

### Implantable Devices

**Non-biodegradable:**

- Osmotic pumps
- Reservoir systems
- Matrix devices

**Biodegradable:**

- PLGA rods
- Collagen implants
- Hyaluronic acid gels

## Nasal Delivery

### Advantages

- Avoids GI degradation
- Bypasses first-pass metabolism
- Direct nose-to-brain delivery possible
- Non-invasive

### Challenges

- Mucociliary clearance
- Enzymatic degradation
- Variable absorption
- Nasal irritation

### Permeation Enhancers

| Enhancer                  | Mechanism               | Safety   |
| ------------------------- | ----------------------- | -------- |
| Bile salts                | Membrane disruption     | Moderate |
| Chelating agents          | Tight junction opening  | Good     |
| Cell-penetrating peptides | Transcellular transport | Good     |
| Surfactants               | Membrane fluidization   | Variable |

### Formulation Strategies

- Solution formulations
- Powder formulations
- Gel formulations
- Nanoparticle formulations
- Microsphere formulations

## Oral Delivery

### SNAC (Sodium N-[8-(2-hydroxybenzoyl) amino] caprylate)

**Mechanism:**

- Absorption enhancer
- Localizes semaglutide to stomach
- Increases transcellular absorption
- Reduces enzymatic degradation

**Clinical Application:**

- Semaglutide (Rybelsus)
- First oral GLP-1 agonist
- 3-4% bioavailability

### Other Oral Delivery Technologies

**Permeation Enhancers:**

- SNAC (sodium caprate)
- Salcaprozate sodium (SNAC)
- Medium-chain fatty acids

**Enzyme Inhibitors:**

- Aprotinin
- Soybean trypsin inhibitor
- Bowel protective agents

**Mucoadhesive Systems:**

- Chitosan
- Lectins
- Thiolated polymers

**Nanoparticle Systems:**

- Lipid nanoparticles
- Polymer nanoparticles
- Cell-penetrating peptide conjugates

## Pulmonary Delivery

### Advantages

- Large surface area
- Thin epithelium
- Rich blood supply
- Avoids first-pass metabolism

### Challenges

- Mucociliary clearance
- Macrophage uptake
- Enzymatic degradation
- Device design

### Formulation Approaches

- Nebulization
- Metered-dose inhalers
- Dry powder inhalers
- Soft mist inhalers

## Transdermal Delivery

### Microneedle Technology

**Types:**

- Solid microneedles
- Coated microneedles
- Dissolving microneedles
- Hollow microneedles

**Applications:**

- Peptide delivery across stratum corneum
- Controlled release
- Self-administration

### Iontophoresis

**Mechanism:**

- Electric current drives charged peptides
- Enhanced transport across skin
- Controlled delivery rate

### Sonophoresis

**Mechanism:**

- Ultrasound enhances skin permeability
- Transient pore formation
- Increased diffusion

## Targeted Delivery

### Antibody-Drug Conjugates

**Structure:**

- Peptide drug
- Linker chemistry
- Targeting antibody

**Applications:**

- Cancer therapy
- Targeted delivery to specific cells
- Reduced systemic toxicity

### Peptide-Targeted Delivery

**Approaches:**

- RGD peptides for integrin targeting
- TAT peptides for cell penetration
- Tumor-homing peptides
- Organ-specific targeting peptides

### Receptor-Mediated Delivery

**Mechanisms:**

- Transferrin receptor targeting
- Folate receptor targeting
- LDL receptor targeting
- Insulin receptor targeting

## Stability Considerations

### Chemical Stability

**Degradation Pathways:**

- Deamidation (Asn, Gln)
- Oxidation (Met, Trp, Cys)
- Racemization (especially Asp)
- Hydrolysis (Asp-Pro bonds)

**Stabilization Strategies:**

- pH optimization
- Temperature control
- Antioxidants
- Chelating agents

### Physical Stability

**Issues:**

- Aggregation
- Adsorption to surfaces
- Conformational changes

**Solutions:**

- Surfactants (polysorbates)
- Excipient optimization
- Container selection

## Formulation Development

### Pre-formulation Studies

- Solubility determination
- pH stability
- Thermal stability
- Compatibility studies

### Formulation Optimization

- Excipient screening
- Design of experiments (DoE)
- Stability testing
- Scale-up considerations

### Quality Attributes

- Purity and impurity profile
- Particle size distribution
- Encapsulation efficiency
- Release profile
- Stability

## Regulatory Considerations

### Bioequivalence

**For generic peptide drugs:**

- In vivo bioequivalence studies
- PK parameters (Cmax, AUC, Tmax)
- PD endpoints when appropriate

### Comparative Bioavailability

**For new formulations:**

- Reference product comparison
- Food effect studies
- Dose proportionality

## Summary

Peptide drug delivery technologies continue to advance, enabling oral, pulmonary, and targeted delivery of peptide therapeutics. Selection of appropriate delivery technology depends on peptide properties, therapeutic indication, and patient needs. Integration of multiple technologies may provide optimal delivery solutions.
