This guide provides detailed reconstitution protocols for therapeutic peptides, including diluent selection, volume calculations, reconstitution technique, and post-reconstitution stability data.

## Reconstitution Principles

### General Guidelines

1. **Always use aseptic technique**: Clean workspace, alcohol swabs, sterile supplies
2. **Allow lyophilized peptide to reach room temperature**: Prevents condensation
3. **Inject diluent slowly down vial wall**: Prevents foaming
4. **Do not shake**: Swirl gently to dissolve
5. **Inspect before use**: Check for particles, discoloration, precipitates
6. **Store per specific requirements**: Refrigerate or use immediately

### Diluent Selection

| Diluent | Composition | Use Case | Notes |
|---------|-------------|----------|-------|
| Bacteriostatic water | Water + 0.9% benzyl alcohol | Most peptides | Standard diluent |
| Normal saline | 0.9% NaCl | Most peptides | Alternative diluent |
| Sterile water | Water only | Sensitive peptides | Preservative-free |
| Acetic acid (dilute) | 0.2% acetic acid | Insulin formulations | For insulin |

### Equipment Needed

- **Alcohol swabs**: 70% isopropyl alcohol
- **Syringes**: Appropriate volume (1–3 mL)
- **Needles**: 18–25 gauge for reconstitution, 25–30 gauge for injection
- **Vials**: Sterile, clear glass
- **Gauze pads**: For handling vials
- **Sharps container**: For safe disposal

## Volume Calculations

### Standard Calculations

**Formula:**
```
Volume needed (mL) = Desired dose (mg or µg) / Concentration (mg/mL or µg/mL)
```

**Example 1:** Reconstitute 5 mg BPC-157 to 1 mg/mL
```
Volume = 5 mg / 1 mg/mL = 5 mL
```

**Example 2:** Reconstitute 10 mg TB-500 to 0.5 mg/mL
```
Volume = 10 mg / 0.5 mg/mL = 20 mL
```

**Example 3:** Reconstitute 2 mg CJC-1295 to 0.5 mg/mL
```
Volume = 2 mg / 0.5 mg/mL = 4 mL
```

## Reconstitution Technique

### Step-by-Step Protocol

1. **Prepare workspace**: Clean surface, gather supplies
2. **Allow vial to reach room temperature**: 5–10 minutes
3. **Clean vial stopper**: Alcohol swab, let dry
4. **Draw diluent**: Into syringe with appropriate needle
5. **Inject diluent**: Slowly down vial wall (do not pierce lyophilized cake)
6. **Remove syringe**: Do not withdraw air yet
7. **Gently swirl**: Until completely dissolved (do not shake)
8. **Inspect solution**: Clear, colorless (unless specified otherwise)
9. **Draw air**: Into syringe (to equalize pressure)
10. **Insert needle**: Through stopper
11. **Inject air**: To equalize pressure
12. **Withdraw solution**: Slowly, avoiding bubbles
13. **Change needle**: To injection needle (25–30 gauge)
14. **Remove air bubbles**: Tap syringe, push gently
15. **Administer**: Per protocol

### Common Reconstitution Errors

| Error | Consequence | Prevention |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Shaking vial | Foaming, denaturation | Gentle swirling only |
| Injecting too fast | Foaming, precipitation | Slow injection down wall |
| Using wrong diluent | Instability, precipitation | Check specifications |
| Not allowing to reach RT | Condensation, contamination | Wait 5–10 minutes |
| Drawing too much air | Difficulty withdrawing | Equalize pressure |
| Using wrong needle | Difficulty, pain | Use appropriate gauge |

### Stability Indicators

**Acceptable:**
- Clear, colorless solution (unless specified)
- No particles or precipitates
- No discoloration
- No turbidity

**Unacceptable (discard if):**
- Cloudy or turbid
- Particles or precipitates
- Discoloration (yellow, brown, pink)
- Floating material
- Strong odor

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

| Issue | Possible Cause | Solution |
|-------|----------------|----------|
| Won't dissolve | Wrong diluent, too cold | Use correct diluent, warm to RT |
| Foaming | Shaking, injecting too fast | Gentle swirl, inject slowly |
| Particulates | Contamination, precipitation | Discard, reconstitute fresh |
| Discoloration | Degradation, contamination | Discard, reconstitute fresh |
| Precipitate | Wrong diluent, temperature | Discard, reconstitute fresh |
| Difficult to withdraw | Vacuum in vial | Inject air to equalize |

### Emergency Procedures

**If injection site reaction occurs:**
1. Stop injection immediately
2. Monitor for allergic reaction
3. Seek medical attention if severe
4. Document incident

**If contamination suspected:**
1. Discard vial immediately
2. Reconstitute fresh vial
3. Use strict aseptic technique
4. Report if adverse event

## Safety Considerations

### Aseptic Technique

- Clean workspace with disinfectant
- Wash hands thoroughly
- Use sterile gloves (if available)
- Alcohol swab all vial stoppers
- Do not touch needle or syringe tip
- Use sterile supplies only

### Sharps Safety

- Use safety-engineered devices if available
- Do not recap needles
- Dispose in sharps container immediately
- Do not overfill sharps container
- Follow institutional protocols

### Waste Disposal

- **Sharps**: FDA-cleared sharps container
- **Vials**: Dispose per local regulations
- **Syringes**: Dispose in sharps container
- **Gloves/other**: Regular waste (if not contaminated)
