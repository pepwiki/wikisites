This reference provides excipient compatibility data for therapeutic peptides, including compatibility with common excipients, diluents, formulation additives, and container-closure systems.

## Compatibility Principles

### General Guidelines

1. **Always check manufacturer specifications**: Follow product-specific guidelines
2. **Conduct compatibility testing**: Before combining peptides with other substances
3. **Monitor for physical changes**: Precipitation, discoloration, particulates
4. **Monitor for chemical changes**: Degradation, potency loss
5. **Document all compatibility studies**: Maintain records

### Compatibility Categories

| Category | Description | Action |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Compatible | No interaction, stable | Use without concern |
| Conditionally compatible | Stable under specific conditions | Follow specified conditions |
| Incompatible | Interaction or instability | Avoid combination |
| Unknown | No data available | Test before use |

|---------|-------------------|-----------|-------|

### Potential Incompatibilities

| Peptide | Potential Issue | Mechanism | Recommendation |
|---------|-----------------|-----------|----------------|
| Peptides | Metal ions | Chelation, aggregation | Avoid metal contact |
| Peptides | Strong acids/bases | Hydrolysis | Maintain pH 4–8 |
| Peptides | Oxidizing agents | Oxidation | Avoid oxidizers |
| Peptides | Heat | Denaturation | Store at recommended temp |

## Formulation Development

### Preformulation Studies

| Study | Purpose | Method |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Solubility | Determine solubility profile | Shake-flask method |
| Stability | Identify degradation pathways | ICH guidelines |
| Compatibility | Screen excipients | HPLC, visual inspection |
| Characterization | Physicochemical properties | DSC, XRD, NMR |

### Excipient Screening

**Priority excipients:**
1. Buffers (phosphate, acetate, citrate)
2. Tonicity agents (NaCl, mannitol, glycerol)
3. Preservatives (benzyl alcohol, phenol)
4. Stabilizers (surfactants, sugars)
5. Antioxidants (ascorbic acid, BHT)

## Quality Control

|-----------|------|------------|
