Peptide modifications are essential for transforming native peptides into viable therapeutics. These strategies extend half-life, improve bioavailability, enhance receptor selectivity, and reduce immunogenicity.

## How to Use This Table

- **Modification**: Chemical or structural change
- **Purpose**: Therapeutic goal
- **Effect**: Impact on PK/PD properties
- **Examples**: Clinically approved or investigational peptides
- **Half-life extension**: Fold-change in serum half-life

## Half-Life Extension Strategies

### PEGylation

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Mechanism**: Covalent attachment of polyethylene glycol (PEG) chains |
| **MW range**: 2–40 kDa (typically 20–40 kDa) |
| **Half-life extension**: 10–100× |
| **Route change**: SC → weekly/monthly dosing |
| **Trade-offs**: Reduced receptor binding, increased MW, potential immunogenicity |

| PEGylated Peptide | PEG MW | Half-life | Indication | Status |
|-------------------|--------|-----------|------------|--------|
| Pegfilgrastim (Neulasta) | 20 kDa | 15–80 hours | Neutropenia | Approved |
| Certolizumab (Cimzia) | 40 kDa | 14 days | RA, Crohn's | Approved |
| Pegloticase (Krystexxa) | 10 kDa | 8–12 days | Gout | Approved |
| Pegvisomant (Somavert) | 5 kDa | 50–120 hours | Acromegaly | Approved |
| Sermorelin-PEG | Various | 2–4 hours | GH deficiency | Phase II |
| GLP-1-PEG | 20 kDa | 7 days | T2D | Investigational |

### Fatty Acid Acylation

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Mechanism**: C12–C20 fatty acid chain attachment for albumin binding |
| **MW increase**: 200–400 Da |
| **Half-life extension**: 50–500× |
| **Route change**: SC → weekly/monthly dosing |
| **Trade-offs**: May alter receptor selectivity, increased lipophilicity |

| Acylated Peptide | Fatty Acid | Half-life | Indication | Status |
|------------------|------------|-----------|------------|--------|
| Insulin detemir (Levemir) | C14 (myristic) | 12–18 hours | Diabetes | Approved |
| Liraglutide (Victoza) | C16 (palmitic) | 13 hours | T2D, obesity | Approved |
| Semaglutide (Ozempic) | C18 (diacid) | 165 hours | T2D, obesity | Approved |
| Tirzepatide (Mounjaro) | C20 (eicosanedioic) | 116 hours | T2D, obesity | Approved |
| Insulin degludec (Tresiba) | C16 (hexadecanedioic) | 42 hours | Diabetes | Approved |
| Dulaglutide (Trulicity) | IgG4 Fc fusion | 117 hours | T2D | Approved |

### Albumin Binding (Non-Acylation)

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Mechanism**: Covalent attachment to albumin or albumin-binding domain |
| **Half-life extension**: 10–100× |
| **Examples**: DAC technology (CJC-1295), PASylation |

| Peptide | Strategy | Half-life | Status |
|---------|----------|-----------|--------|
| CJC-1295 DAC | Maleimide-albumin binding | 6–8 days | Phase II |
| ALB-1211 | Albumin fusion | 14 days | Phase I |
| PASylation | Pro/Ala/Ser sequence | Variable | Investigational |

### Fragment Crystallizable (Fc) Fusion

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Mechanism**: Fusion to IgG Fc domain for FcRn-mediated recycling |
| **MW increase**: ~50 kDa |
| **Half-life extension**: 50–200× |
| **Route change**: SC → biweekly/monthly dosing |
| **Trade-offs**: Large MW, potential immunogenicity |

| Fc-Fusion Peptide | Fc Domain | Half-life | Indication | Status |
|-------------------|-----------|-----------|------------|--------|
| Etanercept (Enbrel) | IgG1 Fc | 4 days | RA, psoriasis | Approved |
| Abatacept (Orencia) | IgG1 Fc | 12 days | RA | Approved |
| Belatacept (Nulojix) | IgG1 Fc | 12 days | Transplant | Approved |
| Dulaglutide (Trulicity) | IgG4 Fc | 5 days | T2D | Approved |
| Albiglutide (Tanzeum) | Albumin + Ala | 5 days | T2D | Discontinued |

## Structural Modifications

### Cyclization

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Mechanism**: Head-to-tail, side chain-to-side chain, or disulfide cyclization |
| **Purpose**: Conformational restriction, protease resistance, receptor selectivity |
| **Types**: Lactam, disulfide, triazole, thioether, click chemistry |
| **Effect**: Improved metabolic stability, reduced conformational entropy |

| Cyclized Peptide | Cyclization Type | Effect | Example |
|------------------|------------------|--------|---------|
| Oxytocin | Disulfide (Cys1-Cys6) | Conformational constraint | Native peptide |
| Vasopressin | Disulfide (Cys1-Cys6) | Conformational constraint | Native peptide |
| Octreotide | Disulfide (Cys2-Cys7) | Conformational constraint | Approved drug |
| Lanreotide | Disulfide (Cys2-Cys7) | Conformational constraint | Approved drug |
| Melanotan II | Lactam (Asp-Lys) | Conformational constraint | Research peptide |
| Eptifibatide | Disulfide + RGD | GP IIb/IIIa selectivity | Approved drug |
| Atosiban | Disulfide (Cys1-Cys6) | OTR antagonist | Approved drug |
| Cyclic RGD | Disulfide | Integrin selectivity | Research peptide |
| Stapled peptides | Hydrocarbon staple | α-helix stabilization | Phase I |

### D-Amino Acid Substitution

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Mechanism**: Replacement of L-amino acids with D-enantiomers |
| **Purpose**: Protease resistance, conformational effects |
| **Effect**: 10–100× improved metabolic stability |
| **Trade-offs**: May alter receptor binding, immunogenicity |

| Peptide | D-AA Substitution | Effect | Example |
|---------|-------------------|--------|---------|
| Desmopressin | D-Arg at position 8 | V2R selectivity, stability | Approved drug |
| Bremelanotide | D-Phe at position 7 | MC4R selectivity | Approved drug |
| Melanotan II | D-Phe at position 7 | MC receptor activity | Research peptide |
| [D-Pen²,D-Pen⁵]-enkephalin | D-Pen (cystine analog) | δ-selectivity | Research tool |
| Dalargin | D-Ala at position 2 | Enkephalin stability | Research peptide |

### N-Methylation

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Mechanism**: Methylation of backbone amide nitrogen |
| **Purpose**: Protease resistance, conformational effects, permeability |
| **Effect**: Reduced H-bonding, improved oral bioavailability |
| **Examples**: Cyclosporin A (natural N-methylated peptide) |

| N-Methylated Peptide | N-Me Sites | Effect | Status |
|----------------------|------------|--------|--------|
| Cyclosporin A | 7 N-Me | Oral bioavailability | Approved drug |
| [Me-Ile⁷]-cyclosporin | 1 N-Me | Reduced immunosuppression | Research |
| N-methylated GLP-1 analog | Variable | Stability | Investigational |

## Stabilization Strategies

### Disulfide Bond Engineering

| Strategy | Effect | Example |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Native disulfide | Conformational constraint | Oxytocin, insulin |
| Non-native disulfide | Novel conformations | Engineered analogs |
| Selenocysteine substitution | Altered redox potential | Research tools |
| Disulfide stapling | α-helix stabilization | Stapled peptides |

### Backbone Modification

| Modification | Effect | Example |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| β-peptides | Protease resistance | Research peptides |
| Peptoids (N-substituted glycines) | Protease resistance | Research peptides |
| D-amino acids | Protease resistance | Desmopressin |
| Retro-inverso peptides | Protease resistance, conformation | Research peptides |
| α/β-hybrid peptides | Protease resistance | Research peptides |

### Terminal Modifications

| Modification | Effect | Example |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| N-terminal acetylation | Aminopeptidase resistance | Many peptides |
| C-terminal amidation | Carboxypeptidase resistance | Many peptides |
| N-terminal myristoylation | Membrane anchoring | Src family kinases |
| C-terminal thioester | Native chemical ligation | Synthetic proteins |

## Formulation and Delivery

### Microsphere Encapsulation (PLGA)

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Mechanism**: Biodegradable polymer microspheres for sustained release |
| **Release duration**: 1–6 months |
| **Examples**: Octreotide LAR, exenatide ER, risperidone Consta |
| **Trade-offs**: Burst release, degradation during encapsulation |

| PLGA-Encapsulated Peptide | Release Duration | Indication | Status |
|---------------------------|------------------|------------|--------|
| Octreotide LAR | 28 days | Acromegaly, NET | Approved |
| Exenatide ER (Bydureon) | 7 days | T2D | Approved |
| Risperidone Consta | 14 days | Schizophrenia | Approved |
| Leuprolide Lupron Depot | 1–6 months | Prostate cancer | Approved |
| Octreotide Pasireotide LAR | 28 days | Cushing disease | Approved |

### Liposomal Encapsulation

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Mechanism**: Lipid bilayer vesicles for peptide delivery |
| **Effect**: Improved stability, targeted delivery |
| **Examples**: Insulin liposomes (investigational), GLP-1 liposomes |

### Implant Devices

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Mechanism**: Non-biodegradable or biodegradable implants |
| **Release duration**: 3–12 months |
| **Examples**: Exenatide minipump, insulin pump systems |

## Selection Guide

| Goal | Recommended Strategy |
|------|---------------------|
| Extend half-life (modest) | Cyclization, D-amino acids |
| Extend half-life (significant) | PEGylation, fatty acylation, Fc fusion |
| Oral bioavailability | D-amino acids, N-methylation, backbone modification |
| Reduce immunogenicity | PEGylation, humanization |
| Targeted delivery | Fatty acylation (albumin), antibodies |
| Depot formulation | PLGA microspheres, implants |
| Conformational restriction | Cyclization, stapling |
