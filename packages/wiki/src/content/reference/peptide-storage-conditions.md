This reference provides comprehensive storage conditions for therapeutic peptides, including lyophilized and reconstituted forms, temperature requirements, light sensitivity, and stability data.

## Storage Principles

### General Guidelines

- **Lyophilized peptides**: Store at −20°C to −80°C
- **Reconstituted peptides**: Use within 30 days (unless otherwise specified)
- **Room temperature storage**: Only for specifically formulated peptides
- **Light protection**: Protect from light (amber vials or foil wrapping)
- **Humidity control**: Keep dry (desiccant packs)
- **Freeze-thaw cycles**: Minimize (aliquot if repeated use)

### Temperature Ranges

| Category | Temperature | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Room temperature | 20–25°C | For stable formulations |
| Refrigerated | 2–8°C | Standard storage |
| Frozen | −20°C | Long-term storage |
| Ultra-frozen | −80°C | For sensitive peptides |

## Reconstitution Guidelines

### General Reconstitution

**Procedure:**
1. Allow lyophilized peptide to reach room temperature
2. Clean vial stopper with alcohol swab
3. Draw appropriate diluent into syringe
4. Inject diluent slowly down vial wall
5. Gently swirl (do not shake)
6. Allow to dissolve completely (5–10 minutes)
7. Inspect for particles or discoloration
8. Store per specific peptide requirements

### Diluent Selection

| Diluent | Use Case | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Bacteriostatic water | Most peptides | Standard diluent |
| Normal saline | Most peptides | Alternative diluent |
| Sterile water | Sensitive peptides | Preservative-free |
| Acetic acid (dilute) | Insulin | For insulin formulations |

## Stability Testing

### ICH Guidelines

**Accelerated stability testing:**
- 40°C ± 2°C / 75% RH ± 5% for 6 months
- 30°C ± 2°C / 65% RH ± 5% for 6 months

**Long-term stability testing:**
- 25°C ± 2°C / 60% RH ± 5% for 36 months
- 2–8°C for 36 months

### Stability Indicators

| Parameter | Test Method | Acceptance Criteria |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------|
| Purity | HPLC | ≥95% |
| Potency | Bioassay | ≥90% of labeled potency |
| Moisture | Karl Fischer | ≤3% |
| pH | Potentiometry | Within specification |
| Particulate | Light obscuration | Meets USP <788> |
| Sterility | Membrane filtration | Sterile |
| Endotoxin | LAL test | <5 EU/mg |

## Transport and Shipping

### Cold Chain Requirements

| Category | Temperature | Packaging |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Standard peptides | 2–8°C | Insulated box with ice packs |
| Frozen peptides | −20°C to −80°C | Dry ice |
| Room temp peptides | 20–25°C | Ambient packaging |

### Shipping Guidelines

- **Documentation**: Include COA, SDS, and storage instructions
- **Packaging**: Use validated cold chain packaging
- **Tracking**: Monitor temperature during transit
- **Receiving**: Inspect upon arrival, store immediately
- **Documentation**: Record temperature excursion if any

## Waste Disposal

### Expired/Unused Peptides

- **FDA-approved**: Follow local regulations for pharmaceutical waste
- **Investigational**: Follow institutional guidelines
- **Reconstituted**: Discard after specified time
- **Needles/syringes**: Use sharps container

### Environmental Considerations

- **Do not flush**: Do not flush down toilet or drain
- **Incineration**: Most peptides require incineration
- **Pharmaceutical waste**: Use licensed waste disposal
- **Documentation**: Maintain disposal records
