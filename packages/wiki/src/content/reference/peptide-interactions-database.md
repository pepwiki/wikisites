This database compiles known interactions between therapeutic peptides and other medications, supplements, and foods. Interactions are classified by clinical significance (major, moderate, minor) and include mechanisms, evidence levels, and management recommendations.

## How to Use This Database

- **Severity**: Major (avoid), Moderate (monitor), Minor (aware)
- **Mechanism**: Pharmacokinetic (PK) or pharmacodynamic (PD)
- **Evidence**: Level A (multiple studies), Level B (limited data), Level C (case reports)
- **Action**: Avoid, monitor, adjust dose, or no action needed

## Clinical Significance Summary

### Major Interactions (Avoid)

1. **Insulin + oral hypoglycemics**: Severe hypoglycemia risk
2. **MT-II + sympathomimetics**: Cardiovascular events
3. **Oxytocin + prostaglandins**: Uterine hyperstimulation
4. **PT-141 + nitrates**: Severe hypoglycemia
5. **Insulin + alcohol**: Hypoglycemia

### Moderate Interactions (Monitor)

1. **GLP-1 RAs + warfarin**: INR changes
2. **GH + corticosteroids**: Reduced efficacy
3. **Insulin + beta-blockers**: Masked hypoglycemia
4. **Oral semaglutide + food**: Altered absorption
5. **Carbetocin + other uterotonics**: Additive effects

### Minor Interactions (Aware)

1. **GLP-1 RAs + metformin**: Additive GI effects
2. **GH + supplements**: Minimal interactions
3. **Peptides + minerals**: Competition for absorption
4. **Insulin + supplements**: Minimal interactions

## Monitoring Recommendations

### High-Risk Interactions

- **Frequent glucose monitoring**: Insulin combinations
- **INR monitoring**: Warfarin combinations
- **Blood pressure monitoring**: Cardiovascular peptides
- **Drug level monitoring**: Narrow therapeutic index drugs
- **ECG monitoring**: Cardiovascular risk

### Moderate-Risk Interactions

- **Periodic glucose monitoring**: Insulin combinations
- **Clinical assessment**: Efficacy changes
- **Side effect monitoring**: Additive effects
- **Lab monitoring**: Hepatic/renal function

### Low-Risk Interactions

- **Patient education**: Awareness
- **Symptom reporting**: Adverse effects
- **Routine monitoring**: Standard care

## Special Populations

### Elderly Patients

- **Increased sensitivity**: All peptide-drug interactions
- **Polypharmacy risk**: Multiple interactions
- **Renal impairment**: Altered clearance
- **Hepatic impairment**: Altered metabolism

### Pediatric Patients

- **Growth effects**: GH interactions
- **Metabolic effects**: Insulin interactions
- **Developmental considerations**: Neuroactive peptides
- **Weight-based dosing**: All interactions

### Pregnant Patients

- **Teratogenicity**: Most peptides contraindicated
- **Uterotonic effects**: Oxytocin, carbetocin
- **Metabolic changes**: Insulin requirements
- **Placental transfer**: Most peptides cross placenta

### Renal Impairment

- **Reduced clearance**: Longer half-life
- **Accumulation risk**: Dose reduction needed
- **Electrolyte effects**: Altered by renal function
- **Drug interactions**: Altered by renal function

## Evidence Levels

### Level A (Strong Evidence)

- Multiple randomized controlled trials
- Consistent results across studies
- Large patient populations
- FDA-approved labeling

### Level B (Moderate Evidence)

- Limited clinical trials
- Observational studies
- Pharmacokinetic studies
- Expert consensus

### Level C (Weak Evidence)

- Case reports
- In vitro studies
- Theoretical interactions
- Limited clinical experience
