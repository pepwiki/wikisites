## 1. Executive Summary

This document catalogues every identified conflict between applicable standards, specifies resolution strategies, and establishes governance for future conflict discovery. Ten conflicts have been identified during Phase 0 requirements engineering. Each conflict is analyzed for root cause, impact, and resolution with explicit ADR references and residual risk quantification.

Conflicts arise because the Wikisites project simultaneously pursues compliance with legal/regulatory mandates (WCAG, GDPR, CCPA), industry best practices (OWASP, Core Web Vitals), professional standards (IEEE, ISO), and domain-specific conventions (IUPAC nomenclature, Schema.org). These standards were developed independently and sometimes impose contradictory requirements on the same design or implementation decisions.

## 2. Conflict Catalog

### CONFLICT-001: WCAG 2.1 AA vs. Aesthetic Design

| Field                     | Value                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Conflict ID**           | CONFLICT-001                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| **Conflicting Standards** | WCAG 2.1 Level AA (Clauses 1.4.3 Contrast Minimum, 1.4.11 Non-text Contrast, 1.4.1 Use of Color, 2.4.7 Focus Visible) vs. Brand Identity Guidelines (Section 5.2 Design System Specification)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| **Nature of Conflict**    | Artistic design choices for visual identity may conflict with accessibility requirements. Specific manifestations: (a) Decorative text in brand colors may fall below 4.5:1 contrast ratio for normal text or 3:1 for large text. The wikipept.com Teal (#0097A7) on White (#FFFFFF) combination yields a contrast ratio of approximately 3.0:1 — below the 4.5:1 WCAG AA threshold for normal text. (b) Complex animations (molecular viewer transitions, flashcard flip effects, page transitions) may trigger vestibular disorders or seizure risks if not properly controlled. (c) Low-contrast decorative borders, dividers, or background patterns may fail non-text contrast requirements. (d) Focus indicators styled to match brand aesthetics may lack sufficient visibility against page backgrounds. (e) Color-only state indicators (error/success/warning) violate 1.4.1 Use of Color without supplementary cues.                                                           |
| **Impact Analysis**       | **Legal:** WCAG 2.1 AA is a Tier 1 mandatory standard. Non-compliance exposes both sites to litigation under ADA Title III, Section 508, and EN 301 549. Lawsuit frequency for web accessibility violations has increased 300% since 2020. **User:** 15-20% of the global population has some form of disability. Excluding these users from an educational platform contradicts the project's educational mission. **SEO:** Google uses accessibility signals in ranking algorithms. **Brand:** Accessibility failures generate negative press and community backlash. **Severity: CRITICAL.**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| **Resolution Strategy**   | Apply the priority matrix: WCAG 2.1 AA takes precedence over aesthetic preferences. **Resolution actions:** (a) Adjust wikipept.com Teal to #00796B (darker teal) which yields 4.7:1 contrast ratio against white, or use Teal #0097A7 only for large text (>=18pt) and interactive elements where 3:1 is sufficient. (b) All animations must respect `prefers-reduced-motion` media query. Flashcard flips and page transitions use `transition: none` when reduced motion is active. (c) Decorative elements use contrast ratios >=3:1 against adjacent colors. (d) Focus indicators use a 2px solid outline in a high-contrast color (#005F73 or #000000) distinct from page background regardless of brand palette. (e) All state indicators use color + icon + text (triple redundancy). **Design token adjustments:** Update `--color-primary` for wikipept.com from #0097A7 to #00796B for text on white backgrounds; reserve #0097A7 for large text and decorative elements only. |
| **ADR Reference**         | ADR-001 (pending: Accessibility-First Design Token Strategy)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| **Residual Risk**         | LOW — After color adjustments and motion controls, residual risk is limited to edge cases in dynamically generated content (community edits, user-uploaded images) where contrast must be enforced via automated CI/CD checks (axe-core). Risk mitigated by automated contrast testing in build pipeline.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |

### CONFLICT-002: GDPR Cookie Consent vs. Performance

| Field                     | Value                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Conflict ID**           | CONFLICT-002                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **Conflicting Standards** | GDPR Article 7 (Conditions for consent), ePrivacy Directive Article 5(3) (Cookie consent requirement) vs. Core Web Vitals (CLS <=0.1, LCP <=2.5s), Google Lighthouse Performance scoring                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| **Nature of Conflict**    | Cookie consent banners, when implemented as client-side JavaScript widgets, impact Core Web Vitals in three ways: (a) **CLS impact:** Cookie banners that appear after initial render push existing content down, causing layout shifts. A banner appearing 500ms after page load can contribute 0.1-0.3 CLS, exceeding the 0.1 threshold. (b) **LCP impact:** Cookie banners that overlay or shift the largest contentful element can delay or obscure LCP measurement. If the banner itself becomes the LCP, it degrades perceived load quality. (c) **INP impact:** Cookie consent management platform (CMP) JavaScript executes on every page load, adding 50-200ms of main thread blocking time, degrading Interaction to Next Paint. (d) **Additional payload:** Most CMP scripts add 30-80KB of JavaScript, increasing total page weight and parse time.                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| **Impact Analysis**       | **Legal:** GDPR Article 7 requires freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent for non-essential cookies. ePrivacy Directive Article 5(3) requires consent before storing cookies. Non-compliance risks fines up to EUR 20M or 4% of annual turnover. **Performance:** CLS >0.1 is a failing Core Web Vitals score, directly impacting Google Search ranking. LCP >2.5s triggers the same. **User Experience:** Delayed content shift frustrates users. **Severity: HIGH.**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| **Resolution Strategy**   | Minimize the performance impact of cookie consent through architectural choices: (a) **Use Cloudflare Web Analytics as primary analytics.** Cloudflare Web Analytics is cookie-free and GDPR-compliant by design — no cookie consent banner is required. This eliminates the primary reason for cookie consent on 90%+ of page views. (b) **Defer Plausible Analytics to post-consent.** If Plausible is added later as a fallback, load it only after explicit consent via a client-side consent state check. (c) **Static cookie consent banner.** If a banner is required (e.g., for future third-party integrations), implement it as a static HTML element in the page template (not injected via JavaScript). The banner occupies a reserved CSS grid slot with `min-height` set, preventing CLS. (d) **Service Worker caching.** Cache the consent state in localStorage to avoid re-prompting on return visits. (e) **Reject-all as prominent as accept-all.** GDPR requires equal prominence for reject and accept options — design the banner with equal-weight buttons. |
| **ADR Reference**         | ADR-002 (pending: Cookie-Free Analytics Architecture)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| **Residual Risk**         | LOW — With Cloudflare Web Analytics as the sole analytics provider, no cookie consent banner is needed. Residual risk exists only if future requirements mandate additional tracking (e.g., A/B testing, marketing pixels), which would require re-evaluation. Risk mitigated by architectural decision to use privacy-first analytics from inception.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |

### CONFLICT-003: Schema.org Markup vs. Privacy

| Field                     | Value                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Conflict ID**           | CONFLICT-003                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| **Conflicting Standards** | Schema.org Structured Data Vocabulary (ScholarlyArticle, LearningResource, Person, aggregateRating, interactionStatistic) vs. GDPR Article 5(1)(c) (Data Minimisation), Article 6 (Lawfulness of processing), CCPA Section 1798.100 (Right to know)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| **Nature of Conflict**    | Structured data markup may expose personal information beyond what is necessary or consented: (a) **Contributor Person schema.** wikipept.com community contributor profiles using Schema.org `Person` type expose usernames, avatars, and contribution counts to search engines and structured data consumers. This constitutes personal data under GDPR if the username is linkable to an individual. (b) **aggregateRating and interactionStatistic.** Community ratings and interaction counts (e.g., quiz attempts, flashcard reviews) may be traceable to individual users if the data granularity is exposed via API. (c) **Author attribution on encyclopeptide.com.** ScholarlyArticle author names are public academic information, but combining them with institutional affiliation and email in structured data could exceed data minimisation principles. (d) **Citation data.** DOI-linked citations expose academic collaboration networks. (e) **Learning progress.** If `educationalLevel` or `assesses` properties are dynamically generated per-user, they constitute personal learning data subject to GDPR.                                                            |
| **Impact Analysis**       | **Legal:** GDPR Article 5(1)(c) requires that personal data be adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary. Exposing personal data in structured data that is indexed by search engines and consumed by third-party applications creates a data processing activity that requires lawful basis. CCPA requires disclosure of personal information categories shared with third parties (structured data consumers). **Privacy:** Users may not expect their learning progress or contribution history to be indexed by search engines. **Severity: MEDIUM.**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| **Resolution Strategy**   | (a) **No per-user structured data.** Schema.org markup on wikipept.com uses aggregate statistics only — never per-user learning data. `aggregateRating` shows site-wide averages, not individual scores. `interactionStatistic` shows total counts, not per-user activity. (b) **Contributor attribution uses pseudonymous data.** Community contributors are referenced by display name only (not email, real name, or IP). Display name choice is the contributor's responsibility. GDPR privacy notice discloses that display names appear in structured data. (c) **encyclopeptide.com author data uses public academic information only.** Author names and institutional affiliations are taken from published papers (already public). No private email addresses in structured data. (d) **Structured data reviewed before deployment.** CI/CD pipeline includes a structured data audit step that flags any personal data patterns (email regex, IP patterns, phone numbers) in JSON-LD output. (e) **DPA with search engines.** Google and other structured data consumers are recognized as data controllers for indexed personal data. Privacy notice discloses this processing. |
| **ADR Reference**         | ADR-003 (pending: Privacy-Safe Structured Data Strategy)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| **Residual Risk**         | LOW — With aggregate-only statistics and pseudonymous contributor attribution, the residual risk is limited to the inherent public nature of contributor display names. Risk mitigated by GDPR privacy notice disclosure and contributor consent at registration.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |

### CONFLICT-004: Security Headers vs. Third-Party Scripts

| Field                     | Value                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Conflict ID**           | CONFLICT-004                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| **Conflicting Standards** | NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 SC-7 (Boundary Protection), SC-8 (Transmission Confidentiality and Integrity), SI-10 (Information Input Validation) / OWASP Top 10 A05:2021 (Security Misconfiguration) / CSP Level 3 vs. Third-Party Script Dependencies (Cloudflare Web Analytics, potential Plausible Analytics, potential future embeds)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| **Nature of Conflict**    | Strict Content Security Policy (CSP) headers protect against XSS, code injection, and data exfiltration, but third-party scripts require CSP exceptions that weaken the policy: (a) **Script-src directives.** Analytics scripts (Cloudflare Web Analytics beacon, Plausible) require explicit `script-src` allowlisting. Each allowed domain expands the attack surface — a compromised analytics CDN could inject malicious scripts. (b) **Connect-src directives.** Analytics beacons require outbound connections to their respective endpoints (e.g., `https://static.cloudflareinsights.com`). Each allowed endpoint is a potential data exfiltration vector. (c) **img-src directives.** Tracking pixels and analytics beacons may load via image requests to third-party domains. (d) **Frame-src for embeds.** If YouTube videos, molecular viewer embeds, or social media widgets are used, each requires frame-src exceptions. (e) **Nonce management.** CSP nonces for inline scripts must be regenerated per-request, complicating static site deployment on Cloudflare Pages where edge caching may serve stale nonces.                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| **Impact Analysis**       | **Security:** OWASP A05:2021 lists Security Misconfiguration as a top-10 risk. A weak CSP is a misconfiguration. NIST SP 800-53 SC-7 requires boundary protection that CSP enforces. Each CSP exception reduces the effectiveness of this control. **XSS Risk:** Community-editable content on wikipept.com creates XSS vectors that CSP is designed to mitigate. Weakened CSP from third-party script allowlisting reduces protection. **Compliance:** NIST SP 800-53 and ISO 27001 Annex A.8 require security controls that CSP directly implements. **Severity: CRITICAL.**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| **Resolution Strategy**   | Minimize third-party script surface while maintaining functionality: (a) **First-party analytics proxy.** Route analytics through a Cloudflare Worker that proxies requests to analytics endpoints. The CSP only allows `script-src 'self'` and `connect-src 'self'`. The Worker handles outbound connections to analytics, so the browser never contacts third-party domains directly. (b) **Cloudflare Web Analytics via Rules.** Cloudflare's built-in Web Analytics can be injected at the edge via Cloudflare Rules (Transform Rules or Workers), not via client-side JavaScript. This eliminates the need for a `<script>` tag entirely, removing the CSP conflict. (c) **Strict CSP baseline.** Default CSP: `default-src 'self'; script-src 'self'; style-src 'self' 'unsafe-inline'; img-src 'self' data: https:; connect-src 'self'; font-src 'self'; object-src 'none'; frame-src 'none'; base-uri 'self'; form-action 'self';`. (d) **Third-party script audit.** Any new third-party script requires a security review and CSP exception request documented in an ADR. (e) **Subresource Integrity (SRI).** All external resources loaded via `<link>` or `<script>` tags include SRI hashes. (f) **Signed HTTP Exchanges.** For future content syndication, use signed exchanges to maintain origin integrity. |
| **ADR Reference**         | ADR-004 (pending: First-Party Analytics Proxy Architecture)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| **Residual Risk**         | MEDIUM — The first-party analytics proxy adds architectural complexity and a potential single point of failure for analytics data collection. If the proxy Worker fails, analytics data is lost (but site functionality is unaffected). Risk mitigated by Cloudflare Workers' 99.99% uptime SLA and graceful degradation (analytics failure is non-critical).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |

### CONFLICT-005: i18n URL Structure vs. SEO

| Field                     | Value                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Conflict ID**           | CONFLICT-005                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| **Conflicting Standards** | I18n URL Structure Best Practices (locale-prefixed paths: /en/, /zh/, /ru/) vs. SEO Best Practices (canonical URL management, duplicate content prevention, hreflang implementation, URL simplicity)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| **Nature of Conflict**    | Localized URL structures create SEO complexities: (a) **Duplicate content risk.** The same English content accessible at both `/angiotensin-ii` and `/en/angiotensin-ii` creates duplicate content unless proper canonical tags and redirects are configured. (b) **Hreflang complexity.** Each page requires `<link rel="alternate" hreflang="x">` tags for every supported locale, plus `hreflang="x-default"` for the fallback. With 6 languages and 500+ pages, this generates 3,000+ hreflang tags across the site — increasing HTML payload and crawl budget consumption. (c) **URL path localization.** Some locales may benefit from translated URL slugs (e.g., `/de/peptid-bindungen` instead of `/de/peptide-bonds`), but this creates maintenance burden and redirect complexity. (d) **Sitemap fragmentation.** Multi-language sitemaps require either a single sitemap with all locale variants (inflating sitemap size) or per-locale sitemaps (increasing crawl management complexity). (e) **Search engine locale matching.** Google may serve the wrong locale version to users based on IP/ language settings, leading to mismatched content-language expectations.      |
| **Impact Analysis**       | **SEO:** Duplicate content can dilute page authority across locale variants. Incorrect hreflang implementation can cause Google to ignore language targeting entirely. Crawl budget waste on duplicate pages reduces indexing of unique content. **Maintenance:** Managing hreflang tags across thousands of pages requires automated tooling. **User Experience:** Users may land on wrong-language content if locale detection fails. **Severity: MEDIUM.**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| **Resolution Strategy**   | (a) **Single canonical locale prefix.** All content is canonical under its locale prefix: `/en/angiotensin-ii` is canonical. The unprefixed `/angiotensin-ii` redirects (301) to `/en/angiotensin-ii`. No duplicate content. (b) **Automated hreflang generation.** Astro's i18n integration generates hreflang tags automatically from the file-based routing structure. Each page's layout component includes a `getAlternateLinks()` function that queries all locale variants of the current page. (c) **English-only URL slugs.** All locale variants use English slugs (`/angiotensin-ii` not `/zh/angiotensin-ii` with translated slug). This simplifies cross-locale routing, reduces redirect chains, and maintains URL stability. Scientific terms are invariant across locales per IUPAC standards. (d) **Per-locale sitemaps.** Generate separate sitemaps per locale (`sitemap-en.xml`, `sitemap-zh.xml`, etc.) referenced in a sitemap index. Each sitemap contains only that locale's URLs with `xhtml:link` hreflang annotations. (e) **x-default points to English.** `hreflang="x-default"` always points to the English version as the fallback for unsupported locales. |
| **ADR Reference**         | ADR-005 (pending: i18n URL Canonicalization Strategy)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **Residual Risk**         | LOW — With canonical locale prefixes and automated hreflang, residual risk is limited to Google's locale detection accuracy (IP-based, language settings). Risk mitigated by proper `hreflang="x-default"` and consistent locale prefixes.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |

### CONFLICT-006: Static Generation vs. Real-Time Data

| Field                     | Value                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Conflict ID**           | CONFLICT-006                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| **Conflicting Standards** | Astro Static Generation / Cloudflare Pages Static Hosting (zero-JS default, edge-cached HTML, build-time rendering) vs. Oligopeptide Data Requirements (real-time database integrations, dynamic pharmacokinetic data, live community content, user-specific learning progress)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| **Nature of Conflict**    | The Astro static-first architecture fundamentally conflicts with dynamic data requirements: (a) **Peptide data freshness.** Reference databases (UniProt, PDB, ChEMBL) update continuously. Static builds produce point-in-time snapshots. A new PDB structure deposited after the last build is invisible until the next deployment. (b) **Community content.** wikipept.com's wiki-style editing requires real-time or near-real-time content updates. Static generation cannot reflect community edits made after the last build. (c) **User-specific data.** Learning progress, quiz scores, flashcard schedules, and personalized recommendations are inherently per-user and dynamic. Static pages cannot display user-specific content without client-side rendering or edge-side rendering. (d) **Search index freshness.** Pagefind generates a static search index at build time. New content added between builds is unsearchable. (e) **Build time scaling.** As content grows (target: 500+ monographs, 1000+ study guides), static build times may exceed Cloudflare Pages build limits (typically 15-30 minutes depending on plan).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| **Impact Analysis**       | **Content Accuracy:** Outdated peptide data in a scientific reference resource undermines credibility and educational mission. **User Experience:** Community edits not appearing immediately discourages contributor participation. **Scalability:** Build times that grow linearly with content volume create a hard ceiling on site growth. **SEO:** Stale content ranks lower in search results for time-sensitive queries. **Severity: CRITICAL.**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **Resolution Strategy**   | Adopt a hybrid rendering strategy using Astro's `output: 'hybrid'` mode: (a) **Static baseline.** All monograph pages (encyclopeptide.com) and study guide pages (wikipept.com) are statically generated at build time. These are the content-heavy, read-mostly pages that benefit from edge caching and zero-JS rendering. (b) **Server-rendered dynamic routes.** wikipept.com uses `output: 'hybrid'` with server-rendered routes for: user dashboard (`/dashboard`), community edits (`/edit/*`), quiz submission (`/api/quiz/*`), progress tracking (`/api/progress/*`), and user authentication (`/api/auth/*`). These are deployed as Cloudflare Pages Functions. (c) **Incremental Static Regeneration (ISR) for encyclopeptide.com.** Use Astro's ISR capabilities (or Cloudflare Workers with cache invalidation) to revalidate monograph pages periodically (e.g., every 24 hours) by fetching fresh data from external APIs. (d) **KV-backed content updates.** Community edits are stored in D1 (relational data) and KV (cached content). A Cloudflare Worker revalidates the static cache for edited pages within 60 seconds of the edit. (e) **Progressive search index.** Combine Pagefind (static build-time index for content pages) with a Cloudflare Workers-backed search API (for dynamically indexed community content). (f) **Build optimization.** Implement content-aware incremental builds (only rebuild pages whose source MDX files changed). |
| **ADR Reference**         | ADR-006 (pending: Hybrid Static-Dynamic Rendering Architecture)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| **Residual Risk**         | MEDIUM — The hybrid architecture adds complexity: two rendering modes, cache invalidation logic, and Worker-side data fetching. Cache staleness risk exists for ISR-revalidated pages (up to 24-hour delay for encyclopeptide.com). Risk mitigated by manual cache purge capability in Cloudflare dashboard and content update notifications via webhooks.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |

### CONFLICT-007: Community Content vs. Scientific Accuracy

| Field                     | Value                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Conflict ID**           | CONFLICT-007                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| **Conflicting Standards** | Open Collaboration Principles (wiki-style editing, community contributions, low barrier to entry) vs. Scientific Accuracy Requirements (peer review, mandatory citations, data verification, IUPAC nomenclature compliance) / ISO/IEC 12207:2017 Clause 10.4 (Quality Assurance)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| **Nature of Conflict**    | The tension between open community editing and scientific accuracy is inherent to wikipept.com's mission: (a) **Edit quality variance.** Community contributors range from domain experts to enthusiastic beginners. Edits may introduce factual errors, outdated information, or misinterpretations of primary literature. (b) **Citation requirements vs. accessibility.** Requiring DOI citations for every claim creates a high barrier to entry for non-academic contributors (students, science enthusiasts). Too few citations allow misinformation. Too many deter participation. (c) **Nomenclature compliance.** IUPAC nomenclature, amino acid codes, and chemical formulas must be precise. Community editors may use informal or incorrect notation. (d) **Vandalism and bad-faith edits.** Open editing systems are vulnerable to deliberate misinformation, particularly concerning for a scientific resource. (e) **Temporal accuracy.** Scientific understanding evolves. Content that was accurate at publication may become outdated. Community edits may reference preprint findings that are later retracted.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| **Impact Analysis**       | **Credibility:** Scientific inaccuracies on an educational platform undermine trust and can propagate misinformation. **Liability:** Incorrect pharmacological data could influence student understanding or, in worst cases, research decisions. **Community Trust:** If bad edits persist, expert contributors disengage. **Educational Mission:** Students learning from inaccurate material receive deficient education. **Severity: LOW** (managed through editorial workflow, not a standards conflict per se, but impacts quality assurance standard compliance).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| **Resolution Strategy**   | Implement a tiered editorial trust model: (a) **Reputation-weighted trust.** Contributors earn reputation through verified accurate edits. Higher reputation = more editing privileges and faster publication. New contributors' edits enter a review queue. (b) **Pending changes for new contributors.** First 10 edits by new contributors require moderator approval before publication. After 10 approved edits, the contributor earns "trusted editor" status with direct publication. (c) **Expert review queue.** Pages tagged as "high-traffic" or "clinical" require expert review before changes are published, regardless of contributor reputation. (d) **Citation tiers.** Define three citation tiers: Tier 1 (DOI-linked primary source) for quantitative claims, Tier 2 (textbook or review article) for general explanations, Tier 3 (community knowledge with inline links) for mnemonics and learning tips. Each content section specifies its minimum citation tier. (e) **Automated quality checks.** CI/CD pipeline runs automated checks on community edits: IUPAC nomenclature validation, amino acid code verification, broken link detection, and contrast/accessibility checks for embedded media. (f) **Version history and rollback.** All edits are version-controlled with full diff visualization. One-click rollback to any previous version. (g) **Misinformation flagging.** Community members can flag content as "potentially inaccurate" with a reason. Flagged content enters a priority review queue and displays a warning banner. |
| **ADR Reference**         | ADR-007 (pending: Tiered Community Editorial Trust Model)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| **Residual Risk**         | LOW — The tiered trust model with expert review for high-impact content, automated quality checks, and version control/rollback provides defense-in-depth. Residual risk is limited to the rare case where a plausible-sounding but incorrect edit passes all automated checks and is reviewed by a non-expert moderator. Risk mitigated by periodic expert audit of high-traffic pages.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |

### CONFLICT-008: Mobile Performance vs. Rich Interactivity

| Field                     | Value                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Conflict ID**           | CONFLICT-008                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| **Conflicting Standards** | Core Web Vitals Mobile Thresholds (LCP <=2.5s, CLS <=0.1, INP <=200ms on mobile 4G throttled) / Performance Budget (<=500KB initial page weight) vs. Interactive Feature Requirements (3D molecular viewer with WebGL, quiz engine with state management, flashcard system with animations, wiki editor with live preview)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| **Nature of Conflict**    | Rich interactive features consume JavaScript bundle size and CPU time that directly conflict with mobile performance budgets: (a) **3D molecular viewer.** Mol\*/NGL Viewer libraries are 200-500KB gzipped. On a mobile device on throttled 4G, loading and initializing a molecular viewer can take 3-5 seconds, exceeding the 2.5s LCP threshold. (b) **Quiz engine.** SolidJS quiz component with state management, answer validation, scoring, and spaced repetition scheduling adds 15-30KB gzipped. While small, it contributes to cumulative bundle size on pages with multiple interactive islands. (c) **Flashcard system.** Flip animations, spaced repetition algorithms (FSRS), and local storage for review schedules add 10-20KB gzipped plus CSS animation overhead. (d) **Wiki editor.** Markdown editor with live preview, syntax highlighting, and diff visualization adds 50-100KB gzipped. (e) **Cumulative JS budget.** With multiple islands per page (e.g., a study guide with embedded quiz + flashcard deck + molecular viewer), total JavaScript can exceed 500KB gzipped, far beyond the performance budget. (f) **CPU time.** Mobile devices have 2-4x slower JavaScript execution than desktop. INP threshold of 200ms is easily exceeded by complex quiz evaluation or molecular viewer rotation on low-end devices.                                                                                         |
| **Impact Analysis**       | **Performance:** Exceeding Core Web Vitals thresholds on mobile directly impacts Google Search ranking and user experience. **User Reach:** Mobile-first educational access is a project goal. Users on low-end devices in developing countries (a key audience for free educational resources) are disproportionately affected. **Accessibility:** Poor mobile performance disproportionately affects users with older devices and slower connections, intersecting with disability and socioeconomic accessibility. **Severity: HIGH.**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| **Resolution Strategy**   | Apply aggressive performance optimization for mobile: (a) **Lazy loading with `client:visible`.** 3D molecular viewers use `client:visible` directive — they only hydrate when scrolled into viewport. This eliminates the viewer from initial page load. (b) **2D structural fallback by default.** On mobile viewports (<=768px), display a static 2D structural diagram by default. 3D viewer is available on-demand via explicit user tap on "View 3D Structure" button. (c) **Bundle splitting per island.** Each interactive island is independently code-split. The quiz island's code is only loaded on pages with quizzes. The flashcard island's code is only loaded on pages with flashcard decks. (d) **Service Worker pre-caching.** After first page load, the Service Worker pre-caches interactive component bundles for subsequent pages. Second-page-load interactive features appear instantly. (e) **Offscreen `client:idle`.** Non-critical interactive features (comparison tables, citation export) use `client:idle` to hydrate during browser idle time. (f) **Mobile-specific budgets.** Lighthouse CI runs with mobile throttling. Performance budgets are enforced: static pages must ship 0KB JS, interactive pages must stay under 200KB JS (not 500KB). (g) **Web Worker offloading.** Quiz evaluation and spaced repetition scheduling run in Web Workers to avoid blocking the main thread, improving INP. |
| **ADR Reference**         | ADR-008 (pending: Mobile-First Interactive Island Strategy)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| **Residual Risk**         | MEDIUM — The 2D fallback for molecular viewers means mobile users get a degraded experience for 3D structural exploration. Risk mitigated by ensuring the 2D fallback is informative (clear structural diagram with key measurements) and the 3D viewer remains available on-demand. The Service Worker pre-caching requires a first-load performance penalty that may affect first-time visitors.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |

### CONFLICT-009: Multi-lingual Content vs. Build Time

| Field                     | Value                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Conflict ID**           | CONFLICT-009                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **Conflicting Standards** | Multi-lingual Content Requirements (6 languages: EN, ZH, RU, DE, FR, JP; complete translation of 500+ monographs and 1000+ study guides) vs. Build Performance (Cloudflare Pages build time limits, developer iteration speed, CI/CD pipeline throughput) / ISO/IEC 12207:2017 Clause 9.5 (Software Construction)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| **Nature of Conflict**    | Multi-lingual static generation multiplies build complexity: (a) **Build time multiplication.** Each page must be rendered 6 times (once per locale). 500 monographs x 6 locales = 3,000 static pages for encyclopeptide.com alone. 1,000 study guides x 6 locales = 6,000 pages for wikipept.com. Total: 9,000+ pages at build time. (b) **Translation file loading.** Loading and parsing 6 sets of locale files (JSON/YAML) with 500+ terms each adds to build time. (c) **Search index multiplication.** Pagefind must index content in 6 languages, including CJK text segmentation for Chinese and Japanese (which requires special tokenization). (d) **Build output size.** 9,000+ HTML pages at an average of 50KB each = 450MB of build output. This approaches or exceeds Cloudflare Pages' build output limits on some plans. (e) **CI/CD feedback loop.** Developers iterating on a single page must wait for the full multi-locale build to complete, slowing development velocity. (f) **Translation synchronization.** Detecting which translated pages are out-of-date with their English source requires diffing across 6 locale directories.                                                                    |
| **Impact Analysis**       | **Developer Experience:** Slow builds reduce iteration speed and increase frustration. **CI/CD:** Build times exceeding 15-30 minutes risk timeout failures on Cloudflare Pages. **Cost:** Longer builds consume more CI/CD minutes. **Content Freshness:** If builds are slow, content updates are deployed less frequently. **Severity: MEDIUM.**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| **Resolution Strategy**   | Optimize the build pipeline for multi-lingual scaling: (a) **Incremental builds.** Only rebuild pages whose source files (MDX or locale JSON) changed since the last build. Astro's content layer tracking detects changed files and rebuilds only affected pages. (b) **Parallel locale generation.** Build each locale in parallel using CI/CD matrix jobs. Six parallel builds complete in roughly the time of one. (c) **Deferred locale builds.** The CI/CD pipeline builds the English locale on every push (fast feedback). Other locales are built nightly or on-demand, not on every commit. (d) **CDN cache warming.** After a partial build (English only), Cloudflare Pages serves the new English content immediately while other locale builds complete in the background. (e) **Translation diff tooling.** Build a custom script that compares English source frontmatter `dateModified` against translated page `dateModified` to identify stale translations. Stale pages display a "This translation may be outdated" banner. (f) **Build output optimization.** Use Brotli compression on Cloudflare Pages to reduce build output transfer time. Implement aggressive HTML minification in the build pipeline. |
| **ADR Reference**         | ADR-009 (pending: Incremental Multi-lingual Build Strategy)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| **Residual Risk**         | LOW — Incremental builds and deferred locale generation reduce build time to near-single-locale levels for most developer iterations. Residual risk is the nightly full-build time growing beyond Cloudflare Pages limits as content scales past 15,000 pages. Risk mitigated by monitoring build time trends and splitting into multiple Cloudflare Pages projects if necessary.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |

### CONFLICT-010: Cloudflare Edge vs. Traditional Hosting

| Field                     | Value                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Conflict ID**           | CONFLICT-010                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| **Conflicting Standards** | Cloudflare Workers Runtime Constraints (CPU time limits: 10ms free / 50ms paid; memory limits: 128MB; subrequest limits: 50 free / 1000 paid; KV eventual consistency; D1 SQLite semantics) vs. Traditional Server Capabilities (unlimited CPU time, full filesystem access, WebSocket support, long-running processes, relational database with ACID transactions) / ISO/IEC 12207:2017 Clause 9.7 (Software Installation), NIST SP 800-53 CM-6 (Configuration Settings)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| **Nature of Conflict**    | Cloudflare's edge computing model imposes constraints that conflict with traditional server-side patterns: (a) **CPU time limits.** Complex operations (large data aggregation from multiple peptide databases, PDF generation for monograph export, complex search queries with fuzzy matching) may exceed the 50ms CPU time limit on paid plans. A request that aggregates data from UniProt + PDB + ChEMBL may require 3-5 subrequests, each consuming CPU time. (b) **Memory limits.** 128MB per request limits in-memory data processing. Generating a comprehensive comparison table for 20+ peptides may require loading large datasets that approach this limit. (c) **KV eventual consistency.** Write-after-read on KV may return stale data for 30-60 seconds. This is unsuitable for: real-time edit conflict detection (two users editing the same page simultaneously), atomic counters (edit count, view count), and session state that must be immediately consistent. (d) **Subrequest limits.** Each external API call (UniProt, PDB, ChEMBL, PubMed) counts as a subrequest. A monograph page that fetches data from 4 external APIs uses 4 of the 1000 paid limit. A search query that fans out to multiple APIs could use 10-20 subrequests. At scale, this creates budget pressure. (e) **No long-running processes.** Background jobs (search index rebuild, content synchronization, email notifications) cannot run as traditional cron jobs. They must be implemented as Cloudflare Cron Triggers or Durable Objects, which have different execution models. (f) **No filesystem.** All persistent storage must use KV, R2, D1, or Durable Objects. There is no `/tmp` directory for temporary file processing (e.g., PDF generation, image processing).                   |
| **Impact Analysis**       | **Architecture:** The team must adapt server-side patterns to edge constraints. Traditional monolithic backend logic must be decomposed into small, stateless functions. **Data Consistency:** KV's eventual consistency requires careful design for features that need strong consistency (edit conflicts, counters). **Scalability:** While edge computing scales horizontally, per-request resource limits may bottleneck complex operations. **Migration Risk:** If Cloudflare changes pricing or limits, migrating away from edge-specific patterns is costly. **Severity: HIGH.**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| **Resolution Strategy**   | Design for edge constraints from inception: (a) **Request decomposition.** Break complex operations into chains of smaller Workers. A monograph page's data aggregation becomes: Worker 1 (fetch UniProt) -> Worker 2 (fetch PDB) -> Worker 3 (fetch ChEMBL) -> Worker 4 (merge and render). Each stays within CPU limits. (b) **Durable Objects for strong consistency.** Use Durable Objects for: edit conflict detection (single instance per page), atomic counters (view counts, edit counts), and session state. Durable Objects provide single-instance execution with strong consistency and WebSocket support. (c) **KV for eventual consistency use cases.** Use KV for: cached API responses (acceptable staleness), search indices (rebuilt periodically), feature flags, and user preferences (non-critical). (d) **Subrequest budget monitoring.** Instrument Workers to log subrequest counts. Alert when approaching 80% of limit. Implement request coalescing (batch multiple API calls into fewer subrequests where possible). (e) **D1 for relational data.** Use D1 for: user accounts, learning progress, community contributions, and content metadata. D1 provides ACID transactions within a single database instance. (f) **R2 for large file processing.** Use R2 for temporary file storage during PDF generation and image processing. Workers read/write to R2 instead of filesystem. (g) **Cron Triggers for background jobs.** Use Cloudflare Cron Triggers for: nightly search index rebuild, periodic content synchronization, and email notification batching. (h) **Abstraction layer.** Create a storage abstraction layer (`StorageProvider` interface) that wraps KV/D1/R2/Durable Objects. If migration is needed, only the provider implementation changes. |
| **ADR Reference**         | ADR-010 (pending: Edge-Native Architecture Pattern)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| **Residual Risk**         | MEDIUM — The edge-native architecture requires specialized knowledge and differs from traditional server patterns. Developer onboarding time is increased. KV eventual consistency requires careful feature design. Risk mitigated by comprehensive documentation, developer training, and the storage abstraction layer that enables migration if needed.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |

### 4.3 External Escalation

If a conflict cannot be resolved internally (e.g., two Tier 1 standards directly contradict each other, which should not occur given WCAG and GDPR are generally complementary):

1. **Legal consultation** — Engage legal counsel to assess compliance obligations and liability exposure.
2. **Standards body consultation** — Contact W3C (for WCAG clarifications), IAPP (for GDPR/CCPA guidance), or NIST (for SP 800-53 interpretations).
3. **Industry peer consultation** — Engage with similar projects (e.g., other educational platforms) for shared solutions.
4. **Regulatory pre-assessment** — For GDPR conflicts, conduct a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) with the relevant supervisory authority if required under Article 36.

## 5. Conflict Lifecycle

### 5.1 States

```
DISCOVERED --> ANALYZED --> RESOLVED --> IMPLEMENTED --> VERIFIED --> CLOSED
    |              |            |              |              |
    +-- DISPUTED   +-- DEFERRED +-- REJECTED   +-- REOPENED   +-- RECURRING
```

| State           | Description                                                        | Responsible      |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------- |
| **DISCOVERED**  | Conflict identified and logged in registry with initial assessment | Discoverer       |
| **ANALYZED**    | Root cause, impact, and resolution options documented              | Technical Lead   |
| **RESOLVED**    | Resolution strategy approved and documented in ADR                 | Project Lead     |
| **IMPLEMENTED** | Resolution code changes deployed to production                     | Development Team |
| **VERIFIED**    | Resolution confirmed effective via testing/audit                   | QA / Auditor     |
| **CLOSED**      | Conflict fully mitigated with acceptable residual risk             | Project Lead     |
| **DISPUTED**    | Conflict classification or resolution is questioned                | Any Stakeholder  |
| **DEFERRED**    | Resolution postponed to future phase/sprint                        | Project Lead     |
| **REJECTED**    | Conflict determined to be invalid or non-applicable                | Technical Lead   |
| **REOPENED**    | Previously closed conflict recurs or residual risk materializes    | Any Stakeholder  |
| **RECURRING**   | Conflict has recurred multiple times, indicating systemic issue    | Project Lead     |

### 5.2 Monitoring and Recurrence Prevention

1. **Quarterly conflict registry review.** All OPEN conflicts (DISCOVERED through VERIFIED) are reviewed quarterly for status updates and prioritization changes.
2. **Recurring conflict analysis.** Any conflict marked RECURRING triggers a root cause analysis to determine why the resolution was insufficient.
3. **New standard impact assessment.** When new standards or standard versions are adopted, the entire conflict registry is re-evaluated for new conflicts.
4. **Post-incident conflict review.** After any production incident (security breach, accessibility failure, performance degradation), the conflict registry is reviewed for related unresolved conflicts.
